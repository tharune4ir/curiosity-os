# Feed Hacker: Headline Autopsy

## The Run Mode

### 1. The Trap (5 min)
Show 3 conflicting or sensationalized claims on the board.
- **Example A (India Context):** "New report says 90% of Indian teens prefer coding over sports." (Hidden: Survey done only in 3 private schools in Bangalore).
- **Example B:** "Amazing! This simple spice in your kitchen cures Diabetes in 3 days." (Hidden: No human trials; based on a single cell-culture observation).
- **Example C:** "Crime rate in the city has jumped by 50%!" (Hidden: The city's population also grew by 60%).
- **Ask:** "Quick hands... who trusts Claim A? B? C? Why?"

### 2. The Autopsy Protocol (20 min)
Groups have 20 minutes to "crack open" one claim using the **Autopsy Sheet**:
1. **The Rewrite:** Write the claim in your own words, removing all "emotional" words (e.g., 'Amazing', 'Scary', 'Shocking').
2. **Find the Denominator:** "90% of teens" — how many teens exactly? From where? What was the total sample?
3. **The Mirror Test:** If the opposite of this claim were true, what would we see?
4. **Axis Check (if chart provided):** Does the Y-axis start at zero? Is it stretched to make a small change look like a vertical cliff?

### 3. The Decompile (10 min)
The teacher leads a decompile not on the "answer," but on the **Mechanism of Deception**:
- "The headline is the bait; the denominator is the hook."
- "Visuals are designed to trigger your 'Recognition Addiction' – you see a chart and your brain skips the numbers to the shape."

### 4. The Transfer (10 min)
Students pick one "WhatsApp Forward" or "Instagram Ad" they saw this week. 
- **The Mission:** Apply one "Autopsy Tool" to it and report back tomorrow.

## Success Criteria
Students can identify the specific "logical leak" (e.g., selection bias, axis truncation) rather than just saying "I don't believe it."
