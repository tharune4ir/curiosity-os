# Bias Court: Same Facts, Different Story

## The Run Mode
### 1. The Setup
Hand out a 'Social Discovery' card (e.g., "A teammate's code failed. Was it sabotage or a mistake?"). 
- **The First Verdict:** Rapid show of hands.

### 2. The Mission
Teacher reveals 4 waves of 'Updates':
- **Wave 1 (Vivid but weak):** "He was seen laughing near the computer." (Availability bias).
- **Wave 2 (Boring but important):** "The logs show a hardware failure at 3:00 PM." (Data over vibes).
- **Wave 3 (Commitment):** "A senior says he did it." (Authority bias).
- **Wave 4 (Pattern):** "This has happened in 3 other classes." (Base rate logic).

### 3. The Decompile
Focus on **Cognitive Debugging**:
- Your brain is a lawyer, not a judge.
- The trap is **Commitment and Consistency**: once you say it, you want to defend it.
