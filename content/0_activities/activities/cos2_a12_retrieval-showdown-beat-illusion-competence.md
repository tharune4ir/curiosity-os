# Retrieval Showdown: Beat the Illusion of Competence

## The Run Mode

### 1. The Setup (5 min)
Hand out a dense, one-page text on a topic students haven't covered yet (e.g., "The Life Cycle of a Neutron Star" or "The History of the Silk Road").
- **Group A (The Readers):** Instructed to reread the text twice and highlight "key terms."
- **Group B (The Retrievers):** Instructed to read once (5 mins). Close the paper. Spend 5 minutes writing EVERYTHING you remember on a blank sheet. No looking back.

### 2. The Study Session (15 min)
Run the timer. 
- **Teacher Move:** Observe Group A. They will look calm, nodding, feeling like "they got this."
- **Teacher Move:** Observe Group B. They will look stressed, pausing, struggling to recall. 
- **Decompile Note:** "Group B is currently building a stronger 'retrieval path' in their brains. Group A is just painting paper."

### 3. The Showdown (10 min)
Collect the texts. Hand out a 10-question factual and conceptual quiz.
- Grade the quizzes immediately (peer-grading is fastest).
- **The Reveal:** Write the average score for Group A and Group B on the board.
- **Expected Result:** Group B will typically outperform Group A significantly.

### 4. The Decompile (15 min)
Lead a discussion on **The Illusion of Competence**:
- "When you reread something, it looks 'familiar.' Your brain says, 'I know this.' But you don't know the content—you just recognize the words."
- "Highlighting is easy; retrieval is hard. Hard = Progress."
- **The Takeaway:** If your study feels easy, you're probably not learning. If it feels like a 'workout,' it's working.

## Success Criteria
Students move from "This feels good" to "I am retrieving effectively."
Artifact: Quiz score comparison chart + Individual 'Free Recall' sheets.
