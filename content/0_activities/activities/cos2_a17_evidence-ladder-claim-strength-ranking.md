# Evidence Ladder: Claim Strength Ranking

## The Run Mode
### 1. The Setup
Show the **Evidence Ladder** graph:
- **Rung 5 (Top):** Consensus / Systematic Review (Many studies agree).
- **Rung 4:** Controlled Study (Replicated data).
- **Rung 3:** Expert/Organization Quote (Based on data).
- **Rung 2:** Observational Anecdote ("I saw it once").
- **Rung 1 (Bottom):** Opinion / Speculation.

### 2. The Mission
Groups have 20 minutes to rank 5 claims about a topic (e.g., "Drinking milk causes X," "Social media leads to Y").
1. **Sort them:** Place each claim on a rung.
2. **Justify:** Why is the specific study higher than the expert quote?
3. **Challenge:** Find one way to move the lowest rung up the ladder.

### 3. The Decompile
Focus on **Information Quality**:
- Don't just ask if it's true; ask **How strong is the evidence?**
- Anecdotes are vivid but dangerous because they don't show the average result.
