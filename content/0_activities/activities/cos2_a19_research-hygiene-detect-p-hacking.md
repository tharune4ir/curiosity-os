# Research Hygiene: Detect P-Hacking & Tiny Samples

## The Run Mode
### 1. The Setup
Show 3 headline claims (e.g., "Drinking juice makes kids score 10% higher!"). 
- **The Catch:** The sample size is 6, and they were all from the same soccer team.

### 2. The Mission
Groups work together to "clean up" the research:
1. **Find the 'N':** How many people were studied? 
2. **Find the 'P':** Was this the only thing they measured, or did they measure 100 things and pick the one that "worked"? (P-Hacking).
3. **Funding:** Who paid for the juice study? (The Juice Growers Association).
4. **Conclusion:** Decide: **Trust, Trash, or Retest.**

### 3. The Decompile
Focus on **Scientific Skepticism**:
- Small samples are noisy; don't trust them without replication.
- Incentives influence what gets published.
