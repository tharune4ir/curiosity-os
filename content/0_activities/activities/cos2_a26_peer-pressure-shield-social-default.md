# Peer Pressure Shield: Social Default & Escape

## The Run Mode

### 1. The Setup (5 min)
Introduce the **Social Current**.
- **Definition:** The unspoken "what we all do" in a group.
- **Example (India Context):** Everyone in the group is bunking a project meeting to watch a cricket match or go to the canteen. You actually want to do the project, but saying that makes you the "party pooper."
- **Ask:** "What is the hardest 'social default' you've faced this month? Don't name names."

### 2. The Escape Pod (15 min)
Pairs build their own **Escape Pods** for 3 specific Indian school scenarios:
1. **The 'One More' Pressure:** "Stay out for one more hour," or "Play one more round of BGMI/Valorant."
2. **The 'Gossip' Pressure:** "Did you hear what X said about Y? Tell me something about X too."
3. **The 'Risky Joke' Pressure:** Doing something risky in the corridor or classroom for a 'dare'.
- **Rules:** The script must be under 5 words. No "Because..." (No justifications).
- **Examples:** "Nah, I've got a hard stop." "I'm good, thanks." "Can't today, catch ya later."

### 3. Simulation (15 min)
One student is "The Pressure" (subtle persuasion). The other is "The Shield."
- **The Shield's Goal:** Repeat their Escape Pod script 3 times. They must stay calm and bored.
- **The Pressure's Goal:** Try to get a justification. "But why? Are you scared? Only 5 minutes!"
- **Switch roles after 3 minutes.**

### 4. The Reveal (10 min)
Decompile the **Trust & Boundaries** logic:
- Real friends respect your "hard stop."
- People-pleasers lose trust because they're unpredictable. Boundary-setters gain trust because they're reliable.
- "No" is a complete sentence.

## Success Criteria
Students can demonstrate a calm, short "No" under moderate peer pressure without offering a long excuse.
Artifact: A set of 3 "Personal Escape Pod" scripts written on cards.
