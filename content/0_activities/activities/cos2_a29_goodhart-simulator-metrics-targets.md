# Goodhart Simulator: When Metrics Become Targets

## The Run Mode
### 1. The Setup
Each group has a 'School' with 4 dimensions: **Learning**, **Wellbeing**, **Innovation**, and **Grades**. 
- **The Initial Mission:** Improve the school by choosing 'Actions' (e.g., "Add library," "Run a workshop").

### 2. The Mission
Teacher introduces a **High-Stakes Metric**: "A donor will only give money to the school with the highest **Homework Submission Count**."
1. **The Gaming:** Groups pivot. They ignore 'Wellbeing' and 'Innovation' to maximize 'Homework Count'.
2. **Result:** Homework count hits the moon. Final school quality (Learning) drops by 50%.
3. **Reveal:** The donor pull-out.

### 3. The Decompile
Focus on **Goodhart's Law**:
- When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.
- Human systems are easily gamed; watch the incentives, not the headlines.
