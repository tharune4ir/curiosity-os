## The Invisible Reality
Archaeologists dig up the past to understand people long gone. They see a dig site as a time capsule: each layer of earth is like a page in history. By carefully brushing soil away, they uncover pottery, tools, or bones, which are clues. It's like solving a crime from thousands of years ago, using artifacts instead of fingerprints.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Many museum exhibits are from archaeological digs. For example, uncovering King Tut’s tomb revealed treasures that taught us about ancient Egypt. Today, archaeologists even use radar and drones to find ruins without digging up farmland.

## The Players Life
You spend days excavating carefully, mapping where each find was buried. You measure and record finds, and then clean and study them in labs. You also date objects (using techniques like carbon dating) to know how old they are. Eventually you share discoveries by writing reports or creating museum displays.

## The Skill Tree
Patience and careful hands, basic geology (to understand soil layers), and knowledge of history and ancient cultures.
