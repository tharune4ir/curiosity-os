## The Invisible Reality
Chemical engineers mix ingredients like chefs, but on an industrial scale. They imagine each chemical as a recipe ingredient; for example, turning sugar and yeast into alcohol or mixing gases to clean water. They know that small molecules (tiny building blocks of matter) join or split like pieces of a puzzle when heated or stirred, so they design exact conditions (temperature, pressure) to make useful products like medicines or plastics.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Everything from your plastic toys to the medicines you take started in a factory designed by chemical engineers. Right now, they even work on creating synthetic (fake but very useful) fuels and materials that could one day replace oil.

## The Players Life
You use lab equipment to mix chemicals safely and watch for reactions (like fizzing or color changes). You make calculations to scale up recipes from a lab beaker to a big tank. Each day involves testing conditions (like changing heat or pressure) to get the right result and checking safety to make sure nothing explodes.

## The Skill Tree
Understanding chemical reactions, careful lab skills (measuring precisely), and math for scaling up recipes.
