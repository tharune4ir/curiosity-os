## The Invisible Reality
Chemists mix and study chemicals like cooking with invisible recipes. They treat molecules (tiny building blocks of stuff) like ingredients: when you combine vinegar and baking soda, for example, they fizz and make a new mixture. By understanding how atoms stick together and break apart, chemists can create new materials, medicines, and dyes. They imagine colorful molecules dancing and binding in tiny solutions.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Every battery in phones, every plastic toy, and every medicine pill began with a chemist discovering how to mix molecules. Today, chemists are creating things like biodegradable plastics and medicines made by artificial microbes.

## The Players Life
You spend time in a lab wearing a safety coat, mixing chemicals in beakers, heating them, and observing color changes. You carefully measure each ingredient and note down exactly what happens. Sometimes you test how to make a drug or a new paint, making sure your reactions are safe and efficient.

## The Skill Tree
Careful measuring and mixing, safety and lab skills, and observation (noticing tiny color or temperature changes).
