## The Invisible Reality
Climatologists study long-term weather patterns. They treat the atmosphere like a big system. For example, they watch greenhouse gases (like CO2) as blankets trapping heat. They use models (like computer weather simulations) to predict climate changes over years and understand phenomena like global warming.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Scientists have measured rising temperatures and melting ice caps. Climatologists help world leaders make decisions on climate policy. Right now, they use satellite data and supercomputers to forecast climate trends for decades ahead.

## The Players Life
You analyze climate data from weather stations and satellites. You might run computer models to simulate Earth’s climate with different conditions. You also write reports to explain findings and may advise governments on climate adaptation.

## The Skill Tree
Meteorology basics, math for models, and understanding of physics and chemistry.
