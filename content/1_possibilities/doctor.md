## The Invisible Reality
Doctors look inside the body like detectives using clues. They use tools such as stethoscopes (which pick up heartbeats, like a super-ear) and X-rays (like taking special photos of bones) to see hidden problems. They think of the body as a complex machine and use science to diagnose and treat illness, often drawing on knowledge of biology and chemistry.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Doctors today use powerful tools: from MRI machines that create images of the brain to gene therapies that treat diseases by changing DNA. A single doctor can see dozens of patients a day, and together, doctors around the world save millions of lives with vaccinations and treatments.

## The Players Life
You examine patients, ask questions about symptoms, and perform physical checks. You write prescriptions for medicines and order tests (like blood tests). You often consult with labs or other specialists and explain diagnoses to patients. Many doctors work long hours, sometimes staying up late to check on critical patients.

## The Skill Tree
Strong knowledge of science (biology, chemistry), communication (explaining diagnoses), and empathy (caring for patients).
