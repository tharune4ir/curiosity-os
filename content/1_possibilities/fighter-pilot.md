## The Invisible Reality
Fighter pilots fly planes that are sensitive to tiny forces. They deal with aerodynamics and extreme G-forces (like being weighed down much heavier). In sharp turns, a pilot might feel like a heavy weight presses them into the seat. The jet's controls—like sticks and pedals—are the pilot's hands and feet as they move through the sky at supersonic speeds, invisible without a cockpit.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
At high speed, turns can create forces of 9 G or more, making a 70 kg pilot feel like 630 kg. Fighter jets can travel faster than the speed of sound, creating sonic booms. Today, pilots control jets with digital computers that make small corrections thousands of times per second.

## The Players Life
You train with simulators and flight hours, studying aerodynamics and tactics. You wear a special suit to help with G-forces and practice breathing techniques. Each mission, you coordinate with radar and ground crew to plan maneuvers, track targets, and safely land.

## The Skill Tree
Quick reflexes, knowledge of flight physics, and calmness under pressure.
