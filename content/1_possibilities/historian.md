## The Invisible Reality
Historians study books, records, and artifacts to tell us what happened in the past. They treat old stories and documents like evidence. It's like being a detective: they piece together events from clues (letters, old newspapers, coins). For example, by reading a soldier’s diary, they understand that person's life in a war.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Every history book is the work of historians. Today’s historians are uncovering new documents online (like letters from the 1800s) and sometimes rewriting what we thought happened. They help us learn from the past mistakes and successes.

## The Players Life
You read old books and records in libraries, translate or understand old languages, and compare sources to get a clear story. You travel to archives or historical sites. You write articles or books, and sometimes teach or make documentaries about historical events.

## The Skill Tree
Research (finding and reading sources), critical thinking (distinguishing reliable accounts), and writing clearly.
