## The Invisible Reality
A marathon runner's body is like an efficient long-distance engine. They use energy slowly by burning fuel in muscles like a car burning gasoline at low speed. Their heart and lungs become powerful pumps, sending oxygen through the body. Over 42 kilometers, every step is tuned so as not to waste energy: even breathing and hydration become precise mechanics.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Marathon runners can burn over 2,500 calories in a single race. That’s roughly the energy found in 25 chocolate bars! To keep going, their bodies use up sugar and fat very efficiently, almost like a hybrid car switching fuel.

## The Players Life
You spend hours running each day, building stamina with long, slow runs and short tempo sprints. You carefully plan meals and water intake. You study route profiles and practice pacing. Coaches often monitor your heart rate and oxygen levels during training to keep you at the right effort.

## The Skill Tree
Endurance (long-term stamina), pacing strategy, and mental toughness.
