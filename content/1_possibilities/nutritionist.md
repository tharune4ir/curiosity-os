## The Invisible Reality
Nutritionists study food and health, acting like chefs and scientists together. They know food is fuel, and figure out what to eat for health. For example, they understand that proteins build muscles and vitamins keep eyes sharp. They measure nutrients in foods and plan diets that balance proteins, carbs, and fats, like constructing a recipe for a healthy body.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Nutritionists influence menus in hospitals and schools to improve health. Today, they might use software to analyze diets and even work with farmers on better crops. Healthy diets can prevent diseases: nutritionists helped show how certain vitamins prevent deficiencies like scurvy or anemia.

## The Players Life
You analyze what people eat (like writing down meals) and calculate nutritional values. You meet clients to advise them on better food choices, often adjusting for health conditions (like diabetes). You also plan healthy meal options for cafeterias or create nutritional plans for athletes or patients.

## The Skill Tree
Understanding of food science, math to calculate diet values, and communication (explaining diet plans).
