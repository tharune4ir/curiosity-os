## The Invisible Reality
A sprinter's body works like a tightly wound spring. They use fast-twitch muscle fibers (like sudden-release rubber bands) to explode out of the blocks. They think about the track as a line they must cover in a few steps. Even tiny arm swings and breathing rhythm matter, because at top speed, the air resistance (drag) is pushing against them like a strong wind.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
The fastest sprinters run 100 meters in under 10 seconds. That means covering 10 meters every second, which is like a small car on a city road. At those speeds, each runner uses more energy in one second than many people do in one minute just sitting!

## The Players Life
Your daily routine includes short, explosive training runs, strength exercises, and studying your form with coaches. You start each run focusing on reaction to the starting gun, then sprint while pumping arms and legs with perfect technique. Coaches use slow-motion videos to improve tiny details like foot placement.

## The Skill Tree
Fast-twitch muscle strength, perfect running form, and intense focus.
