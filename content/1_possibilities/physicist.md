## The Invisible Reality
Physicists are nature’s detectives: they try to find the rules of how everything works. They might think of atoms like tiny magnets or billiard balls. For example, they imagine electricity as a flow of tiny particles (electrons) hitting other particles like tiny pool balls. By looking for patterns (like how each pendulum swing repeats or how sunlight scatters into a rainbow), they discover fundamental laws like gravity or light speed.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Physicists predicted things like radio waves and even the Higgs boson (a particle discovered in 2012) before anyone saw them. Right now, they’re exploring quantum particles and building telescopes to see light from the first stars that formed in the universe.

## The Players Life
You run experiments to test theories: maybe you use lasers to measure distances very accurately, or particle detectors to spot unknown particles. You spend a lot of time calculating and using computers to simulate physics. You also read and discuss other scientists' ideas, and teach or write papers about your findings.

## The Skill Tree
Strong math (for calculations), careful observation, and curiosity for how nature works.
