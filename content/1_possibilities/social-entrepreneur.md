## The Invisible Reality
Social entrepreneurs start businesses to solve social problems, like education or environment, instead of just making money. They treat business tools as forces for good: like using mobile apps to help farmers sell crops or solar power for villages. They blend non-profit goals with business skills, often thinking like activists who also manage budgets.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Many social businesses help thousands of people: for example, some founders built low-cost water filters used by millions, or education platforms reaching remote villages. These ventures often use simple tech (like SMS texts) to do big things.

## The Players Life
You identify a community need (like clean water), brainstorm solutions, and then gather a team. You might create a prototype (like a cheap filter), test it with real people, and refine it. You balance budget and impact, so you might sell something affordably or get donations to keep it sustainable.

## The Skill Tree
Empathy (caring about others' problems), innovation, and business basics (raising funds or budgeting).
