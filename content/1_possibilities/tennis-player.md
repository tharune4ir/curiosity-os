## The Invisible Reality
Tennis is a constant battle of angles and forces. When you hit the ball with a racket, it’s like hitting a spring: more angle and speed send the ball further, while slicing can curve it. The tennis court itself acts like a big clock: positioning yourself is timed carefully. Players gauge the ball’s spin and speed and move their feet so their body applies the correct force.

## The Mind Blowing Hook
Top tennis serves can exceed 240 km/h (150 mph), faster than a Formula 1 pit lane. The ball can bounce tens of times during a long rally, and players can change direction hundreds of times a match, with heart rates over 180 bpm. They also use physics: by hitting the ball higher, they maximize distance, and by adjusting spin they can make it bounce unpredictably.

## The Players Life
Your routine includes practicing serves and groundstrokes, doing drills for speed and agility, and watching game footage. You work on footwork, running to every ball, and coordinate with coaches to analyze opponents. Many hours are spent conditioning and stretching to avoid injury.

## The Skill Tree
Agility and speed, precise racket control, and endurance.
