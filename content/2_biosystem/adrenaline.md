## The Simple Truth
Adrenaline (also called epinephrine) is a hormone that rushes through your body when you're excited or scared. It's like a turbo boost: heart races, breathing speeds up, and muscles get a quick burst of power.

## Everyday Example
If someone honks a car horn at you unexpectedly, adrenaline surges and you jump. Or before a big game, adrenaline makes you feel pumped and ready.

## The Superpower
Adrenaline gives you quick strength and focus in emergencies or games.

## The Danger Zone
Too much adrenaline (from constant stress or excitement) can make you feel anxious, shaky, or have trouble sleeping.

## Action Step
To calm down from an adrenaline rush, take a few deep breaths or move around gently; it helps use up the energy.
