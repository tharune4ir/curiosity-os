## The Simple Truth
ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) is like money or a battery inside your cells. Every muscle movement or thought spends ATP for energy. When ATP runs out, you need food and oxygen to recharge it.

## Everyday Example
When you sprint in cricket, your muscles burn ATP fast. If ATP runs low, your legs feel heavy and you need to rest.

## The Superpower
Having enough ATP means your cells can do work. Good diet and oxygen help cells pack more ATP, giving you more endurance and faster thinking.

## The Danger Zone
If ATP is low (from no food or oxygen), you feel weak, tired, and can't think well. It's like your phone running out of battery.

## Action Step
Don’t skip meals, and take deep breaths. Both help your cells make ATP for energy.
