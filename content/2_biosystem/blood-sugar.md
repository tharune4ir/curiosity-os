## The Simple Truth
Blood sugar level is how much glucose is in your blood. Your body adjusts it like a thermostat: insulin lowers it after meals, and hormones raise it when you’re hungry.

## Everyday Example
If you skip lunch, your blood sugar drops and you feel shaky and hungry. If you eat cake, it rises fast and then drops when insulin comes.

## The Superpower
Keeping blood sugar steady (by eating balanced meals) means you have consistent energy and stable mood all day.

## The Danger Zone
Frequent high spikes or low crashes make you tired, dizzy or even faint. Over time, it can lead to diabetes.

## Action Step
Include protein or fiber with every carbohydrate meal to slow sugar spikes and keep blood sugar stable.
