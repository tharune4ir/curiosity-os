## The Simple Truth
Blue light from screens (phones, TVs) tricks your brain into thinking it's daytime. It suppresses melatonin, so you stay awake even when your brain should be winding down.

## Everyday Example
Using your phone or computer in bed can make it hard to fall asleep because of the blue light shining into your eyes.

## The Superpower
Limiting blue light at night helps you fall asleep faster and sleep deeper.

## The Danger Zone
Too much blue light before bedtime causes insomnia and tiredness in the morning.

## Action Step
Install a blue-light filter on your devices or stop using screens one hour before bed.
