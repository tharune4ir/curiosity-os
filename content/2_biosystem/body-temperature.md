## The Simple Truth
Your body has a normal temperature around 37°C, like a thermostat setting. When you get hot (exercise or fever), you sweat to cool down; when cold, you shiver to warm up.

## Everyday Example
On a hot day you sweat and need water to cool down. In winter, your body might make you shiver to stay warm.

## The Superpower
Keeping body temperature in range means you can play comfortably in different weather.

## The Danger Zone
If your temperature goes too high (fever or heat exhaustion), you can feel weak or confused. Too low (hypothermia) makes you numb and slow.

## Action Step
Drink water and find shade or a cool spot if you feel very hot.
