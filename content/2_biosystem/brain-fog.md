## The Simple Truth
Brain fog feels like your thoughts are cloudy. It happens when your brain isn’t getting enough fuel (like sleep, oxygen, or nutrients) and can’t work clearly.

## Everyday Example
After a very late night and no breakfast, you sit in class and feel blank, forgetful, and unable to focus.

## The Superpower
Understanding brain fog means you fix it by resting, eating, or hydrating. Then you think clearly again and do well in school.

## The Danger Zone
If you ignore this fog (keep sleep-deprived or skipping meals), your schoolwork suffers and you feel frustrated and tired all day.

## Action Step
Take a 5-minute break: stand up, stretch, and drink a glass of water to help clear your mind when feeling foggy.
