## The Simple Truth
Calories measure how much energy is in food. If you eat more calories than you burn, you store extra energy as fat. If you burn more calories (exercise) than you eat, you use fat for energy.

## Everyday Example
A big cookie has many calories. Running and playing sports burns calories. Eating too many calories (without exercise) can make you gain weight.

## The Superpower
Knowing about calories helps you manage your energy and stay at a healthy weight.

## The Danger Zone
Too many calories from junk food make you gain weight and feel sluggish. Too few calories (starving) makes you tired and weak.

## Action Step
Pay attention to portion sizes and balance treats with physical activity.
