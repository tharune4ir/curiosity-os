## The Simple Truth
Your body has a built-in clock called the circadian rhythm, like a watch telling you when to sleep or wake. It runs on a 24-hour cycle and uses light as a signal. Morning sunlight says “time to wake up,” and darkness says “get sleepy.” This clock helps control when you feel tired or hungry and keeps your body working properly.

## Everyday Example
If your school starts at 8am but you play video games all night, you’ll feel very tired in class the next day. That’s because your body clock thinks it should be sleeping, not awake.

## The Superpower
When you follow your body’s clock (sleep at night, wake with the sun), you wake up feeling refreshed and alert naturally. You will focus better in the morning and need fewer alarms.

## The Danger Zone
If you ignore your clock by staying up late or sleeping in, your circadian rhythm gets confused. You might feel sleepy or moody during the day, have trouble sleeping at night, and your brain won’t work well.

## Action Step
In the morning, open your curtains or go outside for a few minutes of sunlight. The light tells your body it’s day, helping reset your clock and make you feel more awake.
