## The Simple Truth
Cortisol is a hormone your body makes when stressed. It’s like an emergency fuel injection: it gives you more energy and alertness to handle stress or danger.

## Everyday Example
Before an exam, cortisol helps you feel alert and focused. But if stress stays high all the time, cortisol stays high and makes you feel jittery or anxious.

## The Superpower
Short bursts of cortisol help you tackle challenges with energy and focus.

## The Danger Zone
Constant high cortisol (from chronic stress) can lead to sleep problems, weakened immunity (getting sick easily), and even weight gain around the belly.

## Action Step
After a stressful event, do something relaxing (like listen to music or go for a walk) to help cortisol levels drop.
