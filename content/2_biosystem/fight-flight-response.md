## The Simple Truth
Fight-or-flight is your body's alarm system. When you sense danger or stress, your brain triggers a burst of energy (hormones like adrenaline and cortisol) to prepare you to run or fight instantly.

## Everyday Example
If a cat suddenly jumps out in front of you, you might jump back and your heart races. You became extra alert in an instant, even if the cat meant no harm.

## The Superpower
This response gives you quick strength and sharp senses in emergencies or important moments, like an exam or sports match.

## The Danger Zone
If this alarm keeps going off (like constant stress at home or school), you stay tense and exhausted. It leads to anxiety, trouble sleeping, and health problems over time.

## Action Step
Notice when this happens (racing heart) and take 5 deep breaths or count to 10 slowly to help deactivate the alarm.
