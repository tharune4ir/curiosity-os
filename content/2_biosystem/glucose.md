## The Simple Truth
Glucose is the main sugar (fuel) in your blood. When you eat carbs, your body turns them into glucose, which acts like gasoline for your cells.

## Everyday Example
After eating rice, glucose floods your blood and gives you energy. Too much at once (like from sweets) needs insulin to store it.

## The Superpower
Stable glucose keeps your energy steady and brain sharp, avoiding tiredness.

## The Danger Zone
Too much glucose at once causes a crash later. Over time, it can lead to diabetes where glucose can't get into cells.

## Action Step
Balance carbs with protein or fiber (like eating dal with rice) to slow sugar release and avoid spikes.
