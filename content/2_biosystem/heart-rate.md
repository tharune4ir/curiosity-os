## The Simple Truth
Heart rate is how many times your heart beats per minute. It’s like how fast a car engine is running (RPM). Exercise or excitement raises it, rest lowers it.

## Everyday Example
After running a race, counting your pulse shows a high number. After sitting quietly, it’ll be low.

## The Superpower
Monitoring your heart rate helps you know if you’re exercising right or need to calm down.

## The Danger Zone
If your resting heart rate is too high from stress or health issues, your heart is working overtime and it can lead to problems.

## Action Step
Check your pulse for 10 seconds after a brisk walk to see your heart rate. It should be higher than at rest.
