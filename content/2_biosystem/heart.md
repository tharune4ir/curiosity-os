## The Simple Truth
The heart is like a pump at the center of your chest. It beats to push blood through vessels to deliver oxygen and nutrients to your body. When you exercise, it pumps faster; when you sleep, it slows down.

## Everyday Example
After running a race, you feel your heart pounding rapidly in your chest. When you rest quietly, it goes back to a slow beat.

## The Superpower
A strong heart means you can play sports longer without getting winded, and you keep your brain supplied with oxygen for sharp thinking.

## The Danger Zone
If your heart is weak (from unhealthy lifestyle), you tire easily climbing stairs and might feel dizzy. Your blood circulation suffers.

## Action Step
Get moving for 15 minutes today (like a brisk walk or jumping jacks). This exercise makes your heart stronger.
