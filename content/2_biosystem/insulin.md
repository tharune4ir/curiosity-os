## The Simple Truth
Insulin (a hormone from your pancreas) is like a delivery boy for sugar. When you eat, insulin carries glucose from your blood into your cells for energy.

## Everyday Example
When you eat, insulin pushes sugar into your muscles and brain. If insulin is slow (like in diabetes), sugar stays in blood and you feel weak.

## The Superpower
Proper insulin function keeps your sugar balanced, giving cells the energy they need and keeping you from feeling shaky or tired.

## The Danger Zone
If insulin doesn’t work well (insulin resistance), sugar stays in blood and you risk diabetes.

## Action Step
Eat balanced meals (not too much sugar alone) and exercise to keep insulin working well.
