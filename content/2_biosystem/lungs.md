## The Simple Truth
Lungs are like bellows or air pumps in your chest. They bring oxygen into your body when you inhale and let out carbon dioxide when you exhale. Oxygen goes from lungs to blood to reach cells.

## Everyday Example
When you play soccer, you breathe harder and faster. Your lungs fill with more air to get extra oxygen to your legs.

## The Superpower
Good lung capacity means more stamina for sports and better oxygen supply to your brain for focus.

## The Danger Zone
Shallow breathing or polluted air means less oxygen reaches your body. You feel weak, tired, and you can get headaches.

## Action Step
Take a few deep breaths outside right now: inhale slowly through your nose, then exhale. This boosts your oxygen intake.
