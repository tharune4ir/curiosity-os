## The Simple Truth
Macronutrients are the main nutrients we need in large amounts: carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. Carbs are quick fuel, protein builds and fixes your body, and fats give long-lasting energy and help absorb vitamins.

## Everyday Example
A meal with rice (carbs), dal (protein), and a little oil or nuts (fat) provides all three macros. If you only ate sweets (carbs) all day, you'd get an energy spike but weak muscles.

## The Superpower
Eating the right balance of macros gives steady energy, muscle strength, and full body repair. You feel full longer and perform better in activities.

## The Danger Zone
Not eating enough of one macro (like no protein) can make your muscles weak. Eating too much sugar (carbs) without protein/fat causes energy crashes.

## Action Step
At your next meal, include something from each macro: grains for carbs, dal or chicken for protein, and some healthy fat (like oil or nuts).
