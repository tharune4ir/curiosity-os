## The Simple Truth
Melatonin is a hormone your brain makes when it’s dark. It tells your body “get sleepy,” like dimming lights in a theater setting a sleep scene.

## Everyday Example
When the lights in your bedroom are turned off, melatonin rises and you start to feel drowsy. If you watch a bright phone screen, melatonin stays low and you feel awake.

## The Superpower
By keeping lights low at night, you help melatonin work naturally. This makes falling asleep easy and sleeping deep, so you wake up refreshed.

## The Danger Zone
Using bright screens or lights at bedtime stops melatonin from rising. You stay awake later, get poor sleep, and feel drowsy the next day.

## Action Step
Turn off or dim bright screens at least an hour before bed to let melatonin do its job.
