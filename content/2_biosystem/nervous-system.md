## The Simple Truth
The nervous system is like a network of wires in your body. It includes your brain, spinal cord, and nerves. It sends signals (like electric currents) from your senses to the brain and from the brain to your muscles.

## Everyday Example
If you touch a hot pan, nerves in your hand instantly send a signal to your brain, then your hand jerks away—all in a split second.

## The Superpower
A healthy nervous system lets you react quickly (catching a ball) and feel sensations (warmth of sun, taste of food). It helps you think, learn, and control your body smoothly.

## The Danger Zone
If nerves are hurt (like from injury or illness), signals slow down. You might feel numbness, weak limbs, or slow reflexes. Chronic stress or lack of sleep also slows your nerves over time.

## Action Step
Take short breaks to stretch and relax if you’re studying or using devices for a long time. It helps your nerves stay free of tension.
