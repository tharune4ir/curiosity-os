## The Simple Truth
Nutrient absorption is when your intestine walls load digested food into your bloodstream, like loading supplies onto trucks. This delivers energy and building blocks to your cells.

## Everyday Example
After your meal, glucose and vitamins pass through the gut wall into your blood, giving energy to your brain and muscles.

## The Superpower
Good absorption means fast recovery from workouts and quick thinking in class.

## The Danger Zone
Poor absorption (like in sick stomach) means you stay tired and weak because nutrients never reached your cells.

## Action Step
Avoid drinking too much water with meals; it can dilute stomach juices. Chew food well to make absorption easier.
