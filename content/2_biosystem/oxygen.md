## The Simple Truth
Oxygen is like pure air fuel. Cells in your body need oxygen to burn food for energy, just like a fire needs oxygen to burn wood.

## Everyday Example
If you hold your breath, you quickly feel uncomfortable because your cells aren’t getting oxygen. That’s why after a swim you breathe deeply.

## The Superpower
Good oxygen flow (breathing deeply, exercising) gives your brain and muscles more energy, making you feel alert and strong.

## The Danger Zone
Not enough oxygen (shallow breaths or high altitude) makes you sleepy, dizzy, and weak. Over time, it can harm your organs.

## Action Step
Go outside for a few minutes and take slow deep breaths of fresh air right now to refresh your body.
