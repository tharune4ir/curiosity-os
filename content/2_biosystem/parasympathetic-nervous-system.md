## The Simple Truth
The parasympathetic system is like the brake in a car. It slows down body functions after stress is gone: heart rate slows, muscles relax, and digestion turns on.

## Everyday Example
After finishing an exam, your breathing and heart slow as you relax. That’s your parasympathetic system helping your body recover.

## The Superpower
Activating this system (through slow breaths or quiet time) helps you relax faster, sleep better, and digest food properly.

## The Danger Zone
If you never slow down (never press the brake), your body stays in fight mode all the time. You’ll have trouble eating, sleeping, and may feel anxious constantly.

## Action Step
Practice belly breathing: inhale deeply through your nose for 4 seconds, then exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds to engage your brake system.
