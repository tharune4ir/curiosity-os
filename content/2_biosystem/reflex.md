## The Simple Truth
A reflex is an automatic reaction your body makes without thinking. It's like hitting the brakes when you suddenly see an obstacle. Your nerves send signals for immediate action.

## Everyday Example
If you accidentally touch something hot, you pull your hand away instantly, even before you feel the pain. That’s a reflex at work.

## The Superpower
Reflexes protect you from harm and help you react quickly in sports and emergencies.

## The Danger Zone
Slow reflexes (from fatigue or nerve damage) can cause accidents because you won’t move in time to avoid danger.

## Action Step
Try a quick reaction test (like catching a dropped ruler). Practicing such drills can keep your reflexes sharp.
