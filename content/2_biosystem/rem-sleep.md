## The Simple Truth
REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep is the dream stage. It’s like your brain filing away memories and learning. Your eyes move under closed lids and your brain is very active, almost like you're awake.

## Everyday Example
If you had a vivid dream last night, you likely woke during REM sleep. Getting enough REM sleep helps you remember what you learned or practiced during the day.

## The Superpower
A full night’s sleep with REM stages helps you learn and remember things better. You wake up sharper, ready to solve problems in school or sports.

## The Danger Zone
Missing REM sleep (like staying up late) means your brain can't organize memories well. You might forget things you just learned and feel more emotional or moody.

## Action Step
Make sure to sleep enough (about 8 hours) so your brain has time for REM cycles and memory filing.
