## The Simple Truth
When you sleep, your body goes through cycles like charging a device in stages. You move from light sleep to deep sleep to REM (dream) sleep, then repeat. Each cycle helps restore your body and brain by repairing muscles and filing memories.

## Everyday Example
If you sleep from 10pm to 6am, you complete about 4-5 cycles. Waking up at 6am after a full night of cycles, you feel fresh. If you sleep only 4 hours, you break cycles in the middle and feel groggy and tired.

## The Superpower
By sleeping enough to complete full cycles, you wake up energetic and clear-headed. Completing cycles means your body finished its repairs and your brain saved what you learned.

## The Danger Zone
Cutting sleep short means you interrupt a cycle (like waking in deep sleep), making you groggy and tired. Chronic lack of full cycles causes sleepiness, poor memory, and even health problems.

## Action Step
Try going to bed a bit earlier tonight to get at least 7-8 hours. This gives you enough time to finish your sleep cycles.
