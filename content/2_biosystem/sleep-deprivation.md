## The Simple Truth
Sleep deprivation means you don’t get enough sleep. It’s like using a phone on low battery all day. Your body and brain don’t finish charging, so you run low on energy and focus.

## Everyday Example
Pulling an all-nighter to study for a test means the next day you are yawning, have trouble focusing, and might feel sick.

## The Superpower
Getting enough sleep keeps your brain sharp and body energetic. You remember lessons better, have good moods, and stay healthy.

## The Danger Zone
Constant sleep deprivation makes you sickly, forgetful, and stressed. You might get headaches, gain weight, and fall asleep in class.

## Action Step
Try to go to bed at a regular time and sleep at least 7-8 hours each night. Even turning off devices 30 minutes earlier helps.
