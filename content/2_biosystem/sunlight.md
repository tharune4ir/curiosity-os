## The Simple Truth
Sunlight in the morning tells your body clock that it’s time to wake up. Light hitting your eyes stops melatonin production and makes you alert.

## Everyday Example
When you open the curtains or step outside for morning sunlight, you feel more awake. Sleeping in a dark room keeps you groggy.

## The Superpower
Getting morning sunlight helps set your circadian rhythm so you sleep better at night and wake up energized.

## The Danger Zone
Lacking morning light (staying in dark indoors) confuses your body clock and makes mornings hard.

## Action Step
Step outside or open a window for sunlight for a few minutes when you wake up.
