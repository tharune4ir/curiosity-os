## The Invisible Rule
Boring moments can push the mind into daydreaming, which can boost creativity and new connections. Brain networks active in mind-wandering are linked with imaginative thinking

## The Matrix Trap
Every tiny boredom moment (bus, waiting, toilet) gets filled with shorts/reels. Your brain never gets quiet time to connect ideas.

## The Escape Hatch
Take a “boredom snack”: 3 minutes with no screen. Just stare out the window or walk slowly. Then write 1 new idea or analogy

## The Superpower
You start getting ‘aha!’ moments that rote students never get, because they never give the brain quiet space.

## The Level Up Action
Today: do ONE 3-minute screen-free boredom break and write ONE idea right after.
