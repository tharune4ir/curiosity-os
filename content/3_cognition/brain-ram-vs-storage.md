## The Invisible Rule
Your brain has tiny “RAM” (working memory). It can hold only about 4 chunks at a time, like a phone with low RAM. Real learning is moving ideas from RAM into long-term storage by building patterns (schemas)

## The Matrix Trap
You try to swallow a full chapter in one sitting. It feels okay at home, but in the exam hall your mind freezes—like a game crashing at the boss fight.

## The Escape Hatch
Study like a data-transfer: take one small piece, understand it, then do 2 minutes of active recall (close book, say/write from memory). That recall is the “save button.”

## The Superpower
You stop feeling “I’m dumb.” You simply stop overloading RAM, so learning becomes calmer and faster.

## The Level Up Action
Pick ONE topic. Write 4 bullet points from memory. Open the book only to fix the bullets you missed.
