## The Invisible Rule
Struggling and then correcting can strengthen learning. ‘Desirable difficulties’ often improve long-term memory even if they feel worse short-term

## The Matrix Trap
You avoid hard questions because you hate getting wrong answers, so you never meet your real weak points.

## The Escape Hatch
Celebrate useful mistakes: attempt first, then check, then write the correction rule. Wrong → corrected = strong memory.

## The Superpower
You grow faster than students who only do easy work to protect ego.

## The Level Up Action
Do one hard question now. If wrong, write: “I was wrong because _____. Next time I will ____.”
