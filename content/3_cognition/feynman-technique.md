## The Invisible Rule
Explaining simply forces you to notice gaps, organize ideas, and connect them—very similar to self-explanation and learning-by-teaching effects

## The Matrix Trap
You use big words from the book and think that means you understand. In the exam, you can’t produce a clean answer.

## The Escape Hatch
Teach the idea using only simple words and one example. If you get stuck, that spot is your real weak point

## The Superpower
You become the student who can explain anything clearly—teachers love that, and exams reward that.

## The Level Up Action
Pick one concept and record a 60-second audio explaining it in your simplest English.
