## The Invisible Rule
Interleaving mixes different problem types, forcing you to choose the right method. Studies show it can improve math learning compared to blocked practice

## The Matrix Trap
You do 20 identical sums in a row. You get fast… but only because you already know the method. In the exam, you don’t know which method to use.

## The Escape Hatch
Mix 3 types in one set (example: linear equation, inequality, word problem). Now you train method-selection, not just repetition

## The Superpower
You become better at ‘surprise questions’ because your brain practiced choosing the tool.

## The Level Up Action
Take your homework set and rearrange it: no two same-type questions back-to-back.
