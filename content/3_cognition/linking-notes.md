## The Invisible Rule
Linking ideas supports sense-making. Tools like concept maps are network-like representations that help organize relationships and can support learning

## The Matrix Trap
You learn chapters as islands. In exams, you can’t connect ideas, so your answers stay weak.

## The Escape Hatch
After every note, link to 2 others: “similar to ___” and “different from ___.” This builds a brain-network, not a memory-list.

## The Superpower
You can write ‘application’ answers and higher-order answers because you see relationships.

## The Level Up Action
Pick one note and add two links: one ‘same family’ link and one ‘opposite’ link.
