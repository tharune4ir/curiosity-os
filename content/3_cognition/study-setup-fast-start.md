## The Invisible Rule
People fail more at “starting” than at “doing.” If-then planning (implementation intentions) increases the chance you actually begin

## The Matrix Trap
You wait for the perfect mood, the perfect playlist, the perfect pen… and 45 minutes disappear.

## The Escape Hatch
Make a default start: “If it’s 7:00 pm, then I sit, open notebook, and do Q1.” No debate. Same start daily

## The Superpower
You become consistent—even if you’re not ‘in the mood.’ Consistency beats genius in exams.

## The Level Up Action
Write your if-then start line now: “If it is ____, then I will ____.” Keep it on your desk.
