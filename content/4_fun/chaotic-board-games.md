## The Core Joy
Your brain loves safe drama. A game gives rules, so you can compete, argue, form alliances, and feel big emotions... without real damage. That shared emotional spike glues people together.

## The Memory Maker
Sunday afternoon. Cousins everywhere. Uno or Ludo on the floor. Someone plays a +4, someone screams 'CHEATER!', and five minutes later everybody is laughing at the same table.

## The Hidden Upgrade
You practice quick thinking, reading faces, and losing without melting down. You also learn how to come back after a fail.

## The Matrix Trap
Everyone in the same room, but each person is alone in their own scroll-loop, laughing quietly at different reels.

## The Weekend Mission
Start a 30-minute 'phone stack' rule: phones in one pile. Pick any game (cards, carrom, Ludo). Play one full round, no excuses.
