## The Core Joy
Friendly teasing is like adding masala: it makes things exciting. The joy comes from closeness-everyone knows it's not real hate, it's play.

## The Memory Maker
During badminton you say, 'Bro your smash is like a wet dosa.' They reply, 'Still better than your footwork.' Everyone laughs and plays harder.

## The Hidden Upgrade
You learn social intelligence: where the line is, how to read comfort, and how to keep competition fun, not cruel.

## The Matrix Trap
Comment wars with strangers online where jokes become attacks and nobody feels safe.

## The Weekend Mission
Try 'tease with permission': only tease about game skills, never body/looks/family. If someone says 'stop', you stop immediately and say sorry.
