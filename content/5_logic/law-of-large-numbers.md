## The Cheat Code
The Law of Large Numbers says that as you repeat an experiment many times, the average result gets closer to the true probability. More trials means more reliable results.

## The Casino Trap
People fall for short streaks. E.g., a coin flipped 10 times might show 8 heads, but that’s luck. They incorrectly think the coin is biased.

## The Real World Hack
A student rolls a dice 6 times and gets many sixes, thinking it’s lucky. Another rolls 100 times and sees about 1/6 sixes. The second trusts long-run averages.

## The Superpower
You trust math of big numbers, so you don’t make hasty decisions based on a few tries. You know bigger data is more accurate.

## The Mission
Flip a coin 20 times and see how close you get to 50% heads. The more flips, the closer you’ll get.
