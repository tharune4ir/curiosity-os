## The Cheat Code
A Nash equilibrium is a situation where no one can improve by changing strategy alone. Everyone’s choice matches up so nobody wants to switch by themselves.

## The Casino Trap
People sometimes stick with a bad plan because nobody will change if they do. For example, if everyone cheats in class, you think 'even if I study honestly, I won’t beat cheaters so why bother?'

## The Real World Hack
On a group project, everyone agreed to share tasks equally. No one wants to change the plan alone, because any change would hurt their own grade. That stable plan is a Nash equilibrium.

## The Superpower
You spot stable deals and also break bad ones. You see when changing strategy won’t help unless others change too.

## The Mission
Think of a situation where people keep doing the same thing. Ask: 'If I change my mind, will it help?' If not, it might be a stable situation.
