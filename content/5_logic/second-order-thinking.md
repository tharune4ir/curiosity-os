## The Cheat Code
Second-order thinking means you predict not just the result of your choice, but what happens after that. It’s like playing chess, planning several moves ahead.

## The Casino Trap
If you only react without predicting, others can outsmart you. For instance, you think failing a test is just one bad grade, but one step ahead you see it affects college plans.

## The Real World Hack
Your friend lies to you. Before you call them out, think: How might they react? If you react angrily now, they might never talk. Planning two steps ahead lets you stay calm and handle it better.

## The Superpower
You spot traps and long-term effects before anyone else. You avoid problems that surprise others later.

## The Mission
When deciding something, ask: 'If I do this, what will happen next?' then one more step after that.
