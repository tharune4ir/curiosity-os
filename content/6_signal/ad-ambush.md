## The Invisible Code
Everything you do online—likes, searches, even just hovering—teaches companies what you might want to buy. Then ads pop up like hidden traps. It's like walking in a market and suddenly seeing a shop that seems to know exactly what candy you wanted when you were a kid. Those targeted ads follow your interests closely to make you click.

## The Digital Trap
You search for 'best backpacks' on one website, and the next day your social media feed is full of backpack ads—even in unrelated apps. You feel like they're spying on you. You click one ad out of curiosity and it takes you to a shady shopping site. The more you click, the more ads chase you around the internet. It feels impossible to escape.

## The Shield Protocol
Treat ads with suspicion. If an ad in your app or site looks like it was made just for you, that’s how it’s supposed to be. Avoid clicking on it. Use an ad-blocker if your device allows it. You can also limit what data you share: for example, turn off ‘ad personalization’ in Google or Facebook settings. Think of ad-blocking as wearing armor in the market alley.

## The Lifeline
If you accidentally clicked on a shady ad or feel overwhelmed by too many ads, don't hide it. Tell a parent or sibling: 'I clicked something weird, what do I do?' They might help clear cookies or guide you through safe practices. There’s no shame—ads are designed to trick even grown-ups. Together you can remove that digital spy out of your life.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to your privacy settings on social media and turn off ad personalization. Also clear your browser history and cookies right now to reset the ads that follow you. Then, whenever you see an ad, ask yourself: 'Did I search for this or did it pop up randomly?'. If it’s random, don’t click it.
