## The Invisible Code
AI can write entire news articles now, but it doesn't actually understand truth. It's like a robot copying patterns from text it has seen. Sometimes it fabricates facts confidently. If you don't double check, you might mistake robot-made fake news for real.

## The Digital Trap
You read an article on a small site that seems real but is AI-written. It claims a natural disaster happened nearby. You get scared and spread the warning. Later, no such disaster occurred. You realize the 'article' was entirely made up by AI.

## The Shield Protocol
Look for signs of AI writing: a very generic writing style, or mistakes like mixing up small details, or saying 'in a statement the company said' without quoting anyone. If something sounds off, check reliable news outlets to confirm. Treat one small site article like an unverified rumor unless a well-known source also reports it.

## The Lifeline
If you acted on AI-generated news, tell someone immediately: 'I think I shared a made-up news.' They can clarify the facts and help correct the message out there. Remind yourself it's hard to tell sometimes – even adults get fooled by AI stories.

## The Empowerment Move
Use AI yourself to test: ask ChatGPT 'Tell me about [recent news topic].' If it wildly makes up an event that isn't in trustworthy sources, you'll see how easily it lies. It’s an eye-opener for understanding AI's limits.
