## The Invisible Code
Apps and games often give you small rewards (a like, a new level, a funny video) just when you need it, to keep you coming back. It's like a shopkeeper giving you a free candy each time you glance at something. These little rewards train you to return again and again, often without you meaning to.

## The Digital Trap
You start a new game that gives you a reward for logging in every day. At first it's fun. Soon, missing one day makes you feel guilty. Before you know it, you log in multiple times a day and check constantly for your daily bonus. You feel anxious if you don't get your treat, and you forgot to study because the game was always calling you back.

## The Shield Protocol
Treat those in-app rewards like tricks. If a game or app gives you a prize, that's fine, but remind yourself it's just to bring you back. Set strict rules: after you check, immediately close the game. You can also set a schedule, like 'I will play after homework'. Apps and games know you love rewards, so make sure you're the one controlling when to have them.

## The Lifeline
It's okay to enjoy games and have fun, and it's normal to feel a little upset when you break your own rule. Everyone gets sucked into a game sometimes. If you feel guilty or anxious about it, talk to your parents: 'I can't stop checking this game.' They might set parental controls or just help you plan other fun things so you’re not alone with the game.

## The Empowerment Move
Enable app timers or parental controls on your games right away. Set them to only let you play 30 minutes a day. Also, choose one fun offline activity (like drawing or chatting with a friend) to do immediately after you log off, so your brain gets used to something else being rewarding too.
