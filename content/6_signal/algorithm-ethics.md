## The Invisible Code
Algorithms are often designed to be efficient, but that doesn't always mean they're fair. Algorithm ethics is the study of how to make these machines more just and less biased. Sometimes, an algorithm might accidentally treat certain groups of people unfairly because of how it was trained. Recognizing this helps you be a more critical user of technology.

## The Digital Trap
You see a news story about an AI that was used to choose the best players for a team, but it consistently skipped talented players from a certain school. You realize the AI had a hidden bias. It wasn't 'thinking' fairly, it was just following a flawed pattern. You fell for the idea that the machine is always right.

## The Shield Protocol
Be critical of 'automated' decisions. If an app or site treats someone unfairly, speak up! Support developers who work on 'open source' and fair AI. Ask yourself: 'Is this machine being fair, or is it just following a bias?' Staying aware and questioning automated systems is how we keep the digital world just and equal for everyone.

## The Lifeline
If you see something online that seems unfair because of an algorithm, talk to a teacher or parent: 'I think this app is biased against some people.' they'll help you understand how machines learn and explain what we can do to make them better. They love that you're being a socially aware citizen and will help you speak up. Together we can build a fairer digital world!

## The Empowerment Move
Research one story about 'algorithmic bias' (like in facial recognition) right now. Tell one friend about what you found. It's a big step toward being a more aware and ethical user of technology!
