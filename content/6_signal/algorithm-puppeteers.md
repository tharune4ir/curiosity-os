## The Invisible Code
A secret programmer tells a story by choosing what posts and videos you see. It's like an invisible puppeteer pulling strings behind your phone, deciding each video or post that will make you feel excited, scared, or happy. You don't see it happening, you just see a never-ending show that feels like magic.

## The Digital Trap
Imagine you open a video app and start watching a funny clip. When it ends, another starts. And another. You watch one video after another without realizing how long you've been on your phone. The puppeteer is showing you exactly these videos because they know you like them. You end up spending hours without even noticing.

## The Shield Protocol
Know that the app is built to keep you watching with one video after another. The first step is to notice when it happens: if you keep watching without thinking, an algorithm is pulling your strings. Set a goal for how long you'll watch before you even open the app (like 10 minutes) and use your phone's timer or a reminder to stop when time is up.

## The Lifeline
You might feel surprised at how quickly time disappeared or embarrassed that you couldn't stop. That's normal – even adults get caught by endless videos. Talk to a trusted older sibling or parent and tell them how it felt to be unable to stop. They can help you come up with a healthy plan, because apps are designed to grab your attention and you'll feel better having a plan together.

## The Empowerment Move
Before you open any social media or video app, set a 10-minute timer on your phone (or use the screen time limit feature). When it rings, take a deep breath, put the phone down, and tell someone what you watched.
