## The Invisible Code
Big internet companies don't just want your likes; they want your time and attention. In simple terms, your attention is their currency. They use every trick—like bright colors, games, and viral videos—to keep you staring at the screen. It's like you're a prized player in a game and the apps are competing to capture your attention and keep it as long as possible.

## The Digital Trap
Imagine opening a social media app. On your screen, you see notifications, bright images, and games that you like. Each time you click or watch, the app counts it as a win. You're hooked by one thing after another—like you got trapped in a puzzle box that keeps giving you hints and rewards so you don't leave. By the end, you realize you've been there all afternoon and didn't even do your homework!

## The Shield Protocol
Think of your time like money in your pocket. Before you give it away, you want to make sure it’s worth it. Decide how much time you’ll spend online each day and stick to it. Don't be afraid to close the app for a while. Also, turn off notifications for everything that is not important (like random games or social apps). It's like switching off a neon sign in a shop so the flashy ads don’t distract you.

## The Lifeline
It's easy to feel guilty about wasting time, but remember, the apps are designed to make you do just that—they want your attention more than your money. Even busy grown-ups struggle with this. Talk to your parents or a teacher and say, 'I spent too much time on my phone.' They won’t scold you; they might share their own experience of trying to cut down screen time. You can ask them to help you keep track.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, go into your phone settings and set a daily screen time limit for social media apps (like 30 minutes per app). Or uninstall one game that eats your time. Every time your phone reminds you you've hit the limit, take a break and step outside for fresh air or do something offline you enjoy.
