## The Invisible Code
Your attention span is how long you can focus on a single task without getting distracted. The internet, with its quick videos and flashing ads, is designed to break your focus into small pieces. Protecting your attention span means training your brain to stay focused, which is a key skill for learning and doing your best work.

## The Digital Trap
You tried to read one chapter of a book, but every few minutes you checked your phone for a notification. After an hour, you'd only read two pages and felt frustrated. You realize that your attention span has become so short that you can't even focus on something you enjoy. You've been trained to be distracted by the apps you use.

## The Shield Protocol
Practice focus exercises: try to do one task (like reading, drawing, or a puzzle) for 15 minutes without any interruptions. Gradually increase the time. Use 'Focus Mode' on your device to block distractions. Remember, focus is like a muscle—the more you train it, the stronger it gets. Be the master of your own mind, not a slave to every notification!

## The Lifeline
If you're struggling to stay focused and it's affecting your schoolwork, talk to a teacher or parent: 'I feel like I can't concentrate on anything for long.' they'll give you some focus tips and help you set up a distraction-free work area. They love that you're being self-aware and will support you. Together we can strengthen your attention span!

## The Empowerment Move
Find a book or a craft project right now and try to work on it for just 10 minutes without looking at your phone. If you feel the urge to check, just take a deep breath and keep going. You've just trained your 'Attention Span'!
