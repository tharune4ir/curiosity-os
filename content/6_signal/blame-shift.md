## The Invisible Code
Trickers might try to put the blame on you to keep you quiet. They will say things like, 'If you hadn’t done X, this wouldn’t happen,' making you feel guilty. It's a tactic like a magician misdirection—make you focus on your error while they escape accountability.

## The Digital Trap
After clicking a shady link, a scammer contacts you and says, 'This is happening because you were stupid. No one will help you now.' You feel ashamed and afraid to talk about it, thinking maybe they’re right.

## The Shield Protocol
When someone blames you, take a step back. Remember: scammers are trying to confuse you. Practice saying to yourself: 'This is not my fault; it's on them for tricking me.' Even if you made a mistake, that doesn't mean you deserve trouble. People make mistakes online all the time.

## The Lifeline
Tell someone you trust: 'This person said it was my fault.' Hearing yourself say it out loud makes it clearer that it's a lie. Adults know this tactic and will reassure you that it wasn’t your fault. You’ll feel a lot better knowing someone stands by you instead of blaming you.

## The Empowerment Move
Write down on paper or in your phone: 'If you blamed me, it’s not my fault.' Keep it handy. Whenever someone online tries to make you feel guilty, read it. You can also practice saying, 'Let me ask an adult about this' before reacting, to give yourself time to think clearly.
