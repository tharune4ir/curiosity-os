## The Invisible Code
Some people make fake profiles online and pretend to be someone they’re not. It's like a masked stranger at a playground pretending to be your classmate. The mask is a profile photo and some info. They use this disguise to trick you into trusting them.

## The Digital Trap
One day, you receive a friend request from a good-looking teen girl who says she goes to your school. She chats with you, asks personal questions, and seems friendly. You feel happy. Later, she asks for a picture of you or your location. It feels weird but you fear you might lose her friendship so you share. After that, her questions get creepier. You eventually find out it was a stranger all along.

## The Shield Protocol
Remember: always verify who is behind the screen. If someone new messages you, call out their real name or ask something only a real friend would know. If they dodge it, it's a red flag. Also check if they have common friends or multiple pictures on social media – if not, be suspicious.

## The Lifeline
If you realize you've been talking to a fake account, you might feel embarrassed or angry. It's okay to feel that way, but don't stay silent. Tell a parent or teacher immediately: 'Someone who I thought was my friend online isn't real.' They have seen this happen and can help protect you. They're not mad; they just want to keep you safe.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, review your friend list. If anyone's profile seems odd (no shared friends, weird pictures), block and delete them. Also ask a sibling or friend to show you how to report or block suspicious accounts on your social media apps.
