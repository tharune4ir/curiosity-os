## The Invisible Code
Curiosity is great, but apps and websites try to exploit it by making everything look like a mystery you just have to click. It's a habit of clicking first and thinking later. Stopping that habitual click means taking back control of your attention and being a more conscious user of the web.

## The Digital Trap
You're browsing and see a 'hidden' link with a mysterious icon. Without thinking, you click it. It takes you to a site full of ads and confusing buttons. You realize you clicked just because you were curious, not because it was really helpful. You wasted time on a digital distraction that was designed to hook your curiosity.

## The Shield Protocol
Practice a 'pause before you click' habit. Every time you see a link or button that looks exciting but mysterious, wait for 3 seconds. Ask yourself: 'Why am I clicking this? Will it help me or just distract me?' If it's the latter, just ignore it and move on. You're training your brain to be in control, not your curiosity!

## The Lifeline
If you've been clicking too many random things and feel overwhelmed, talk to a parent: 'I feel like I'm just clicking everything without thinking.' they'll help you find ways to focus and explain how websites are designed to distract. They love that you're being honest and will help you regain your focus. Together you can be more mindful online!

## The Empowerment Move
For the next 10 minutes that you're online, try to only click on three things that you actually planned to view. If you feel the urge to click a 'mystery' link, wait 10 seconds and then decide. You've just practiced 'Mindful Clicking'!
