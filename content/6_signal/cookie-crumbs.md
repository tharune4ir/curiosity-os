## The Invisible Code
Websites often use cookies (small data files) to track what you do. It's like leaving bread crumbs everywhere you go on the internet. These crumbs help companies follow you around, showing you more stuff based on your tracks, even beyond what you search for.

## The Digital Trap
You search for new shoes on one site and then suddenly see shoe ads on every other site. You realize sites are using cookies to remember you. This isn't a trap that steals money, but it feels creepy to be watched so closely.

## The Shield Protocol
Go to your browser’s settings and block third-party cookies or use incognito mode for searching sensitive things. Regularly clear cookies and your browser history. It's like erasing your trail on a sandbox before closing your app. This helps reduce how much your interests are tracked and targeted.

## The Lifeline
Explain to an adult: 'I found out websites were following me with cookies.' They might help adjust privacy settings or install an ad-blocker. You'll learn together that being tracked is normal but we can manage it. You’re taking control, not being paranoid.

## The Empowerment Move
Clear your browser cookies right now (find 'Clear browsing data' in your browser settings). Notice how some sites forget you're logged in—that's okay. Now, use a site as guest or incognito for a bit and feel the difference in ad types.
