## The Invisible Code
Cyber resilience is the ability to recover and adapt when something goes wrong online (like a hack, a scam, or a bullying incident). It's like being a digital athlete: you might get knocked down, but you have the strength and tools to get back up and keep going. Building resilience means being prepared and knowing who to turn to for help.

## The Digital Trap
You were tricked by a phishing link and lost access to your favorite game account. You feel sad, angry, and defeated. You want to just quit the internet forever. You were hit by a digital setback and didn't know how to bounce back. You felt like your online life was over just because of one mistake.

## The Shield Protocol
Recognize that everyone makes mistakes online. If something bad happens, take a deep breath and tell a trusted adult immediately. They can help you fix the problem and give you support. Learn from the experience so you're better prepared next time. Think of every digital challenge as a way to build your 'bounce-back' muscles!

## The Lifeline
If you're struggling after an online incident, talk to a parent or counselor: 'Something bad happened online and I'm feeling really down.' they'll help you handle the fallout and explain that it's okay to feel upset. They love that you're being brave and showing resilience, and will support you every step of the way. Together we can bounce back and be stronger than ever!

## The Empowerment Move
Think of one thing that could go wrong online (like a lost password) and write down a simple plan for what you would do (like 'I'll tell my dad immediately'). Having a plan is the first step to being cyber resilient!
