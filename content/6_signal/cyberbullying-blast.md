## The Invisible Code
Online bullies use anonymity to spread rumors or insults from behind a screen. It's like someone throwing stones at your house at night so you can't see who they are. Because it's behind a screen, they feel braver to say mean things they wouldn't say in person.

## The Digital Trap
One day, an embarrassing photo of you is shared in a class group chat with cruel jokes. You feel hurt but it's from anonymous accounts or maybe people too scared to own up. You see your classmates laughing. You wonder if you did something wrong to deserve this, but you didn't. The bully is hiding behind the chat.

## The Shield Protocol
Don’t respond to bullies or try to fight back online; it often just makes things worse. Instead, block and report the bullies. Save evidence by taking screenshots (just in case). Remember: the target is you, not your worth. Try to focus on your strengths and ignore the noise as best you can.

## The Lifeline
No matter what, tell a trusted adult immediately. Show them the messages. The cyber bully wants you to feel alone and embarrassed. When you tell someone, the bully loses power. Your parents or teachers can reach out and stop the harassment. You are not weak for asking for help; you are being brave!

## The Empowerment Move
Set your social media profiles to private right now to stop strangers from contacting you. Then talk to a parent or school counselor and agree that from now on, if anything like this happens, you'll tell them immediately. Knowing someone is there for you is your best shield.
