## The Invisible Code
Data brokers are companies that collect and sell information about people—your likes, age, location, and even what you search for. They're like digital spies who watch what you do and then sell those 'clues' to other companies. This data is used to target you with ads or even influence your opinions. Knowing they exist helps you be more careful with your data.

## The Digital Trap
You use three different apps and they all seem to know you're interested in a new video game. You realize that those apps are all sharing or selling your data to a central broker. Suddenly, your whole online world is filled with ads for that game. Your personal interests have become a product for sale, and you never even gave permission.

## The Shield Protocol
Use privacy-focused browsers and tools that block trackers. Avoid sharing sensitive info on public profiles. Be very careful with 'Free' apps and services, as they often make money by selling your data. Think of your info like a precious gem: you only show it to people you really trust, and you definitely don't let strangers sell it!

## The Lifeline
If you're concerned about companies selling your data, talk to a parent: 'I found out companies sell my info, how can I stop it?' they'll show you how to use privacy tools and help you adjust your settings. They're happy you're being a smart consumer and will support you in protecting your data. Together you can be less of a product and more of a person!

## The Empowerment Move
Go to a site like 'OptOut.info' (or a similar local tool) and see if you can limit how much your info is sold. Also, switch your search engine to something more private like DuckDuckGo for one week. Notice how different your search results and ads look when you're not being tracked as closely.
