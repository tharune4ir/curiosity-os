## The Invisible Code
Data sovereignty is the idea that you should have ultimate control and ownership over your personal data—who can see it, use it, and sell it. It's like being the king or queen of your own information. You should have the power to decide what happens to your digital footprints and not just have them taken by companies.

## The Digital Trap
You wanted to delete an old account but found out the app has the right to keep your data forever. You feel like you've lost control over your own online life. You realize you don't have sovereignty over your personal info because the company decided for you. You were treated as a product rather than an owner.

## The Shield Protocol
Support laws and organizations that fight for your right to control your data. Read the privacy section of apps carefully and choose services that let you delete your info easily. Think of your data as your personal property: it belongs to you, and you should be the one who decides its fate! Staying informed is your best way to maintain your data sovereignty.

## The Lifeline
If you're frustrated that you can't control your data, talk to a parent: 'I found out some apps keep my info forever, how can I stop it?' they'll explain that laws are changing to give users more rights and help you research better apps. They love that you're being a smart and self-aware citizen. Together we can fight for your right to own your digital life!

## The Empowerment Move
Research one 'Data Privacy' law (like GDPR or a local one) right now to see what rights you have over your data. Tell a parent or friend about one thing you learned. You're building your awareness of 'Data Sovereignty'!
