## The Invisible Code
Deepfakes are videos or audios created by AI that make real people appear to say or do things they never did. They are very realistic and can be very convincing. It's like a digital mask that can be put on anyone’s face. Spotting them is becoming a critical skill in our modern world.

## The Digital Trap
You see a video of a famous celebrity saying something completely out of character. It looks very real and you're shocked. You share it and it goes viral. But later you find out the video was a deepfake created by an AI. You feel embarrassed and realize you helped spread a sophisticated lie.

## The Shield Protocol
Look for small mistakes: weird eye movements, blurry edges around the mouth, or sound that doesn't match the lips. If a video shows someone saying something crazy or unbelievable, verify it on a trusted news site first. If no one else is reporting it, it's likely a deepfake. Be a smart observer and don't be easily fooled by lifelike fakes!

## The Lifeline
If you've seen a video that seems too wild to be true, ask an adult: 'Does this look real to you? It sounds a bit off.' they'll show you how to look for those tiny AI mistakes and explain how deepfakes are made. They love that you're being a careful viewer and will help you distinguish between real and fake. Together you can spot the code behind the mask!

## The Empowerment Move
Next time you see a 'surprising' video of a public figure, look closely at their eyes and mouth for 10 seconds. Then search for the exact quote on a trusted news site. If you can't find it, assume it’s a deepfake. You've just practiced a high-tech detective skill!
