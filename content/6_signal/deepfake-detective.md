## The Invisible Code
Deepfakes are videos or audio made by computers that look or sound real but are fake, like a hologram mimicking a person. It's like a shapeshifter in a movie, pretending to be someone you know. Computers analyze faces and voices and remake them convincingly.

## The Digital Trap
You see a video of a teacher saying shocking things; it looks real because the face and voice are perfect. You share it, and everyone is upset. Later a news report says it was fake (deepfake). You feel guilty for spreading lies.

## The Shield Protocol
Always double-check funny or shocking videos. If someone is saying unbelievable things, search the internet or trusted news – real outlets would cover it. Look for clues: does the person's mouth move a bit oddly, or does their voice sound robotic? When in doubt, pause and don't share.

## The Lifeline
Admit to someone: 'I shared a video that was fake.' You'll learn that people make mistakes with new tech tricks. Explaining it helps you understand and never do it again. Teachers or parents will likely discuss how to verify and be proud you recognized it eventually.

## The Empowerment Move
Watch one short video tutorial on how deepfakes are made (like on YouTube). Understanding how they're made helps you see the signs. Also, the next time you're unsure about a video, ask a friend 'Do you think this is real or deepfake?' and talk about it before sharing.
