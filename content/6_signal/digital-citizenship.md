## The Invisible Code
Digital citizenship is about being a responsible, kind, and ethical member of the online world. It means treating others with respect, protecting your privacy, and using technology for good. It's like being a good neighbor in your local community, but on a global scale. Your actions help make the internet a better place for everyone.

## The Digital Trap
You see someone being bullied in a comment section and you decide to just stay quiet. You feel like it's not your problem. But later you realize that by staying quiet, you were letting the bully win. You missed your chance to be a good digital citizen and stand up for what's right. You weren't a participant, just a witness.

## The Shield Protocol
Always be kind and respectful in your comments and messages. Support others who are being treated unfairly. Protect your own privacy and help others do the same. If you find something helpful or inspiring, share it! Being a good digital citizen means using your superpower for good and encouraging others to do the same.

## The Lifeline
If you're ever unsure about the right thing to do online, talk to a teacher or parent: 'What should I have done in that situation?' they'll help you understand the best way to be a responsible user and explain how to handle tricky online social rules. They love that you're being a thoughtful person and will support you. Together we can be the best digital citizens possible!

## The Empowerment Move
The next time you see someone do something kind online, leave a positive comment or 'like' their post. It’s a small step that makes the internet a better place. You're practicing your 'Digital Citizenship'!
