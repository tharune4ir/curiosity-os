## The Invisible Code
Digital empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others online. Because we can't see people's faces or hear their voices, it’s easy to forget that there’s a real person with real feelings on the other side of every screen. Practicing digital empathy means being kind and considering how your words might affect others.

## The Digital Trap
You left a quick, mean comment on a video you didn't like. You felt like it was just a joke and didn't matter. But then you see the person who made the video replied, saying they were very hurt. You realize you forgot there was a real human being behind that video. You failed to use your digital empathy and caused real pain without even meaning to.

## The Shield Protocol
Before you post a comment or message, imagine saying it to the person's face. If it would be unkind or hurtful in person, don't say it online! Use emojis and kind words to show that you care. Support others who are being treated poorly. Being kind is a choice you make every time you're online. Treat everyone with the same respect you want for yourself!

## The Lifeline
If you've been hurt by someone's words online or feel guilty about something you said, talk to an adult: 'Someone was mean to me online' or 'I think I was mean to someone else.' they'll help you handle the situation and explain how to be more empathetic. They love that you're being a thoughtful and kind digital citizen. Together we can make the internet more empathetic and welcoming for everyone!

## The Empowerment Move
The next time you see a post or video you really like, leave a short and kind comment right now. Notice how good it feels to spread a little digital kindness. You're practicing 'Digital Empathy'!
