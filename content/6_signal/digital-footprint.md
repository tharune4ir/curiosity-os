## The Invisible Code
Everything you post, like, or share online leaves a mark. It's like walking on wet sand: your footprints stay even after you go. Future teachers, friends, or even strangers can look back at what you've done. Unlike sand, though, your digital footprints might never disappear unless you try very hard.

## The Digital Trap
You post an embarrassing video of yourself dancing in front of friends. It's fun at the moment, but weeks later you apply for a school exam and see the examiner liked your video profile. You feel embarrassed and worried. The video you thought was private now follows you around, hurting your confidence.

## The Shield Protocol
Before you post or share anything, ask: 'Could this be a problem later?' If it's personal or embarrassing, it's safer to keep it offline. Clean up older accounts: delete or deactivate any pages you don't use. Use privacy settings so only friends can see your posts. It's like checking the whiteboard after class to erase what you don't want everyone to remember.

## The Lifeline
If someone finds an old post or photo of you and it upsets you, talk to a parent or teacher. They won't be mad; they'll help you handle it. Sometimes they may know how to delete it or hide it. Always remember, the internet can hold onto mistakes, but adults have tools and know how to help fix them.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, Google yourself or your username to see what others might find. Delete any old or embarrassing profiles you don't need. Also, adjust your social media privacy to 'Friends Only' instead of 'Public'. From now on, pause and think for 5 seconds before posting anything.
