## The Invisible Code
Digital wellbeing means having a healthy relationship with technology. It's about using the internet for good (learning, connecting) while also having plenty of time for offline life (playing, resting, family). It’s like eating a balanced meal: some digital treats are fine, but you need your real-world 'veggies' too!

## The Digital Trap
You love your favorite game so much that you spend all your time playing it. You stop seeing your friends in person and your grades start to drop. You feel tired and stressed, and even the game isn't as fun anymore. You've lost your balance and don't even realize it.

## The Shield Protocol
Set boundaries for your screen time. Use app timers or parental controls to help you stick to them. Prioritize offline activities like sports, reading, or just chatting with family. If you feel like your phone is 'calling' you even when it's off, that’s a sign you need a break. Be the master of your technology, not its servant!

## The Lifeline
If you feel like your online life is taking over too much, talk to a parent: 'I think I'm spending too much time on my screen.' they'll help you set healthy limits and find fun offline things to do. They love that you're being aware and will support you in finding a better balance. Together you can live a happy, balanced life!

## The Empowerment Move
Plan one 'Offline Hour' for yourself today where you put your phone in another room and do something you love, like playing a board game or drawing. Notice how much better you feel when you're fully in the real world. You're building your digital wellbeing!
