## The Invisible Code
Platforms are built like magic carpets that never stop. No matter how much you scroll, new content keeps appearing. It's like a story with no ending—every time you try to quit, more scrolls pop up. The algorithm keeps the feed going so you stay glued to the screen for hours.

## The Digital Trap
You open your favorite app to see just one or two new posts. But before you know it, you've scrolled for an hour. New photos, videos, and jokes kept coming without pause. You got so absorbed that you missed your favorite show on TV because you were still scrolling.

## The Shield Protocol
Understand there is no real 'last page' on these apps. Use a timer or set a daily limit on your phone. Tell yourself, 'Okay, after 5 minutes of scrolling, I'll do something else.' When you notice your finger automatically flicking up, that's your cue to stop. You control the scroll, not the app.

## The Lifeline
It might feel embarrassing or hard to admit that you spent so much time scrolling. But remember, these apps are designed to do that – even parents often fall for it. Talk to a parent or elder sibling and say, 'I lost track of time on my phone.' They won’t scold you. Together you can agree on some limits or fun offline plans.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, set a 5-minute timer on your phone before you open any social app. When the timer goes off, even if you're on something interesting, put your phone away and go do one thing you enjoy, like grabbing a snack or talking to someone in person.
