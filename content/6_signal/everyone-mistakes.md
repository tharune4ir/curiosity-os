## The Invisible Code
No one is immune to digital trickery. It's like tripping on a flat floor – it happens to the best of us. Scammers design tricks to fool anyone. Think of it as a hidden banana peel: anyone can slip, not just kids or tech newbies.

## The Digital Trap
You clicked a link in a message and got a virus. You think 'I'm so dumb,' but keep it secret. You imagine your friends and family would laugh or be disappointed if they knew.

## The Shield Protocol
Replace 'I should have known better' with 'It could happen to anyone.' Tell yourself stories of teachers or parents who've done the same. This calms panic. Realize that the problem is with the bad link, not you. It takes the shame away and lets you focus on fixing it.

## The Lifeline
Share your story: 'Mom, I clicked on something suspicious.' You might find she or another adult also did that once. Sharing the 'everyone slips' fact will make you feel normal and supported. Adults won't judge; they'll just want to help.

## The Empowerment Move
Look up on your phone 'famous phishing stories' for a couple of minutes. You'll see news about even companies getting hacked or people like tech CEOs fooled. This reminds you mistakes happen to everyone.
