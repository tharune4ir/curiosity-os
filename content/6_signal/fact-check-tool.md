## The Invisible Code
The internet has detectives called fact-checkers (like Snopes, AltNews in India). You can use search engines to see if something has already been debunked. Searching for 'fact check' along with the claim is like using a flashlight in the dark: it makes hidden truths visible.

## The Digital Trap
You see a message claiming a popular politician said something outrageous in a speech. You feel outraged too. Instead of sharing, you do a quick search for 'speech politician claim fact check' and find a credible site saying it's false. You catch the lie just in time.

## The Shield Protocol
Develop a habit: any time you see a dramatic claim or image, pause and search 'is this true'. Add 'fact-check' or check the first page of search results for credible sources. If only blogs or social media have it, it's probably false.

## The Lifeline
If you already forwarded something false and someone asks you about it, say 'Actually I saw a fact-check that said it's not true.' This shows you took responsibility and educated yourself. Your friends may learn to do the same.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to Google and type 'fact check [recent viral claim]'. See if any fact-check websites (like AFP, India Today Fact Check, etc.) come up. Do this once a week with a trending topic as practice.
