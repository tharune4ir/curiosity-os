## The Invisible Code
Not everything you read or see online is true. A lot of info is made up to trick people or just to be funny. Fact-checking is the skill of verifying information by looking for evidence from reliable sources. It's like being a detective: you hunt for the truth instead of just believing what you're told.

## The Digital Trap
You see a post claiming that school will be closed tomorrow because of a surprise holiday. Excited, you share it with your friends. But later you find out it was just a prank. Everyone is annoyed that they got excited for nothing. You realize you should have checked the school's official website or asked a parent first.

## The Shield Protocol
Before you believe or share anything that sounds too good to be true (or too scary), do a quick check. Search for the info on a well-known news site like BBC, CNN, or your local news. Look for at least two different sources that say the same thing. If only one weird site mentions it, it's likely false. Become a truth detective!

## The Lifeline
If you're not sure if something is true, ask an adult: 'Is this real? I found it online.' They have more experience and can help you verify it. They're proud that you're being careful with the truth. Sharing your detective work with them is a great way to learn together. Remember, it's better to check and be sure than to share a lie.

## The Empowerment Move
Next time you see a surprising 'fact' online, try to find it on a second website right now using Google. If you can't find it anywhere else, assume it might be a lie. Practice being a detective with every big news story you see!
