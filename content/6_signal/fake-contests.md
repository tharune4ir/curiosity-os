## The Invisible Code
Contests online sometimes ask for likes and shares, but real contests usually have clear rules and no secret tricks. Fake contests use your excitement to spread their fake ad faster. It's like a playground rumor: 'Tell 10 friends, win a toy!' – if it sounds made up, it probably is.

## The Digital Trap
A page on social media posts: 'Win an iPad if you like and share this post!' You eagerly follow instructions and share. Weeks later, you get a message 'Click here to claim your prize.' You click, but it's just a survey collecting your data. No iPad ever comes. You realize it was a scam to get clicks.

## The Shield Protocol
Ask: 'Where are the rules and proof of winners?' If a contest has no official site or rules, it's likely a scam. Don't click on posts just because your friends do. Skip 'share to win' posts unless it's from an official, trusted source (like a well-known company page you recognize). Real contests don't usually spread like wildfire among random pages.

## The Lifeline
If you joined a fake contest and shared the post, you might feel silly. Don't keep it secret – tell a parent or teacher. They will help you delete the post (so it stops spreading) and check if any info was given to scammers. Everyone falls for these tricks; the important part is stopping it and learning.

## The Empowerment Move
Scroll through your posts and delete any contest shares you see. When you see a contest ad, search the company on Google to see if it's real. If it doesn’t have a real website, ignore it. Also, limit how much you share - only share contests you and your family know about.
