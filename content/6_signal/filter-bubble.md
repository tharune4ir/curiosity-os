## The Invisible Code
You have a special pair of glasses online. Every site or app puts on your glasses and only lets you see things you like. Anything outside those glasses is hidden. This is how filter bubbles work: the algorithm decides which things to show you based on your past likes, making your online world smaller and smaller.

## The Digital Trap
You always click on football videos, so your friends comment on a cricket match video you shared saying: 'Why do you only talk football? Are you ignoring cricket?' You barely even know what happened in cricket because your filter bubble mostly showed football stuff. You feel annoyed and maybe confused, not knowing the rest of the story.

## The Shield Protocol
Remember that your feed is customized. Actively seek out news or posts that are NOT in your usual bubble. Follow someone different, or go to a news site and browse categories you usually skip. It's like stepping outside your house into the real world. Even if it feels strange, it gives you the bigger picture.

## The Lifeline
It might feel odd or upsetting to see things you normally avoid, but talking helps. Tell an older person, 'I only see one kind of thing on my feed. Should I look at something else?' Maybe they have other news or pictures to show you. This will help you learn new things and realize others think differently, which is a good thing.

## The Empowerment Move
Subscribe or follow one account that posts a different topic from what you always read. Or go to Wikipedia and look up something totally random for 5 minutes. Every time you find something new, you're popping a bubble of the algorithm!
