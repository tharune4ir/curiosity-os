## The Invisible Code
Grooming is when a stranger online tries to become your friend or gain your trust so they can eventually hurt or trick you. It’s a slow process, starting with compliments, gifts, or secrets. They try to make you feel like they are your only real friend, while trying to isolate you from your family.

## The Digital Trap
A stranger joins a game you're playing and starts giving you free items. Soon, they're chatting with you every day, saying how cool you are. Then they say: 'Don't tell your parents, they wouldn't understand our friendship.' You feel flattered but also a bit nervous. Eventually, they ask to meet you in person. You realize too late it was a trap to get you alone.

## The Shield Protocol
Recognize the signs: avoid people who try to keep your friendship a secret from your family. If someone asks for personal info, photos, or to meet in person, that's a huge danger sign! Never go anywhere to meet someone you only know online. Tell an adult immediately if someone makes you feel uncomfortable or asks you to keep secrets.

## The Lifeline
If a stranger has been talking to you and you’re scared or confused, tell a parent or teacher right away: 'Someone I met online is acting weird.' They will help you block the person and keep you safe. You won't get in trouble for talking to them – the adult just wants to protect you. Being honest is your strongest shield against bad strangers.

## The Empowerment Move
Set a strict rule for yourself right now: never share your full name, address, or school with anyone you only know online. If you ever feel pressured to keep a secret, tell a parent immediately. Practice saying 'no' to requests that feel even slightly weird.
