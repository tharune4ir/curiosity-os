## The Invisible Code
Reverse image search lets you find where else a picture is on the internet. It's like uploading a photo into Google's detective mode. If the same photo appears on other news or image sites from years ago, you know it's not new.

## The Digital Trap
You find a photo of a man lighting a candle and a caption says 'Celebration at Kumbh Mela'. You share it thinking it’s from the current event. Later, you learn it was a different event from years ago, and you realize the context was wrong.

## The Shield Protocol
When you see a photo used as news, hold off on sharing. Save or screenshot it and do a reverse image search (using Google Images or apps). If it shows up as an older event, you’ll know it's miscaptioned.

## The Lifeline
Share with friends: 'Hey, check this out, this photo is actually from 2010, not today!' This way you teach them too. You become the fact-finder, not the rumor starter. People will appreciate that you’re helping them get the truth.

## The Empowerment Move
Download Google Lens or a similar app. The next time you see a news photo that puzzles you, open Lens on it. Use it to find similar images online. Practicing once or twice will make it quick to do later.
