## The Invisible Code
Some people steal real photos and names to create fake profiles. It's like a puppet show where someone uses a friend’s picture to talk to you. You think you're talking to someone you know, but it’s actually a stranger using their face.

## The Digital Trap
You see a post from your best friend's profile saying: 'I'm stuck at the mall, can you send me some money for a cab?'. You feel worried and almost send it, but you realize your friend is actually sitting right next to you! You check the profile and find it’s a fake one with the same name and photo. The hacker used your friend's face to try and trick you.

## The Shield Protocol
Be suspicious: if a friend starts acting weirdly online or asks for money/personal info, don't trust the message immediately. Call them or talk to them in person to confirm. Check the profile for small differences—like a misspelling or no mutual friends. Always double-check before giving away anything or doing what the message says.

## The Lifeline
If you or your friend got targeted by an impersonation trick, tell an adult right away: 'Someone is pretending to be my friend.' They can help report the fake account and warn others. It's not your fault; scammers are very clever. Sharing this helps everyone stay alert and stops the trickster from hurting others.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, search for your own name and your close friends' names on social media. If you find any duplicate profiles, report them immediately. Also, help your friends by telling them to put their profiles on 'Private' so strangers can't easily steal their photos.
