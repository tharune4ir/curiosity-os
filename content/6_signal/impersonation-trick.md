## The Invisible Code
Sometimes people pretend to be someone else online. It's like a stranger wearing a mask of someone you know. They might send messages as if they're your friend or teacher to gain your trust. They rely on the fact that you won't suspect a friendly face.

## The Digital Trap
You get a message from 'Mom' on a family group chat saying she needs money urgently and can't come to the phone. You panic and send money. Later, you find out it was a hacker with a similar phone number, and your real mom never sent that message. You feel upset and tricked.

## The Shield Protocol
Always double-check. If someone asks for money or personal things online, verify it in person or by calling them directly. If a message sounds odd (like mom asking for cash), contact that person another way before doing anything. Don't trust the name on the screen alone – think of it as wearing a mask that can hide identity.

## The Lifeline
If you send money or info to an imposter, tell an adult immediately: 'I think I just sent my info to a fake.' Adults will help you recover what you can (like blocking the number, changing passwords). Remember, it's not your fault for falling for it; these tricks can fool the best of us.

## The Empowerment Move
If you get any urgent-sounding message from someone you know (like family or teacher), call them or talk to them in person before doing anything. Keep a family group chat only with actual family members, and maybe a secret code word so everyone knows the message is really from them.
