## The Invisible Code
The internet provides an endless stream of information—news, posts, videos, and jokes. When there's too much for your brain to process, you feel overwhelmed and stressed. This is 'Information Overload.' Recognizing it is the first step to taking a break and keeping your mind healthy and clear.

## The Digital Trap
You spent three hours scrolling through a news feed and watching videos on five different topics. By the end, you feel tired, irritable, and your head feels 'full.' You can't remember most of what you saw, and you missed your chance to play outside. You were overwhelmed by the digital flood and didn't even notice.

## The Shield Protocol
Take regular breaks from the screen. Use a timer to remind yourself to step away every 30 minutes. Focus on one activity at a time—like reading a single article or watching one full video. If you feel stressed, close the app and do something offline. Remember, it's okay not to see everything! Your mental health is more important than any feed.

## The Lifeline
If you're feeling overwhelmed and stressed by the internet, talk to a parent or teacher: 'I feel like there's just too much on my screen.' they'll help you find ways to cut down and explain that even grown-ups feel this way. They're happy you're being honest and will help you find a healthier balance. Together you can find peace of mind!

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, close all your tabs and take a deep breath. Stand up and stretch for 2 minutes. Notice how your mind feels once the digital flood stops. Make it a habit to take a 'Quiet Mind' break every hour!
