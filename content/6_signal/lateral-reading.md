## The Invisible Code
Dynamic reading means instead of just scrolling down a single page, you open new tabs to see what others are saying about the same topic. It's like checking three different maps before you go on a hike. By 'reading laterally' (across different sites), you get a much better sense of what's true and what's not.

## The Digital Trap
You read an article that claims a certain food is dangerous. The page looks very professional, with lots of graphs and charts. You get scared and immediately stop eating it. But if you had opened another tab and searched for that claim, you would have found three other sites explaining that the study was completely wrong. You were trapped by one single source.

## The Shield Protocol
Whenever you find information that seems important or shocking, don't just stay on that page. Open a new tab and search for the main topic or claim. See what other respected news sites or organizations are saying. This helps you figure out if everyone agrees or if that one page is just making things up. It's like being a detective checking multiple witnesses!

## The Lifeline
If you've read something concerning and want to know more, ask a parent or teacher: 'I read this on one site, should I check others?' they'll show you how to search across the web and find reliable info. They love that you're being a careful reader and will help you distinguish between a single rumor and a confirmed fact.

## The Empowerment Move
The next time you read a big claim online, try opening three separate tabs from different news sites to see if they all say the same thing. If they don't, it’s a big sign you need to be careful. You've just practiced 'Lateral Reading'!
