## The Invisible Code
A lifeline is like having an emergency button on your phone. The moment you press it (by telling someone), rescue comes. It's a plan: who to call, and what to say, so you act fast and not panic.

## The Digital Trap
An unexpected event happens online (like a hack). You freeze and don’t know what to do next. Hours pass without action because you felt lost.

## The Shield Protocol
Prepare your lifeline ahead of time. Write down 'If this happens, I will...' actions for scenarios (e.g., tell Dad, then notify teacher). Knowing the steps makes it easier. It's like having a fire drill for online panic, so when something bad happens you already know how to escape.

## The Lifeline
When trouble hits and you use your plan, you will feel in control instead of scared. Calling someone you trust is hitting your lifeline button; it signals 'get help now'. Your trust circle will act fast, just like firefighters, to put out the 'internet fire.'

## The Empowerment Move
Write an 'If-Then' note for one scenario: e.g., 'If I see something scary, I will immediately call [Name]'. Keep it on your phone's home screen or sticker on your desk. Practice saying 'if this, then that' out loud a couple of times to lock it in memory.
