## The Invisible Code
Your phone and apps can share exactly where you are if you're not careful. It's like wearing a blinking neon sign with your home address on it. Strangers or dangerous people can see where you live or play, which can be very unsafe.

## The Digital Trap
You take a selfie with the Eiffel Tower filter and tag your location 'New Delhi.' A stranger sees it and now knows your city. Next, someone compliments your photo, but it feels creepy. They already knew you were nearby. You realize you should not have shared your actual location or geotag so openly.

## The Shield Protocol
Keep location on 'off' for apps that don't need it (many don't). For social media, never share your live location or tag your exact address. Instead of telling everyone where you are, just say the city if necessary. Think of it as keeping your house keys safe: you wouldn't leave them out for everyone to find.

## The Lifeline
If someone online asks where you live or is too curious about your routine, it's good to tell a trusted adult immediately. There's no shame—adults always talk about safety rules. They can explain how to adjust settings or talk to the person if needed. Better safe now than sorry later.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to your phone settings now: Turn off location sharing for any apps you don't need (like games or chat). On social media, remove location tags from old posts (or delete and repost without the tag). Make your profile not show your city at all if you can.
