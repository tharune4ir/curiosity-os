## The Invisible Code
Media literacy is the ability to analyze and evaluate the stories, images, and videos you see online. It’s about being a critical thinker and not just a passive consumer. It’s like having a special filter for your brain that helps you spot bias, truth, and fiction in the digital flood. Becoming media literate is a superpower in the modern age!

## The Digital Trap
You see a post that makes you very angry and you want to share it right away. You feel like you have to let everyone know! But then you pause and realize the post was designed to make you angry and that it might not be true. You caught yourself before you were trapped by your own emotions. You used your media literacy filter!

## The Shield Protocol
Every time you see a surprising or emotional story, ask: 'Who made this and why?' Check for facts, sources, and multiple perspectives. Trust but verify! The more you practice being a critical observer, the harder you are to trick. Your mind is your best defense against the digital flood of info. Stay smart and curious!

## The Lifeline
If you're not sure how to analyze a piece of media, ask a parent or teacher: 'Why do you think this story was made in this way?' they'll show you how to look for those hidden messages and explain how media works. They're proud that you're being a smart consumer and will help you build your literacy. Together we can see through the noise!

## The Empowerment Move
Find one news story today and try to find two different websites that have a slightly different view on it. Notice how each 'media' source tells the story in a different way. You've just practiced your 'Media Literacy'!
