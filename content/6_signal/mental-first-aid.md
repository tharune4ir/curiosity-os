## The Invisible Code
After something scary happens online, you need to take care of your feelings just like a scraped knee needs cleaning. This means doing things that calm you down (like deep breaths, talking, taking a break) and reminding yourself everything is fixable.

## The Digital Trap
A rumor spreads about you on social media and you feel panic and sadness. You start avoiding people and feel your stomach in knots.

## The Shield Protocol
Immediately do something soothing: take five deep belly breaths and step outside for 5 minutes. Remind yourself it's temporary. If feelings are too big, tell an adult you trust that you're upset and ask for a hug or to just sit together quietly. That helps your brain process the stress safely.

## The Lifeline
Once you share how you feel ('I feel hurt by this'), the adults or friends around you can help calm you (maybe with a warm drink, a walk, or just company). This is like bandaging the emotional cut. Remember: in a real emergency, people call out for help; do the same for your feelings.

## The Empowerment Move
Keep a small 'calm-down kit' (even mentally) – things like headphones with a favorite song, or a funny video bookmarked. Next time something bad happens, use one item: play that song or video immediately. It distracts your mind and reminds you things will feel okay again.
