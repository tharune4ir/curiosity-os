## The Invisible Code
Scammers love to lure you with 'free' gifts. They use bait like free games, gift cards, or money, but there’s always a catch. It's like someone offering a free candy that is actually poisoned. If it seems too good to be true, it probably is a trap.

## The Digital Trap
You get a message saying 'Congratulations! You won a free iPhone! Click here to claim.' You click and it asks for your address and some money for shipping. Excited, you pay. Then your account is charged more for random stuff and you get no phone. The 'free gift' was a scam to take your money.

## The Shield Protocol
Remember: real giveaways won't ask you for money or secret info. If you see a 'win a prize' message, double-check the official site (does it look like a scam?). Never share your family’s financial details to claim a gift. If someone seems overly eager to give away money, they might be a trickster.

## The Lifeline
If you fall for a free gift scam, don’t hide it. Tell a parent: 'I clicked on a giveaway, and they took money.' It's not shameful—scammers lie very convincingly. Parents will help you contact banks or authorities if needed. They’ve probably been tricked like this before too.

## The Empowerment Move
Block or delete any messages that say you've won something you didn't even enter. Check with parents before clicking 'free gift' links. Also, install an ad blocker on your browser to reduce these scam pop-ups.
