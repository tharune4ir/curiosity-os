## The Invisible Code
In some situations, anything that worries or scares you should be shared with someone you trust. There is a 'No Secrets' rule: just as doctors break confidentiality if you’re in danger, we always break secret when it’s about safety online. Keeping dangerous secrets is never a rule you should follow.

## The Digital Trap
An online scammer says, 'This is our secret, don’t tell anyone.' You think it’s just between you two. You hide it and worry, feeling lonely and trapped.

## The Shield Protocol
As soon as you hear 'Don't tell anyone,' activate your Rule A: 'Tell everyone trustworthy!'. Tell a parent or teacher immediately. Keeping that secret longer only lets scammers hurt more people. By telling, you make it everyone’s problem instead of just yours.

## The Lifeline
When you break the secret and tell someone, you will feel immense relief. The worry floating above you will disappear. Your adult will stop the scammer together. This rule keeps you safe and strong – and you should always remember it.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, make a hand gesture (like breaking a pencil in half) whenever you think 'I shouldn’t tell anyone.' This physical action will remind you that actually, the secret should be broken and shared.
