## The Invisible Code
Offline or online, if someone asks you to keep a secret that makes you feel weird, it’s a big red flag. Bad people use secrets to keep you from getting help. The 'No Secrets' rule means you should always tell a trusted adult if someone asks you to hide something online.

## The Digital Trap
A 'friend' you met in a game tells you a secret about another player and says, 'Don't tell anyone, this is just between us.' You feel a bit special, but then they start asking you to keep secrets from your parents too. You feel anxious and alone. You eventually realize they were trying to isolate you and make you depend only on them.

## The Shield Protocol
If anyone asks you to keep a secret from your parents or teachers, tell them 'No' and tell your adult immediately. Real friends don't make you hide things from people who love you. If a conversation feels 'hush-hush' or creepy, that’s your signal to speak up. Your parents are your partners in safety!

## The Lifeline
If you've been keeping a secret from your parents and you're scared, it's okay. You can tell them now: 'I’ve been keeping a secret online and I’m nervous.' They will be relieved you told them and will help you handle it. They won't be mad at you; they'll be proud of your bravery. Breaking a bad secret is a brave move.

## The Empowerment Move
Make a promise to your parent or teacher right now: 'If anyone online ever asks me to keep a secret, I will tell you immediately.' Knowing this rule is like having a direct line to help when things get weird.
