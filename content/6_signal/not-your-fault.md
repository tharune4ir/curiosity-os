## The Invisible Code
Everyone, even very smart adults, sometimes gets fooled online. It’s like a magician fooling even grownups; the trick can be that good. Just because something happened doesn’t mean you did something stupid. Scammers make their tricks very believable on purpose.

## The Digital Trap
You see news that your favorite phone brand will give a free phone to everyone who clicks a link. You click it, and it asks for personal details. When your parents find out, they look mad and say you should have been smarter. You feel worse, thinking, 'I am stupid, why did I do that?'

## The Shield Protocol
When you start blaming yourself, stop and remember: anyone could fall for this, given the same situation. Practice saying, 'That scam was made to fool me, not the other way around.' Let that be a shield against self-blame.

## The Lifeline
Talk to someone like, 'I feel really dumb right now.' They’ll probably share a time when they made a mistake too. This helps you see you’re not alone. Adults understand how these tricks work and will help you recover instead of making you feel bad.

## The Empowerment Move
Call or text a friend or sibling right now, and tell them a small mistake you made recently (even if it's nothing dangerous). You'll see they accept it and comfort you. This reminds you mistakes are normal. Do this every time you feel ashamed; sharing takes away the sting.
