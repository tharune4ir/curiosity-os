## The Invisible Code
Sometimes old stories or events get shared as if they are new. It's like hearing a playground rumor from last year but acting like it's happening today. News context matters; without it, old news can cause new panic.

## The Digital Trap
You see a news snippet about a flood in your city and rush to rescue, only to discover it's a video from a past year. People wasted time and you felt misled.

## The Shield Protocol
Always look for date or location clues in news. Try searching 'today' or '2026' along with the story title. If it's old, the current year will show no results or there will be articles saying 'This happened in 2024'. Always cross check the date before reacting.

## The Lifeline
Tell others who fell for it: 'Hey, this was last year, not now.' Being the one to correct it makes you look smart and trustworthy. It helps calm everyone because they see 'Ah, we got fooled by old news' and laugh it off.

## The Empowerment Move
Next time you see 'breaking' news on a forward, click 'view original article' or search the headline in quotes. You might find the original source with a date. If the date is old, delete it. Confirming dates becomes second nature!
