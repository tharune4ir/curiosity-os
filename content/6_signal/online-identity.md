## The Invisible Code
Your online identity is the image of yourself that you create across multiple apps, sites, and games. It’s built from your posts, photos, comments, and likes. Just like in real life, your digital identity should reflect who you truly are, while also being something you're proud of. Managing it is how you keep your digital reputation healthy and strong.

## The Digital Trap
You created a silly, mean-spirited profile when you were younger and forgot about it. Now, as you're applying for something important, you realize that old identity still exists. It doesn't represent who you are now, but it’s the first thing people see when they search for you. You realize your past online masks can sometimes stay on longer than you'd like.

## The Shield Protocol
Be mindful of what you share and how you behave online. Regularly review your profiles and delete anything that doesn't represent who you are today. Use privacy settings to separate your private life from your public image. Think of your online identity as a garden: you need to weed out the bad stuff so the good stuff can grow!

## The Lifeline
If you're worried about your online image or want to change it, talk to a parent or counselor: 'I want to clean up my online profiles, can you help?' they’ll show you how to delete old data and help you build a more positive digital presence. They love that you're being a smart and self-aware person. Together you can create an identity you're proud of!

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, go to your main social profile and delete one old or 'cringe' post that you don't think is necessary anymore. Then, write a short and positive 'About Me' section that truly represents who you are today. You've just taken a step to manage your online identity!
