## The Invisible Code
Websites and apps know angry posts make people click and comment more. So they show you things that make you furious or scared. It's like there's a hidden button on the screen that, when pressed by your anger, gives them a reward. If you keep hitting the outrage button, they keep handing you more fire to fuel your anger.

## The Digital Trap
You see a post saying something mean about a group you care about. Immediately, your fists clench. You share the post and write a long angry reply. For the next day, your feed is full of even more posts making you angry, because the app noticed how much you reacted. You spend hours ranting online, without realizing someone is manipulating your feelings.

## The Shield Protocol
Know that anger clicks are like trapdoors. Before you share or comment on anything that makes you super mad, pause. Take a deep breath and count to five. Ask yourself: 'Is this post really true? Does sharing it help?' If you feel really hurt or angry, step away and talk to a friend or teacher. Algorithms push the angriest stuff first to grab your attention, so fight back by stepping away.

## The Lifeline
It can feel embarrassing to admit that something on your phone made you lose your cool. But remember: these apps are built to make everyone angry, even grown-ups! Telling someone 'this post made me angry' can actually help you calm down. Your friends or parents might even tell you it happened to them too. You are not alone or weak for feeling outraged.

## The Empowerment Move
Next time you see something infuriating online, don’t click or comment yet. Instead, text it to your best friend and say 'is this true?'. Or do something else for 5 minutes to cool off before reacting. You can close the app for a break or speak to someone face-to-face.
