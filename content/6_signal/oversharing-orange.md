## The Invisible Code
Every detail you post is like dropping clues about yourself. Oversharing is like coloring with permanent marker; once it's out there, you can't erase it. People can use these clues (like where you go, who you're with, or what you wear) to figure things out about you.

## The Digital Trap
You post a photo holding up a map at an amusement park and say, 'My dream came true!' The next day, a stranger recognizes the park and posts, 'I bet I know where you went after!' with a photo of your house. Suddenly, you feel unsafe because you accidentally revealed the park location and others found your home from clues in the picture.

## The Shield Protocol
Think 'Is this really for everyone or just my close friends?' before posting. Remove geotags from photos and don’t share your location or daily routine. If you're unsure, ask, 'Would I tell this to a stranger in person?' If the answer is no, don't post it. Guard your info as you would guard a secret diary.

## The Lifeline
If you notice you've given too much away, it's okay—tell a parent. Maybe delete or change the post, or tighten your privacy. Parents and teachers are used to handling these mistakes; they're not angry, just relieved you're aware. It’s better to fix it now with their help.

## The Empowerment Move
Go through your recent posts and see if any have location or personal details (like full address in the text). Either delete or change them so they're private. Then set your phone's camera to never automatically add location tags to photos.
