## The Invisible Code
The internet learns from everything you click on and like. It's as if the computer reads your mind and only shows things it thinks you'll enjoy. It remembers your age, where you live, and what you've clicked in the past to choose posts just for you. You get a unique feed designed only for your eyes.

## The Digital Trap
You love cooking, so you watch a lot of cooking videos. Then everything on your app is about food. Great recipes everywhere! But when your mom asks for help with math homework, all your attention is stuck on cooking. The feed is so focused on what you like that you miss other important stuff in the real world.

## The Shield Protocol
Think of your feed as a menu written by a chef who only knows your favorite dish. To broaden it, manually look up new topics: a math fun video, a news story, or a dance video, even if you think you won't like them. This adds variety. Also turn off location and age sharing if an app lets you, so it can't narrow you down too much.

## The Lifeline
It can be surprising to realize your app is limiting you. Talk about it: 'My app only shows me one type of thing!' Parents or older friends might help you explore new content. Maybe ask them to show you something interesting they follow online. Together, you can expand your feed and make sure it shows different types of posts.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to your app settings and disable auto-play or 'for you' recommendations. Instead, search for one completely different topic right now (like dinosaurs or sports if you never do). This tells the algorithm: 'Hey, show me different things.' You'll train your feed to be less narrow.
