## The Invisible Code
'Phubbing' (phone snubbing) is when you ignore someone you're with in person by constantly checking your phone. It's like being in two places at once but not fully in either. It can hurt your relationships and make the people you care about feel unimportant. Recognizing phubbing is the first step to being more present and respectful.

## The Digital Trap
You're hanging out with a friend, but every 2 minutes you glance at your phone to check for new likes. Your friend stops talking and looks sad. You realize you were ignoring them and they felt like your phone was more important than their company. You were 'phubbing' without even noticing, and it hurt your friendship.

## The Shield Protocol
When you're with friends or family, put your phone in your pocket or face-down on the table. Make it a rule: no phones during meals or important conversations. Be fully present and listen to the people you're with. If you absolutely have to check something, say 'Excuse me, I just need to check one thing.' It's like being a good host: you want your guests to feel valued and heard!

## The Lifeline
If you've been 'phubbed' or feel like you're the one doing it, talk to a friend: 'I'm sorry I was on my phone, let's put them away.' they'll appreciate your honesty and will probably agree to do the same. They love that you're prioritizing your friendship. Together you can enjoy more meaningful time in the real world!

## The Empowerment Move
The next time you're with a friend, challenge yourself to not look at your phone for one full hour. Notice how much better the conversation feels when you're both fully present. You've just defeated 'Phubbing'!
