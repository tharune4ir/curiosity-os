## The Invisible Code
Propaganda is information (sometimes true, sometimes not) that's used to push a specific political or personal idea. It often uses very strong emotions like fear or pride to get you to agree. It's like a biased coach who only tells you how great his team is and never mentions their mistakes. It's meant to change how you think without you even noticing.

## The Digital Trap
You see a video that says 'Our group is the ONLY one who knows the truth, everyone else is lying to you!' It makes you feel like an insider and very proud. You stop listening to anyone else and only watch those videos. Soon, your entire view of the world is shaped by that one group. You’re in a propaganda pit and don't even know it.

## The Shield Protocol
Be aware when a post uses very strong 'us versus them' language or makes you feel super angry or proud. Ask yourself: 'Who is saying this and why do they want me to feel this way?' Seek out other views and information from trusted sources. Don't let one group define your entire reality. Stay curious and ask questions!

## The Lifeline
If you've felt stuck in one point of view and want to know more, talk to an adult: 'I heard this one group says everything else is a lie. What do you think?' they'll help you look at other perspectives and explain how propaganda works. They're proud that you're being a critical thinker. Together you can find the whole truth!

## The Empowerment Move
Find one topic where you feel very strongly, and then search for one trustworthy news article from a completely different perspective. You don't have to agree with it, but just seeing the other side is a big step. You're building a stronger, more flexible mind!
