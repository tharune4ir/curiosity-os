## The Invisible Code
Algorithms often have a 'shuffle' of ideas ready for you, like a roulette wheel of posts. They guess what you might like next and throw it at you, hoping you'll keep watching or reading. It's like being in a magic show where you never know what the magician will pull out next, and you're always surprised enough to stay.

## The Digital Trap
You finish watching a short video and the app automatically plays a new one. Sometimes it's about homework help, sometimes a weird dance. It feels random and exciting, and you think 'Just one more.' But one more turns into 10. By the time you look at the clock, you've spent two hours watching a crazy mix of videos and missed your class online.

## The Shield Protocol
Remember, every 'Next up' video or suggested post is chosen to keep you watching. Don't let the roulette spin you out of time. Turn off autoplay settings if you can, so you choose each video. Or every time you see 'Recommended for you', ask yourself 'Is this what I really want to see?' If not, scroll past or close the app.

## The Lifeline
Feeling like time flew by can make you anxious, but talk to someone about it. A parent or teacher can help you set boundaries so it doesn't happen again. Say something like, 'I meant to watch just one video, but the app kept playing more.' They'll understand, and next time they can remind you to take breaks.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to the settings of any video or social app you use and turn off 'autoplay' or 'up next' features. If you can't find it, simply put the phone down at the end of each video. You could also keep a notepad by you and write down ideas for activities to do instead of watching another random video.
