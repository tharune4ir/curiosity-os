## The Invisible Code
A rescue network is your team of helpers. It's like having superheroes on speed dial – family, friends, teachers, or even helplines. When an online problem happens, they 'fly in' to help. You don't have to be alone; heroes usually work together, not solo.

## The Digital Trap
You feel all alone dealing with a nasty chat or trick. You convince yourself 'no one can help me, this is my problem'. You stay quiet, and the issue keeps getting worse, just like a fire grows without firefighters.

## The Shield Protocol
List out your personal rescue heroes now. Include at least one parent, one teacher or coach, and maybe a friend. If something happens online, pick up the phone or go to them straight away and say 'Help! Something's up with my phone.' Just having their contact makes you brave to reach out.

## The Lifeline
When trouble hits and you use your plan, you will feel in control instead of scared. Calling someone you trust is hitting your lifeline button; it signals 'get help now'. Your trust circle will act fast, just like firefighters, to put out the 'internet fire.'

## The Empowerment Move
Save the numbers of your rescue heroes in your phone contacts or write them down where you can reach. The next time something unusual happens, scroll to 'Contacts' and pick one, even if it feels small. Practice by occasionally checking in with one of them about a daily thing – it gets easier to talk.
