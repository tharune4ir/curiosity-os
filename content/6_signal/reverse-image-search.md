## The Invisible Code
Sometimes, photos on the internet are taken out of context or even completely faked. 'Reverse image search' is a way to find where a photo originally came from. It's like having a special detective tool that tells you a photo’s history—whether it was from 10 years ago or from a different city entirely.

## The Digital Trap
You see a dramatic photo of a 'rare blue tiger' found in your local forest. It looks amazing and you share it with everyone! But later it turns out it was just a photo of a normal tiger that someone had colored blue in an app. You feel silly and realize you helped spread a fake picture.

## The Shield Protocol
If you see a photo that looks too wild or important to be real, use a tool like Google Image Search or TinEye to 'reverse search' it. If the photo comes from a different event or year, you've found the lie. Also, look for signs of editing: weird shadows or blurry edges. Being a smart image detective is a great skill!

## The Lifeline
If you're unsure if a photo is real, ask an adult: 'Can you help me check if this photo is truly what it says?' they'll show you how to use reverse search and help you figure out the photo's history. They love that you're being a careful observer and will help you spot fakes. Together you can see the truth!

## The Empowerment Move
The next time you see a 'viral' or unbelievable photo, try a reverse image search on your phone right now. You might discover that the 'current' photo is actually from an old movie or a different country. You've just mastered a powerful detective tool!
