## The Invisible Code
Rumors are like wild seeds; once planted (shared) they grow quickly and become hard to stop. Instead of spreading unverified messages, think of them as fire: you don't pass it on before making sure it's safe. Share responsibly.

## The Digital Trap
You forward a rumor about a celebrity scandal without checking. Soon, it's everywhere and even media outlets pick it up. Later it's debunked and people see you as the originator. Your reputation and trust suffer.

## The Shield Protocol
Stop and think: 'Am I absolutely sure this is true before I forward it?' If not, don’t pass it on. Practice waiting 2 minutes before sharing anything that sounds juicy or dramatic. If in doubt, don't share—demonstrate the saying 'Don't be part of the gossip machine'.

## The Lifeline
If you accidentally spread a rumor, tell friends 'Guys, I just found out that wasn’t true, sorry.' They will respect you more for correcting it than if you had left it hanging. It turns you into a responsible member of the group, not a culprit.

## The Empowerment Move
Memorize a quick two-line phrase like 'Think, check, then share.' Repeat it whenever you want to forward a message. It will freeze you for a moment to verify the info first.
