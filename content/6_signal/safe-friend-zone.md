## The Invisible Code
Accepting random friend requests or messages is like letting strangers into your home. If you only let in people you know and trust, it's much safer. Bad people often pretend to be friendly, so only add people you've met or who have friends in common with you.

## The Digital Trap
You get a friend request from someone named 'Aisha Sharma' who has no mutual friends. She sends a message asking for your help with an account problem. You decide to help, sharing some info, and later realize she was a fake hacker. She used your account to scam others.

## The Shield Protocol
Before accepting a friend or follower, check if you truly know them. Look at their friends list and photos; if it seems empty or fake, ignore it. If someone insists on being your friend, try contacting them through another way (like asking a mutual friend or seeing them at school) to verify. Always remember: real friends don't pressure you online.

## The Lifeline
If a stranger somehow contacts you or you accept by mistake, tell an adult: 'I think I added someone I don't know in real life.' They can help remove them and keep an eye out. You're not in trouble for making a mistake; even adults occasionally accept wrong friend requests. The goal is to learn and be careful next time.

## The Empowerment Move
Go to your social media and remove any friends you don't actually know in real life. Check your friends list right now for anyone you wouldn't invite to a birthday party. Then, before accepting a new request, pause and think: 'Would I recognize this person in school or on the street?'
