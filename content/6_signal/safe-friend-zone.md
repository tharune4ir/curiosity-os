## The Invisible Code
The digital world can feel huge, so you should keep your 'friend circle' tight. Imagine your online space like your home: you only invite in people you actually know and trust. By only adding real friends, you keep out strangers who might mean harm.

## The Digital Trap
You get a friend request from someone with a cool avatar and you think, 'Hey, more friends are better!' You accept. Soon after, they start sending you weird links or asking personal questions. You realize you have no idea who this person is in real life. You feel uncomfortable having a stranger in your digital home.

## The Shield Protocol
Have a simple rule: only accept requests from people you have met in person. If you're not sure who someone is, ask a mutual friend or just ignore the request. It’s okay to have a small friend list; it’s safer that way. Also, regularly check your friend list and remove anyone you don't talk to or recognize anymore.

## The Lifeline
If you accidentally added someone who turns out to be weird, don't feel bad. Tell a parent: 'I added someone I shouldn't have, can you help me block them?' They'll show you how to manage your list and keep things safe. You can always un-friend or block someone – you're in control of your online circle.

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, go through your social media or game friend list and remove any name you don't recognize or haven't talked to in a month. Then, set your account so only friends-of-friends can send you requests.
