## The Invisible Code
Some apps look fun but are actually traps. They might have viruses or tricks that steal your info. It's like a candy that looks delicious but is filled with dirt. One click and all your secrets can be out.

## The Digital Trap
You see a game called 'Super Ninja Adventure' on Google Play. It has flashy pictures and easy levels at first. After playing a day, it asks you to 'login to continue'. You enter your email and password and suddenly ads pop up everywhere, and your phone freezes often. The next day, you can't open your photos. The app had a virus that infected your phone.

## The Shield Protocol
Only download apps from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store) and check reviews and how many downloads it has. If it's a strange store or the app has only a few reviews, avoid it. Also see if it requests silly permissions (like a game wanting access to your contacts or messages). If it's weird, don't install it.

## The Lifeline
If you accidentally installed a bad app, don't hide it. Tell an adult right away: 'My phone is acting weird because I got a new app.' They can help remove it or reset your device. You’re not in trouble; everyone accidentally downloads a dud app sometimes. Working together, you’ll fix it.

## The Empowerment Move
Go through your phone and delete any apps you don't recognize or don't use. Always read app permissions when you install new ones (pause and think: 'Why does this game need access to my location?'). Enable Google Play Protect or similar security feature if available.
