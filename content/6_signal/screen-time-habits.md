## The Invisible Code
Screen time habits are the routines you've built around how you use your devices—when you check your phone, how long you play games, and what you do before bed. Good habits help you stay in control and keep your life balanced, while bad habits (like checking your phone first thing in the morning) can make you feel stressed and distracted.

## The Digital Trap
Without thinking, you reach for your phone the second you wake up. Suddenly, 30 minutes have passed and you're already late for school. You started your day by letting other people's posts and notifications control your mood. You've built a bad early-morning habit and don't even realize how it’s hurting your focus.

## The Shield Protocol
Be intentional about your habits. Set a rule: no screens for the first and last hour of the day. Use an alarm clock instead of your phone as an alarm. Plan your screen time instead of just letting it 'happen.' Remember, your habits are like paths you've walked many times—you can choose to build better paths that lead to a happier and more focused life!

## The Lifeline
If you want to build better screen time habits, talk to a parent: 'I want to change how I use my phone in the morning/evening.' they'll help you set up new routines and will probably join you in build better family habits. They love that you're being a smart and self-aware user. Together we can build routines that make us feel great!

## The Empowerment Move
Choose one 'New Habit' for today—like putting your phone away during dinner—and stick to it. Notice how much more you enjoy your meal and the conversation. You've just started to build a better 'Screen Time Habit'!
