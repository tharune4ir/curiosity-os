## The Invisible Code
Keeping a dangerous secret is like swallowing poison: it harms you from the inside. The longer you stay quiet, the more it hurts. The real poison is the secret, not the mistake. Only by sharing the secret do you start to feel better.

## The Digital Trap
You sent a private message to someone and it ended up in a wrong chat. You keep it a secret because you feel guilty. Every day, you become more nervous and can't concentrate in class, as if something is eating you from the inside.

## The Shield Protocol
Think of secrets as tiny leaks that need letting out. Make a plan to tell someone you trust as soon as possible. Remind yourself: hiding this is making it worse. Replace the thought 'If I tell, I'll get in trouble' with 'If I don't tell, things will only get harder.'

## The Lifeline
When you spill the secret, you'll feel an immediate relief, as if poison is leaving your body. Your adult will handle the problem with you. You will be surprised at how understanding they are. The 'poison' loses its power when shared.

## The Empowerment Move
Write in your journal or a note: 'Secrets are poison.' Then practice saying it out loud. Whenever you feel stuck, pretend you're a superhero telling your sidekick the plan—out loud, in private or on your phone—so it feels natural to speak up later.
