## The Invisible Code
Public Wi-Fi (like at a cafe) is like a park bench where anyone can sit and see what you’re typing. Hackers can snoop on open networks easily. It’s as if all your data packets are postcards that everyone can read unless you encrypt them.

## The Digital Trap
You connect your phone to 'free_wifi123' in a coffee shop to save data. You log in to your email without thinking. Later, someone hacks that open network and steals your password. They can see and capture your information because it was unprotected.

## The Shield Protocol
Only use secure (password-protected) Wi-Fi networks you trust (like home). If you must use public Wi-Fi, don’t log into important accounts (bank, email) without using a VPN or mobile data. Better: turn off auto-join Wi-Fi in your phone settings so it doesn't connect to unknown networks automatically.

## The Lifeline
If something weird happens after using public Wi-Fi (like messages asking to change passwords), tell an adult immediately. They can help secure your accounts. You won't get in trouble; even adults sometimes need reminders to use safe networks. It's better to be safe than sorry.

## The Empowerment Move
Turn off auto-join for public Wi-Fi in your phone settings now. And whenever you’re out, use your phone's mobile data for important stuff, or a VPN app. Also, delete any network you accidentally saved that's public.
