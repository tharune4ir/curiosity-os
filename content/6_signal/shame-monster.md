## The Invisible Code
Shame is like a monster hiding in your heart, making small mistakes feel like giant beasts. Scammers feed that monster to keep you quiet. But once you light a candle (talk about it), the monster runs away and you see clearly again.

## The Digital Trap
After a video game, you accidentally click on an ad that was malicious. You feel sick with embarrassment every time you see that ad again. The Shame Monster grows and whispers 'You're so dumb' whenever you try to talk about it.

## The Shield Protocol
Name the monster: remind yourself, 'Oh, that's just the Shame Monster talking, not me.' Take a few deep breaths and say out loud 'I didn't do anything wrong, I was tricked.' It can’t grow if you refuse to feed it with silence or guilt.

## The Lifeline
Tell someone: 'I'm ashamed I clicked something by mistake.' You’ll find that as you talk, the monster gets smaller. Loved ones will comfort you and make it clear you’re not stupid. This helps the monster vanish. Everyone has these moments, and sharing them helps you get rid of the shame.

## The Empowerment Move
Whenever you feel that shame creeping up, do something physical to kick it away: stand up, stretch, or even do a silly dance. Silly movements can make you feel less serious and more in control. It's hard for the Shame Monster to stand up to some sunshine!
