## The Invisible Code
Holding in stress makes it explode inside. But talking about problems is like releasing steam from a pressure cooker – you immediately feel better. Sharing breaks the cycle of fear and gives the scammer no power over your thoughts.

## The Digital Trap
A rumor spreads about you on social media. You stay awake at night, upset and scared to talk about it. You grow anxious and distracted by school.

## The Shield Protocol
Whenever you start feeling overwhelmed by something online, remember: one minute of talking can undo hours of worry. Call or meet someone and say exactly what's bothering you. It’s that simple. Expressing feelings is the opposite of keeping a secret, so it stops the mental storm.

## The Lifeline
After you tell someone, you'll notice how your breathing eases and your mind is clearer. They might offer advice or just listen, but either way, you will feel supported and less alone. By sharing, you've already won half the fight against the problem.

## The Empowerment Move
This afternoon, after school, set a 'check-in' time with a family member or friend. For example, during dinner, say one thing that upset you today (online or offline). Getting into the habit of sharing daily things makes it easier to share big problems when they come.
