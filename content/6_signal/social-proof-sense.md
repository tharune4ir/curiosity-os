## The Invisible Code
Just because a post or opinion is liked by many, doesn’t make it true. It's like everybody in class saying the sky is green; they're wrong just because they're many. Popularity is not proof.

## The Digital Trap
You see a viral tweet with thousands of retweets saying a new rule at school is changed. You believe it because 'everyone is saying it,' and follow the rumor. Later, you find out it's entirely false, started by one student.

## The Shield Protocol
Realize that going viral can happen to false info too. Instead of believing something just because it's shared a lot, check for credible sources. If a crazy post is viral, see if official sites or known newspapers report it. Trusted facts don't need popularity—they stand on truth.

## The Lifeline
If you believed something just because it was popular and it turned out wrong, talk it out: 'Everyone believed X, but it was false.' This will help you process it. People will likely share how easy it was to fall for a viral rumor too, making you feel better.

## The Empowerment Move
When you see a post with huge share count or many likes, pause and ask: 'Does a lot of people liking this make it true?' Then double-check the info independently. This mental check helps break the viral spell.
