## The Invisible Code
Surveillance capitalism is the idea that companies make huge profits by watching (surveilling) your every move online and using that data to predict and influence your behavior. Every click, search, and like is a piece of data they use to sell things to you. It's like a massive laboratory where you're the subject and your attention is the product.

## The Digital Trap
You're watching a video on a social media app and then suddenly you see an ad for a product you just talked about with a friend. You feel like the app is listening or reading your mind. It's not magic; it’s just the sophisticated system of surveillance capitalism at work. Your personal life has been turned into a series of data points for profit.

## The Shield Protocol
Be aware of how much info you're sharing. Use privacy tools, block trackers, and limit your time on sites that profit from your attention. Support organizations that fight for digital privacy. Think of yourself as a person, not just a data point! Staying smart and aware is your best defense against being a product.

## The Lifeline
If you're concerned about how companies use your data to influence you, talk to a parent: 'I feel like my apps are watching me to sell things.' they'll help you understand the business side of the web and explain how to protect yourself. They love that you're being a critical thinker and will support you. Together you can be more than just a consumer!

## The Empowerment Move
Find one app that you use a lot and check its privacy settings right now. See if you can turn off 'data collection' or 'ad targeting.' Then, spend one hour offline and notice how much more in control you feel. You're building your awareness against surveillance capitalism!
