## The Invisible Code
Techno-stress is the feeling of anxiety or pressure caused by the constant use of technology—too many notifications, the fear of missing out (FOMO), or the pressure to reply to everyone immediately. It's like being on a treadmill that keeps going faster and faster. Recognizing techno-stress is how you know it's time to slow down and take a break.

## The Digital Trap
You have 50 unread messages in your group chat and everyone is expecting a reply. You feel a tightness in your chest and a headache starting. You feel like you can't keep up and that everyone is mad at you. You're suffering from techno-stress and didn't even notice. You were overwhelmed by the pressure of being 'always on.'

## The Shield Protocol
Turn off non-essential notifications. Set specific times to check your messages and social media. Let your friends know that you won't always reply immediately. Take 'digital breaks' where you put your phone away for an hour. Remember, it's okay not to be 'on' all the time! Your mental health is more important than any message or post.

## The Lifeline
If you're feeling stressed by your phone or computer, talk to a parent or teacher: 'I'm feeling very anxious because of all the messages on my phone.' they'll help you set boundaries and explain that even grown-ups feel this way. They love that you're being honest and will support you in finding a more peaceful balance. Together we can reduce the stress of the machine!

## The Empowerment Move
Right now, go to your phone settings and turn off notifications for one app that makes you feel anxious. Notice how much calmer you feel when that little red circle is gone. You've just started to manage your 'Techno-Stress'!
