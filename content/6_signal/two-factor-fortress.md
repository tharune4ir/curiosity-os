## The Invisible Code
Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second lock on your accounts. Even if someone cracks your password (first key), they still need a second key (like a code texted to you). It's like having a password to your house and an extra gate code. Both are needed to get in.

## The Digital Trap
Someone phished your email password. Without 2FA, they easily log in. But if you had 2FA on, they would need the code sent to your phone as well, which they wouldn't have. If you don't use 2FA and your account gets hacked, you lose all your messages and photos.

## The Shield Protocol
Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere you can (email, social media, school accounts). It usually sends a code to your phone or email you must enter after your password. Think of it like carrying a secret code card: without it, no one can unlock your account even with the password.

## The Lifeline
If your account is hacked despite 2FA, or if you lose your phone (which could carry the code), tell an adult right away. They will help you recover the account with backup codes or customer support. Again, it's not your fault if something happens; you've already done a lot by using 2FA.

## The Empowerment Move
Enable 2FA on your most important accounts right now. On WhatsApp and Gmail, go to Settings > Security and turn on '2-Step Verification'. Write down backup codes and store them where only you (and maybe a trusted parent) can find them in case your phone is lost.
