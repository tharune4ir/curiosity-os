## The Invisible Code
Sometimes random people will pop into group chats or comment sections pretending to be funny or friendly, but they could be up to no good. It's like being invited into a stranger's house party—you never know who is really behind the mask. Always question anyone you meet only online.

## The Digital Trap
You’re in a school study group on WhatsApp. A new member called 'Uncle Dharmik' joins and says hi, bragging about some lottery win and asking if anyone wants money. At first, it's weird, but some classmates humor him. He then asks for your bank account to send 'inheritance'. You realize that something doesn't add up.

## The Shield Protocol
If someone you don’t know well tries to join your chat or asks personal stuff, mute or remove them. Only allow people who have real connections (like classmates you know). Ask your group: 'Does anyone really know Uncle Dharmik?' If not, block that contact immediately. Safety comes before politeness online.

## The Lifeline
If someone strange messages you asking for info (like money), it's okay to laugh it off but always tell an adult. Say, 'Someone I don’t know asked me for my bank details.' They might guide you on reporting the number or even informing police in serious cases. You won’t be in trouble—they'll keep you safe.

## The Empowerment Move
Immediately go through your group chats and block anyone you don’t know. Then, tell your friends or group members that you've done it and why. Keep an eye on any group when you notice a new person – if something seems off, remove them and inform a teacher or parent.
