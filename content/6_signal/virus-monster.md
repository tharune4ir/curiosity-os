## The Invisible Code
A computer virus is like a hidden monster in a game that suddenly hurts your character. If you open the wrong file or link, a virus can quietly enter your device and cause trouble: it can steal your data, slow your phone, or even ruin files. You don’t see it at first, but it can multiply and create chaos.

## The Digital Trap
You download a cool new wallpaper pack from a random site. When you open it, it looks fine. But shortly after, your phone starts showing lots of ads, and your battery drains fast even when you’re not using it. The next day, you can't open your photos. The app had a virus that infected your phone.

## The Shield Protocol
Keep a real antivirus or security app on your phone/computer (even just basic ones in Play Store or Apple Store). Also, only download files or apps from trusted sources (official stores, not pop-up websites). If a site or download looks suspicious (like too good to be free), avoid it. Also update your system regularly, as updates often patch security holes.

## The Lifeline
If your device acts strange after a download (like showing pop-ups or being slow), tell an adult immediately: 'My phone/computer has a virus.' They can run an antivirus scan or reset it. You won't be blamed – even tech-savvy people get viruses by mistake. It's better to fix it fast together.

## The Empowerment Move
Enable automatic updates on your phone/computer so it gets security fixes. If possible, install an antivirus app like Norton or AVG and run a quick scan right now. Before downloading anything next time, ask yourself if this source looks trusted.
