## The Invisible Code
AI can imitate anyone's voice now. It's like a parrot that learned to sound exactly like your friend. Scammers can use recorded clips to make you think someone you trust is talking on the phone or in a video, asking for things.

## The Digital Trap
You get a call from someone who sounds exactly like your aunt. They urgently ask for money for a 'trip'. You send money right away. Later you find out your real aunt was safe at home; it was a voice deepfake. You feel ashamed.

## The Shield Protocol
If someone calls or sends voice messages asking for money or private info, use another way to check: call them back on their saved number or ask a family member. Do not trust voice alone. Remember: voices can be cloned, like a ghost imitation.

## The Lifeline
Tell your parents: 'Someone with mom’s voice called and I got tricked.' They will believe you and help. Voices are easily copied nowadays; it's not your fault. They can block numbers and alert others. It's better to share that odd call immediately.

## The Empowerment Move
Next time you get a surprising call or voice message, say a secret code like your pet’s name or a family joke to the caller and see if they know it. If not, hang up. This checks if it's really them.
