## The Invisible Code
WhatsApp is full of forwards and forwards of messages. Some are jokes, some are scary rumors. It’s like the game of telephone at school, where a story changes as it goes around. By the time it reaches you, it might be totally false but people might believe it because it came through family or friends.

## The Digital Trap
You get a message saying 'Add 10 friends to this group or your phone will crash!' and it looks urgent. Everyone in your family keeps forwarding it. You panic and add random numbers. It was just a silly chain message; nothing real would have happened.

## The Shield Protocol
Don't forward chain messages without checking. A quick trick: googling a sentence from the message often shows if it's fake. For warnings like that, ask a parent or older sibling if it's true before doing it. If a message sounds like a game or threat, it usually is a prank or hoax.

## The Lifeline
Show your family message to a parent and say, 'Is this true?' They'll either laugh and tell you it’s false, or do a quick search. This way you’ll stop worrying. Family might be sending it out of habit, not meaning harm. Together, you can decide not to forward it further.

## The Empowerment Move
Open your chat and find one forwarded message you doubted. Search its main claim on Google. If you find fact-check articles or no evidence, delete it. Practice questioning at least one forwarded message you see every day.
