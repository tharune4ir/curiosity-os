## The Wealth Secret
Cheap things break fast and cost more to replace. Poor people are forced to buy cheap shoes every year, while the rich buy one pair for life.

## The Middle Class Trap
Buying the 'cheapest' possible option for a tool they use every day (like a chair or a bag), only to have it break in six months.

## The Leverage Play
'Buying it once.' Saving up to buy the highest quality version of a tool that will last you ten years, saving money in the long run.

## The Superpower
Quality leverage.

## The Actionable Cent
Invest in one high-quality item that you use every single day.
