## The Invisible Architecture
Accessibility (a11y) means designing digital products that work for people with disabilities — visual, hearing, motor, and cognitive. Screen readers read web pages aloud for blind users. Keyboard navigation lets people who cannot use a mouse browse the web. Color contrast requirements ensure text is readable by people with color blindness. Captions make videos accessible to deaf users. In India, over 2.6 crore people have visual impairments alone. Designing without accessibility excludes them completely.

## The Consumer Trap
Most developers add accessibility as an afterthought — or never. They build beautiful visuals for sighted, able-bodied users and accidentally lock out millions. Worse, in many countries accessibility is now a legal requirement — companies have faced lawsuits for inaccessible websites. Ignoring it is both ethically wrong and legally risky.

## The Builder Hack
A builder adds simple accessibility features that take 30 minutes but help thousands: alt text on every image (read by screen readers), proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3), keyboard-focusable buttons, and high enough color contrast. Free tools like the axe browser extension catch 70% of accessibility issues automatically.

## The Superpower
Accessible design is almost always better design for everyone. Good contrast helps people in bright sunlight. Keyboard navigation helps power users. Captions help people in noisy environments. Building for edge cases improves the experience for all cases.

## The First Line Of Code
Install the 'axe DevTools' browser extension (free). Go to any website — including your school's or government portals. Click the axe icon. Run analysis. Watch how many accessibility violations appear. Most will have dozens.
