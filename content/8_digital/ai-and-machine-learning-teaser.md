## The Invisible Architecture
Traditional programming tells a computer exactly what to do: IF spam email, then move to spam folder. Machine learning flips this: you show the computer 1 million examples of spam and non-spam emails, and it figures out the rules itself. The computer builds a 'model' — a mathematical function that predicts whether any new email is spam. The more examples you show it, the better the model gets. ChatGPT, Spotify recommendations, BGMI aimbots, Swiggy's 'you might like' suggestions — all machine learning.

## The Consumer Trap
When people interact with ChatGPT, they feel like they're talking to something that understands. They aren't. ChatGPT is an extremely sophisticated pattern-matching machine that was trained on trillions of words of human text and learned to predict the next most likely word, over and over. Understanding this removes both the mysticism and the fear.

## The Builder Hack
A builder trains their first ML model on Google Colab — using a free GPU — in one afternoon. Using scikit-learn (Python), they build a model that predicts house prices from neighborhood data, or classifies images of cats vs dogs. The model is 30 lines of code. The understanding of what it's doing is priceless.

## The Superpower
AI is the most powerful tool ever built, and it is accessible to any teenager with a laptop and internet. Those who understand how to use, evaluate, and build AI systems will have an enormous advantage in every field — medicine, business, science, art — for the next 50 years.

## The First Line Of Code
Go to 'teachablemachine.withgoogle.com'. In 5 minutes, train an AI model that recognizes your hand gestures using your webcam — no code required. That is a real neural network you built and trained.
