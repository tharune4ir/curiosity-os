## The Invisible Architecture
When Hotstar streams IPL to 50 million Indians simultaneously, there's no single server serving all 50 million. That would melt. Instead, Hotstar uses a CDN — Content Delivery Network — which is a fleet of servers placed in every major city worldwide. Chennai users get the video from the Chennai CDN server. Mumbai users get it from the Mumbai one. The content is 'cached' (pre-copied) on nearby servers. Your video travels 20km instead of 2000km.

## The Consumer Trap
People in India complain 'why is Netflix so fast but this small Indian website so slow?' The answer is usually: Netflix uses a global CDN and the small website serves everything from one server in Bangalore. The gap is infrastructure, not 'better code'.

## The Builder Hack
A builder launching their first website uses Cloudflare's free CDN tier. In 5 minutes, their website gets copied to 300+ global locations. Their site suddenly loads fast for users in the US, Europe, and Southeast Asia — for 0.

## The Superpower
You understand why big companies spend millions on CDN infrastructure and how you can get the same benefit for free as a small builder. This is how startups punch above their weight.

## The First Line Of Code
Go to 'cdnplanet.com/tools/cdnfinder'. Type in 'netflix.com'. See which CDN companies Netflix uses to deliver content to you. The answer will surprise you.
