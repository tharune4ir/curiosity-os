## The Invisible Architecture
A website needs to run on a computer that is always ON, always connected to the internet, and accessible from anywhere on Earth. Nobody can leave their personal computer on 24/7. So developers rent a 'virtual machine' from companies like AWS, Google Cloud, or Azure. These companies own enormous data centers with thousands of servers. For 500/month, you can rent a small fraction of one of these servers — it runs your code, serves your website, and is reachable from any device on Earth, all the time.

## The Consumer Trap
When startups say they're 'on AWS', people think it's a special club. It's just renting computers. Amazon built the world's most reliable and flexible computer rental service, and now every startup from 2-person teams to Netflix uses it. You can sign up in 10 minutes.

## The Builder Hack
A builder deploys their first website for free using Vercel or Railway.app. They push their code to GitHub, connect their GitHub to Vercel, and their website is live on the internet — automatically — every time they push new code. The entire deployment pipeline in 15 minutes.

## The Superpower
You can put anything you build in front of the entire world. An app on your laptop is a personal project. An app on the cloud is a product. The difference between a builder and a creator is the deploy button.

## The First Line Of Code
Go to vercel.com. Sign up with GitHub. Click 'New Project'. Import any GitHub repository (even an empty one with a single HTML file). Click Deploy. Your project is live on the internet with a public URL.
