## The Invisible Architecture
Imagine a Zomato restaurant with one impossibly fast chef. Every single thing your computer does — playing BGMI, loading Instagram, playing a YouTube video — is just an instruction written on a small 'order ticket'. The CPU is that chef. It reads tickets one by one and executes them billions of times per second. Its speed is measured in GHz — 3 GHz means 3 billion instructions every single second.

## The Consumer Trap
When a normal person's computer slows down, they just restart it and hope for the best. They have no idea why it was slow — was it too many 'orders'? A bad 'chef'? They will never know.

## The Builder Hack
A builder looks at their Task Manager (Windows) or Activity Monitor (Mac) to see which program is sending the most 'orders' to the CPU. They identify the greedy app hogging the chef's attention and close it. Problem solved in 10 seconds.

## The Superpower
You can diagnose your own computer like a doctor. You stop blaming 'the computer' and start identifying the exact culprit. This is the mindset of an engineer.

## The First Line Of Code
Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc right now on Windows. Click 'CPU'. Watch which app is eating the most of your chef's time. That's your CPU being a chef in real life.
