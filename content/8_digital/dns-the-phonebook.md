## The Invisible Architecture
You type 'google.com' into your browser. But the internet has no idea what 'google.com' is — it only understands IP addresses. So your device secretly asks a DNS (Domain Name System) server: 'What is the IP address of google.com?' The DNS server replies: '142.250.195.46'. Your browser then goes to that address. DNS is a massive phonebook — it translates human-readable names into machine-readable numbers, distributed across thousands of servers worldwide.

## The Consumer Trap
When a website is 'down', half the time it's not the website — it's a DNS failure. People restart their routers, call customer service, and wait for hours. They have no idea they could fix it in 10 seconds by changing their DNS server.

## The Builder Hack
A builder registering their first domain (like 'myapp.in' on GoDaddy) learns they need to set DNS records — specifically an 'A record' that points their domain name to the IP address of their server. Without this, no one can find their website. They control the phonebook entry for their name.

## The Superpower
You can fix internet problems others cannot. You can register domains, point them to servers, set up custom email addresses, and understand how websites get 'taken down' (their DNS records are deleted).

## The First Line Of Code
Go to Settings > WiFi > Advanced on your Android. Change your DNS to 8.8.8.8 (Google's DNS) or 1.1.1.1 (Cloudflare's DNS). Your internet may get faster and safer instantly.
