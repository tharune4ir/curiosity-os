## The Invisible Architecture
When you send a WhatsApp message from Mumbai to your cousin in Delhi, the data doesn't travel through the air like a radio signal. It travels as pulses of LIGHT through a glass cable thinner than a human hair. These cables run under roads, across the ocean floor, and through mountains. Light pulses represent 1s and 0s — light ON means 1, light OFF means 0. One fiber optic cable can carry terabits of data per second.

## The Consumer Trap
Everyone uses Jio Fiber and sees 'fiber optic broadband' on the box, but they think it's just marketing language for 'fast internet'. They don't know they are literally receiving data encoded in light pulses arriving at their home.

## The Builder Hack
A builder choosing a server location for their app knows to pick a data center close to their users. Even light — the fastest thing in the universe — takes measurable time to cross oceans. A Mumbai user connecting to a US server adds 150ms of delay just for the light to travel there and back.

## The Superpower
You understand network latency at a physical level. You can make architectural decisions about server location, CDNs, and data centers with real understanding — not guesswork.

## The First Line Of Code
Open Command Prompt and type 'ping google.com'. The number in milliseconds (ms) is literally the time it took light to travel from your computer to Google's server and back.
