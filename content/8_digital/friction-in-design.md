## The Invisible Architecture
Friction is anything that slows a user down or makes a task harder than it needs to be. Every extra click, every unnecessary form field, every confusing label, every slow loading screen — all friction. E-commerce companies have shown that removing friction from checkout can increase revenue by 30%. Amazon's '1-click purchase' patent was worth billions purely because it removed the friction of a checkout form. Friction is the silent killer of conversions, registrations, and user happiness.

## The Consumer Trap
Every time an app asks you to 'Create an account to continue', it's adding friction. Every IRCTC CAPTCHA is friction. Companies add friction for business reasons (they want your email) even when it hurts user experience. The tension between business goals and user experience is a constant battle in product design.

## The Builder Hack
A builder adds 'Continue as Guest' to their checkout flow after learning that 35% of users abandon carts when forced to create an account. They reduce required form fields from 12 to 5 (eliminating phone number, birth date, and other fields the business wanted but users resented). Conversion rate improves immediately.

## The Superpower
You see friction everywhere — in apps, government portals, physical queues, and real-world processes. You are compelled to design solutions that remove unnecessary steps. This mindset is the core skill of a product manager.

## The First Line Of Code
Time yourself doing the next thing you do online — booking a ticket, making a payment, signing up for something. Write down every step, every click, every wait. Now ask: which of these steps could be eliminated? That list is your design to-do.
