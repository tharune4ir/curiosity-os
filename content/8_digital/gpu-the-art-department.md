## The Invisible Architecture
The CPU is a brilliant chef who does one thing at a time, very fast. The GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) is not one chef — it is 5,000 mediocre chefs all working simultaneously. Each one handles one tiny piece of your screen. This parallel processing makes GPUs perfect for graphics (every pixel needs to be calculated independently), but also for training AI models — which also need the same kind of 'do millions of simple calculations simultaneously' architecture.

## The Consumer Trap
Gamers know GPUs mean better graphics. But no one talks about the fact that the AI revolution — ChatGPT, Stable Diffusion, every AI tool — runs almost entirely on GPUs. NVIDIA became the most valuable company in the world by making GPUs that the entire AI industry runs on.

## The Builder Hack
A builder training their own AI model knows they can rent a GPU on Google Colab for FREE, instead of buying expensive hardware. They can train a model that would take a normal laptop 3 months to train, and finish in a few hours — using cloud GPUs.

## The Superpower
You understand the physical backbone of the AI revolution. When someone says 'the GPU shortage', you know exactly why it matters and who it affects.

## The First Line Of Code
Search 'Google Colab'. Open a notebook. In the menu, go to Runtime > Change Runtime Type > GPU. You just connected to an NVIDIA A100 GPU somewhere in Google's data center, for free.
