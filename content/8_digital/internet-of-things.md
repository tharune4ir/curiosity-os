## The Invisible Architecture
IoT (Internet of Things) is the network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity. Your WiFi router, smart bulb, fitness band, Alexa speaker, and car GPS are all IoT devices — computers embedded in physical objects, sending data to the internet and receiving commands from it. Each IoT device is essentially a small computer with sensors, a network chip, and firmware — running the same client-server model as any website, just in a physical object.

## The Consumer Trap
When you say 'Alexa, turn off the lights', your voice travels from Alexa's mic to Amazon's servers (where voice recognition AI runs), back to your home router, to the smart bulb — in under a second. The 'smart' in smart home is almost entirely cloud-based intelligence, not local processing. If Amazon's servers go down, your lights don't respond.

## The Builder Hack
A builder uses a Raspberry Pi (3,000 mini-computer) with a temperature sensor to build a home monitoring system. The Pi reads temperature, sends data to a free cloud dashboard (like ThingSpeak), and sends a WhatsApp message if temperature crosses a threshold. Full IoT project: 3,000 and one weekend.

## The Superpower
You can bridge the physical and digital worlds. IoT builders create systems that sense reality, process it digitally, and act on the physical world — agriculture sensors, industrial monitoring, smart homes. This is one of the most exciting and impactful application areas of software.
