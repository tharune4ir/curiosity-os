## The Invisible Architecture
Every device connected to the internet needs an address so packets know where to go. An IP address is exactly like a home address for your device. Your home has 'Flat 4B, Tower 2, XYZ Society, Mumbai - 400001'. Your device has '192.168.1.15' (local) or '103.21.244.0' (public). Local IP is your flat number inside your building (your home network). Public IP is the address of the entire building (your router) that the outside world sees.

## The Consumer Trap
Everyone uses WhatsApp assuming total privacy. But every message sent is tagged with your device's IP address. The server receiving your message knows exactly where in the world you are sending from, which telecom provider you use, and potentially your city.

## The Builder Hack
A builder launching a website knows that if they host it on a shared server, their website shares an IP address with potentially 1,000 other websites. If another website on that IP sends spam, Google may flag the entire IP — hurting their site. So savvy builders get a dedicated IP.

## The Superpower
You understand digital identity at a fundamental level. You know how websites track your location, how VPNs mask your IP (they give you a different building address), and how servers identify traffic.

## The First Line Of Code
Go to 'whatismyip.com'. That is your public IP address — the address the entire internet sees you as. That single number represents your connection to the world.
