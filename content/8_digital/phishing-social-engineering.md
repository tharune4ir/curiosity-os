## The Invisible Architecture
The weakest link in any security system is not the software — it's the human. Social engineering is the art of manipulating people into revealing passwords, clicking malicious links, or granting access. A phishing attack sends you an email that looks exactly like SBI's email, with a link to a perfect copy of SBI's website, asking you to verify your account. The URL is slightly different — 'sbi-secure-login.com' instead of 'sbi.co.in' — but the page looks identical. You enter your password. It goes directly to the attacker.

## The Consumer Trap
Millions of Indians lose money to OTP fraud and phishing every year. The fraudsters don't hack banks — they call grandparents pretending to be bank employees and ask for the OTP. The bank's security is perfect; the human is the vulnerability.

## The Builder Hack
A builder building an app that handles money implements multi-factor authentication AND educates users in the app itself: 'Our support team will NEVER ask for your OTP.' This in-app security education is a product feature, not just a legal disclaimer.

## The Superpower
You are immune to most social engineering attacks because you understand their mechanics. You cannot be fooled by 'verify your account' emails or 'your account is compromised, share OTP' calls. This protection is worth more than any antivirus software.

## The First Line Of Code
Hover your mouse over any link in an email (don't click!). Look at the bottom-left of your browser — it shows the actual URL the link will take you to. If it doesn't match who sent the email, it's phishing. Test this on real emails today.
