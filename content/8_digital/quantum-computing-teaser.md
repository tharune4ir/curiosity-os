## The Invisible Architecture
Normal computers use bits — switches that are ON or OFF. Quantum computers use qubits. A qubit can be 0, 1, or BOTH AT THE SAME TIME (called superposition). Imagine a coin: a normal bit is a coin that's either heads or tails. A qubit is a coin that is SPINNING — simultaneously heads and tails — until you look at it. This lets quantum computers explore millions of possible solutions simultaneously instead of checking them one by one. For certain problems (like cracking encryption), this is incomprehensibly faster.

## The Consumer Trap
News headlines scream 'Quantum computer cracks all encryption!' and people panic. But quantum computers are currently about as powerful as a calculator for most tasks — they only outperform classical computers at a very specific set of problems. The panic is mostly overblown for now.

## The Builder Hack
A smart builder thinking about cybersecurity in 2024+ knows that today's encryption (RSA) could theoretically be broken by a large quantum computer. So they stay aware of 'post-quantum cryptography' — new encryption methods being designed to resist quantum attacks. Being early to this transition is a career superpower.
