## The Invisible Architecture
The CPU is the chef, but where does the chef put ingredients while cooking? On the kitchen countertop! RAM is that countertop — temporary working space. When you open WhatsApp, it jumps from your storage (fridge) onto the countertop (RAM) so the chef (CPU) can access it instantly. The bigger your countertop, the more apps you can have open at once without everything slowing down.

## The Consumer Trap
Every phone ad says '8GB RAM!' and everyone nods and buys it without understanding what RAM even does. They think more RAM means 'more storage for photos'. It does not. It means a bigger countertop.

## The Builder Hack
A builder running a Node.js web server knows: if they load too much data into RAM at once without clearing it, the program crashes. This is called a 'memory leak'. They write code that clears the countertop after each task — like a good chef who wipes down the counter.

## The Superpower
You understand why your 10,000 phone with 6GB RAM feels smoother than your friend's 4GB RAM phone. You can make informed hardware decisions and write efficient code that doesn't waste countertop space.

## The First Line Of Code
Open 10 apps on your phone, then check battery and RAM usage in Settings > About Phone. Watch the countertop filling up in real time.
