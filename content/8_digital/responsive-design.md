## The Invisible Architecture
A website that looks perfect on a laptop but breaks completely on a phone has failed responsive design. Responsive design means the layout automatically adapts to any screen size — desktop, tablet, phone, TV. In CSS, 'media queries' detect the screen size and apply different rules: 'If screen width < 600px, stack columns vertically instead of side by side'. In India, over 70% of internet usage is mobile. If a website isn't responsive, 70% of users have a terrible experience.

## The Consumer Trap
Old e-commerce and government websites in India were built for desktops in 2005. On a phone, elements overlap, text is unreadable, and buttons are too small to tap. These websites drive mobile users away but the organization doesn't notice because they only look at it on their desktop. This is a business-destroying blind spot.

## The Builder Hack
A builder designs 'mobile first' — they design for the smallest screen first and scale up, instead of designing for desktop and struggling to shrink it. This forces ruthless prioritization of content. What absolutely must be visible on a 5-inch screen? That becomes the core of the design.

## The Superpower
Your products work for everyone everywhere — from a 8,000 Android phone in rural Rajasthan to a 2 lakh MacBook in Mumbai. Your reach is the entire connected population of India, not just the desktop-using minority.

## The First Line Of Code
Press F12 on any website. Click the phone icon at the top of the DevTools panel. Your browser instantly simulates different phone screen sizes. Toggle between iPhone and Samsung Galaxy views. Watch how good (or terrible) the website looks at each size.
