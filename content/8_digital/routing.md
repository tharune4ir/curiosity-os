## The Invisible Architecture
When a packet needs to travel from Mumbai to London, it doesn't follow one fixed path. At each step, a router — a powerful computer whose only job is redirecting traffic — looks at the packet's destination IP and decides the best next stop. It's like a series of traffic police officers at every intersection who look at your auto-rickshaw and point you in the right direction. If one road (cable) is congested or broken, the router finds a different road.

## The Consumer Trap
When you switch from WiFi to mobile data, your internet 'works' seamlessly. People think the internet is one thing. It's actually thousands of independent networks (called Autonomous Systems) that have agreed to talk to each other through a protocol called BGP — and any of them can break at any moment.

## The Builder Hack
A builder using a CDN (Content Delivery Network) like Cloudflare is essentially placing their content at routers near every major city in the world, so user packets never have to travel far. This is advanced routing optimization — and it makes websites load 5x faster globally.

## The Superpower
You understand internet resilience — why the internet doesn't go down when one cable breaks — and you can design apps that are smart about network geography.

## The First Line Of Code
Run 'tracert google.com' in Command Prompt. Each numbered line is a different router your packet visited. You can literally see the path your data took across the physical internet.
