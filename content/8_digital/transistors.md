## The Invisible Architecture
A transistor is a microscopic switch made of silicon — a material found in sand. Modern CPUs contain over 50 billion transistors on a chip the size of your thumbnail. Each transistor can switch between ON and OFF billions of times per second. They are so small that over a million can fit in the period at the end of this sentence. These switches are the physical embodiment of Binary — 0s and 1s made real in silicon.

## The Consumer Trap
When a new iPhone comes out and people say 'it has a 3-nanometer chip', everyone is impressed but no one knows what it means. It means the transistors are so small that 3 nanometers is roughly 15 silicon atoms wide. That's the actual magic behind 'faster phone'.

## The Builder Hack
A smart builder understands Moore's Law — the observation that transistors double in a chip roughly every 2 years. This is why the 15,000 phone you buy today will be considered slow in 3 years. Builders time product launches around chip releases.

## The Superpower
You understand why hardware gets faster every year, why certain computations are physically impossible on today's hardware, and why quantum computing (which uses quantum states instead of ON/OFF) is such a big deal.
