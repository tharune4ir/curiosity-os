## The Black Box Revealed
ANI (Artificial Narrow Intelligence) is AI that is superhuman at one specific task — playing chess, diagnosing one disease from medical scans, translating between two languages. Every AI that exists today is ANI. AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) is AI that can learn and apply intelligence across any domain, the way humans can — reading, reasoning, planning, adapting, creating across all areas simultaneously. AGI does not exist yet. The gap between ANI and AGI may be the most important question in all of technology.

## The Lazy Human Trap
When AI writes a brilliant essay, people say 'AI is becoming intelligent' and panic about AGI. But writing a brilliant essay is still narrow — it is text prediction at massive scale. True AGI would mean AI that can walk into an unfamiliar problem in any domain and reason its way through it the way a smart human can. We are not there.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Use ANI systems for what they are actually good at: narrow, specific, repetitive tasks. Do not expect a language model to fix your plumbing, manage your social relationships, or understand your emotional situation deeply. Matching the right narrow tool to the right narrow task — instead of expecting general intelligence — makes you a better AI user.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The question of when and whether AGI arrives is the central question of AI development. It affects everything: regulation, safety research, business strategy, career planning. Students who understand the precise meaning of AGI — versus the hype around it — can evaluate news about 'AI breakthroughs' without either dismissing them or panicking.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'I am going to give you tasks. After each one, tell me honestly if this is something you are doing using narrow AI (pattern-matching at scale) or something that would require genuine general intelligence to do.' Then give it: a math problem, a translation task, a new philosophical dilemma, a question about what someone in a photograph is feeling.
