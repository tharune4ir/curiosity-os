## The Black Box Revealed
Current AI mostly responds to prompts with text. AI agents are the next step: AI that takes sequences of real-world actions to achieve a goal. An agent can browse the internet, write code and run it, send emails, book appointments, fill forms, and control software — all autonomously. You give it a goal ('research the best laptop for my budget and prepare a comparison report'), and it figures out the steps and executes them without you doing anything.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People will give AI agents tasks without specifying constraints or checkpoints. An unconstrained agent optimizing for a goal can take unexpected, irreversible actions. Telling an AI agent to 'clear your email inbox' without defining 'clear' could mean deleting instead of archiving. Agent errors are not just wrong text — they can be wrong actions with real-world consequences.

## The Symbiosis Hack
When using AI agents, always define: what success looks like (goal), what boundaries they cannot cross (constraints), what they must check with you before doing (checkpoints), and what actions are reversible vs. irreversible. This framework — goal, constraints, checkpoints, reversibility — is a skill that transfers to managing any automated system or even human employees.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Agentic AI will be the dominant paradigm within the next 5-10 years. Understanding what agents can and cannot do, how to design them safely, and how to supervise them intelligently will be a core professional skill across almost every industry. The students who understand agents today will design and manage them tomorrow.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'If I gave you, as an AI agent, the task of "Help me learn French in 6 months," what specific actions would you take, in what order, and what would you need to check with me before doing? Map out a complete agent workflow.' See if it can design an agentic system coherently.
