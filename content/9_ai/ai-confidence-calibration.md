## The Black Box Revealed
A well-calibrated predictor is one whose confidence matches its accuracy. If it says it is 90% confident in 100 different answers, about 90 of them should be correct. AI language models are often poorly calibrated — they sound equally confident whether they are 100% right or making something up. The confident tone is a stylistic pattern learned from authoritative human writing, not a genuine signal of knowledge or certainty.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Confident tone = trust. This is a cognitive bias (authority bias) that AI exploits unintentionally. The most dangerous AI errors are not the ones where the AI says 'I am not sure.' They are the ones where the AI is completely certain and completely wrong.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Always ask AI to give you its confidence and its alternative hypotheses: 'After your answer, rate your confidence from 1-10 and list 2 alternative answers that could also be correct.' This forces the AI into a probabilistic mindset. A humble, well-calibrated AI answer is safer than a confident wrong one.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Calibration is a core concept in data science, forecasting, and decision-making. The best forecasters (doctors, meteorologists, intelligence analysts) are calibrated: they know the limits of their knowledge. Training yourself to think in probabilities rather than certainties is a rare and powerful cognitive upgrade.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI 20 factual questions across different domains. After each answer, ask it to rate its confidence. Then fact-check all 20 answers. Calculate: do high-confidence answers correspond to higher accuracy? This is a direct test of AI calibration you can do yourself.
