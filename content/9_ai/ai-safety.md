## The Black Box Revealed
AI safety is the research area dedicated to ensuring that as AI systems become more capable, they remain beneficial and aligned with human values and do not cause catastrophic harm. This includes: preventing AI from being misused (cybersecurity, weapons), preventing AI from developing unintended dangerous behaviors (reward hacking, deceptive alignment), and ensuring AI remains controllable as it becomes more capable. Some of the world's most brilliant mathematicians and philosophers work on these problems full-time.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People dismiss AI safety as science fiction — something about killer robots that is irrelevant to today's AI. But today's AI safety problems are real: biased hiring algorithms, manipulative recommendation systems, AI-generated disinformation, vulnerable AI agents. The problems exist right now on a spectrum from concerning to catastrophic.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Engage with AI safety as both a practical and intellectual discipline. Practically: always assess the risk before deploying any AI system (who could be harmed? how?). Intellectually: explore the alignment problem — it is one of the most interesting and difficult problems in science. The overlap between philosophy, mathematics, psychology, and computer science makes it uniquely fascinating.

## The Future Proof Superpower
AI safety is massively underfunded relative to AI capability development. The organizations working on it are desperate for talented people. An Indian student who develops genuine expertise in AI safety — interpretability, red-teaming, alignment theory — has a direct path to working on what may be the most consequential technical problem of the century.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'Design a simple AI system to distribute food aid in a disaster area. Now, what are 5 ways this system could go catastrophically wrong, even if the developers had the best intentions? What safeguards would you build in?' This is practical safety thinking in miniature.
