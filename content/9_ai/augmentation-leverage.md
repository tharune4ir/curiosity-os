## The Black Box Revealed
In 2005, a freestyle chess experiment let humans use computers in competition against each other. The winners were not the best chess players, and not the best chess computers. They were human-computer teams called 'centaurs' — where the human provided strategic insight and the computer provided calculation depth. Together they beat both. This is augmentation leverage: the human-AI pair outperforming the pure human and pure AI. The strategic position to occupy is always the centaur, not the person being replaced.

## The Lazy Human Trap
The trap is treating AI as a replacement for your own thinking rather than an amplifier of it. Centaurs win because the human brings something the AI cannot: strategic intuition, contextual judgment, creative leaps, emotional intelligence, and knowledge of what the goal actually means. Remove the human and you get weaker chess. Remove the computer and you get weaker chess.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Develop your 'centaur profile': identify which parts of your thinking or work are best done by you (strategy, creativity, judgment, relationship, context) and which are best done by AI (research, summarization, drafting, formatting, calculation). Then design your workflow explicitly so you do your parts and AI does its parts. The explicit design is what separates intentional augmentation from accidental dependence.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The centaur model will be the dominant professional archetype of the AI age. The highest-value humans will be those who can orchestrate AI capabilities with their own judgment to produce outcomes neither could achieve alone. This is a skill that requires practice, self-awareness, and intentional design — starting now.

## The Prompt Experiment
Take a project you are currently working on. Map out every task in it. Assign each task to one of three categories: (1) Human only, (2) AI only, (3) Human+AI centaur. Then design a workflow for the centaur tasks — exactly when you work, when AI works, and how you hand off between each other. Run the project this way and evaluate.
