## The Black Box Revealed
Every major technological shift has triggered fear of mass unemployment: the printing press, the industrial revolution, electricity, computers, the internet. In every case, the technology created more jobs than it destroyed — but different jobs. The pattern: technology automates specific tasks within jobs, eliminating some jobs entirely but creating new jobs around the new technology. AI is following this pattern — but it is hitting cognitive work for the first time. Previous automation hit physical and routine jobs. AI hits knowledge work, which is new territory.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Two failure modes: (1) dismissing AI's impact because 'new technology always creates jobs' — this is statistically true historically but may not capture the speed and breadth of this transition, (2) panicking that 'AI will take all jobs' — this ignores the consistent pattern of technological complementarity. Neither blanket response is accurate.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Map your future career choices along two axes: how automatable are the core tasks, and how complemented by AI are the remaining tasks? Careers that are highly complemented by AI (where AI makes the human better at the human parts) are safer than careers that are fully automatable. A lawyer who uses AI for research and drafting becomes more productive. A data entry clerk whose entire job is data entry is at risk.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The students who survive and thrive in automation transitions are those who can identify, develop, and pivot to the human skills that emerge as most valuable after automation. These have historically been: creativity, complex social interaction, ethical judgment, physical dexterity in unstructured environments, and entrepreneurship. Start building now.

## The Prompt Experiment
Pick your dream career. Ask AI: 'Analyze my dream career of [X] in detail. Which specific tasks within this career are most automatable by AI in the next 10 years? Which tasks become MORE valuable because of AI? What new tasks might emerge in this career because of AI?' Use the answer to stress-test and strengthen your career plan.
