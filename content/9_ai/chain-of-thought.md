## The Black Box Revealed
By default, AI jumps to an answer immediately — like a student who blurts out the answer without thinking. But if you add 'Think step by step' to your prompt, something remarkable happens. The AI is forced to generate intermediate reasoning steps before reaching its answer. This is called Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting. Because AI generates text left-to-right, the act of writing out steps actually improves the quality of the final answer. Writing the reasoning creates the reasoning.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students use AI to get answers, not to understand processes. They copy the final answer from an AI without reading the reasoning steps. This is catastrophic for learning. The reasoning steps are the most valuable part — they show you the mental model for solving this class of problem.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Always ask AI to 'think step by step' for any problem involving logic, math, or analysis. Then read the steps critically. When a step seems wrong or unclear, challenge it: 'In step 3, why did you assume X? What if X were not true?' This turns AI into an interactive tutor that teaches reasoning, not just answers.

## The Future Proof Superpower
AI models that reason step-by-step (like o1, o3 series) are more powerful and more reliable than those that guess directly. The skill of structured, step-by-step reasoning is what separates excellent students from average ones — and Chain of Thought is a tool for practicing this mental habit daily.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI to solve a CBSE-level maths word problem in two ways: once with no instructions (direct answer), and once with 'Think step by step, show all working.' Compare the accuracy. The CoT version will almost always be more correct.
