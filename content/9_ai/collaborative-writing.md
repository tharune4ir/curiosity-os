## The Black Box Revealed
The most powerful way to write with AI is not to let AI write for you. It is to write your rough, imperfect, genuinely-yours first draft — and then use AI as a skilled editor, not a ghostwriter. Your first draft has your voice, your perspective, your life experience. AI's contribution is mechanical excellence: grammar, structure, vocabulary range, flow. The combination — your content, AI's form — produces writing that is both personal and polished.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students let AI write their first draft and then lightly edit it. The result is AI's voice, AI's ideas, slightly personalized. Teachers recognize this immediately. More importantly, the student loses the experience of wrestling with their own ideas in writing — one of the most powerful thinking exercises that exists.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Use the 'You Draft, AI Edits' protocol: Write your full first draft in one sitting without opening AI. Accept that it will be imperfect. Then open AI and paste your draft with this prompt: 'Here is my draft. Do not rewrite it. Instead, give me: (1) three specific sentences that are unclear and why, (2) three places where a stronger word would work, (3) one structural change that would improve flow.' Make those targeted edits yourself.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Every great writer uses editors. What is new is that AI makes world-class editing accessible to every student, in every language, instantly. The student who masters the writer-editor collaboration — maintaining their voice while accepting structural help — will produce writing that is both authentic and excellent.

## The Prompt Experiment
Write 3 paragraphs on any topic — personal, academic, creative. Upload to AI with two instructions: 'First, describe the voice and personality you detect in this writing. Second, suggest 5 targeted improvements WITHOUT rewriting any complete sentence.' Evaluate whether AI understood your voice.
