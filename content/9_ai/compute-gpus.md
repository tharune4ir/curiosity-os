## The Black Box Revealed
Training a large AI model is like running 10,000 cricket matches simultaneously and tracking every single ball, player, and score in real time. Normal computer chips (CPUs) can only handle one match at a time. GPUs (Graphics Processing Units) — originally built to render video game graphics — can handle thousands of calculations at once. This parallel processing is what makes training modern AI possible. One large AI training run can cost more than building a school.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People think AI is just 'software' and therefore cheap or free to make. The compute cost is enormous. This is why only a few big companies or rich countries can train the most powerful AI models from scratch. This creates a massive global power imbalance.

## The Symbiosis Hack
A smart student uses this knowledge strategically. Instead of trying to 'run AI yourself,' you understand that AI-as-a-service (using APIs, Claude, Gemini) gives you access to trillion-dollar compute infrastructure for free or very cheap. You leverage the engine without owning it — like riding a metro instead of building a railway.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The AI chip race (NVIDIA, AMD, Google's TPUs, India's planned semiconductor push) will define which countries lead AI. Understanding compute is understanding geopolitics and economics of the next decade. This knowledge is rare among students your age.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'If I wanted to train a simple model to recognize 10 handwritten digits, estimate how many multiplications a GPU would perform. Break it down step by step.' See if it can reason about compute scale.
