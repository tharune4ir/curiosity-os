## The Black Box Revealed
When you start a new conversation with an AI, it remembers nothing from previous conversations. Every session, it is born fresh with no memory of you. Even within a long conversation, it can only hold so much in its 'working memory' (the context window). If a conversation runs very long, the AI literally cannot see what was said at the beginning anymore — it falls off the edge of its context window. This is not how human relationships or human tutors work at all.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students establish a learning relationship with an AI, think they are 'making progress' together, then start a new session and find the AI has no memory. They spend the first 10 messages re-explaining their situation. This is wildly inefficient and frustrating.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Maintain a personal 'AI context document' — a 200-word summary of who you are, your learning goals, your preferred style, and what you have been working on. Paste this at the start of every new AI session. This is your 'AI relationship memory' that you maintain yourself, not the AI.

## The Future Proof Superpower
AI systems with true long-term memory of individual users are coming. But they raise profound privacy questions: do you want an AI to remember everything you ever told it? Understanding the trade-offs between memory, personalization, and privacy is a critical digital literacy skill.

## The Prompt Experiment
Have a rich 20-message conversation with an AI about a complex topic. Then open a brand-new session and ask the same AI: 'What did we discuss in our previous conversation?' It will have no idea. This stark demonstration of context forgetting is one of the most educational AI moments you can have.
