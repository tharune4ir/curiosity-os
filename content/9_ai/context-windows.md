## The Black Box Revealed
When you talk to an AI, it does not remember your previous conversations from yesterday. Even within a single conversation, it can only 'see' a limited amount of text at once — this is the context window. Older models had a window of 4,000 tokens (about 10 pages). New models have windows of 100,000+ tokens (200+ pages). But here is the key: even in a large context window, AI pays less attention to the middle of a long conversation — a phenomenon researchers call 'lost in the middle.'

## The Lazy Human Trap
People dump enormous amounts of text into an AI conversation expecting it to perfectly track everything. As the conversation grows longer, early details get 'lost in the middle' and the AI starts forgetting or contradicting itself. Many students submit their entire textbook chapter and wonder why the AI gives generic answers.

## The Symbiosis Hack
A smart student works within the context window strategically. For long projects, they start fresh conversations for each distinct task. They use structured summaries: 'Here is what we decided so far: [bullet points]. Now continue from here.' They keep the AI's working memory clean and focused.

## The Future Proof Superpower
AI companies are racing to expand context windows. But even an infinite context window requires strategies for focus. The human skill of summarizing, chunking, and prioritizing information becomes *more* valuable as AI context grows, not less.

## The Prompt Experiment
Start a long conversation with an AI. 15-20 messages in, ask it to recall a specific detail from message 2. See if it remembers accurately. Then ask it what you discussed 'in the middle' of the conversation. Watch how context fades.
