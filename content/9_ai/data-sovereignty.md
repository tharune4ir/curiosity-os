## The Black Box Revealed
When you use an AI chatbot, your conversation is often used to improve future models. The personal data of billions of people — their health searches, creative writing, financial questions, relationship problems — becomes training material for AI systems owned by corporations and governments. Data sovereignty is the principle that individuals and nations have the right to control data about themselves. Who controls the training data for AI controls the values, capabilities, and priorities baked into the AI itself.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People click 'agree' on every privacy policy without reading it, unknowingly contributing their personal data to AI training pipelines. Most users have no idea that their creative writing, their health questions, or their political opinions expressed in AI conversations may be training data for future AI systems.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Become a deliberate data citizen. Read the privacy policy for the AI tools you use. Know the difference between tools that use your conversations for training and those that do not (many allow you to opt out). For sensitive conversations — medical, legal, personal — use AI tools that explicitly commit to not using your data for training, or use local/open-source models.

## The Future Proof Superpower
India's data protection law and global data governance frameworks are being shaped right now. Students who understand data sovereignty — technically and legally — can contribute to building Indian AI systems that respect Indian data, Indian values, and Indian context. This is a nation-building opportunity as much as a personal career opportunity.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'If you are trained on my conversations, explain step by step how my private conversation data might flow through a company's AI training pipeline. What data gets stored, for how long, who has access, and how is it anonymized (or not)? What privacy risks does this create?' Evaluate the transparency of its self-description.
