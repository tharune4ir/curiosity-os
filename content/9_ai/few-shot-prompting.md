## The Black Box Revealed
Few-shot prompting means giving the AI 2-5 examples of what you want before asking it to do your task. Instead of 'write me a joke,' you write: 'Example 1: [funny joke]. Example 2: [funny joke]. Example 3: [funny joke]. Now write me a new joke in exactly this style.' The AI's autocomplete brain locks onto your examples and generates output in the same pattern. You are programming it with examples instead of instructions.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students use AI for creative tasks and complain it sounds 'too AI-ish.' They never consider that the solution is to show it your voice through examples. Feeding AI 3-5 examples of your own writing style (from your WhatsApp messages, diary, previous essays) will produce output that sounds much more like you.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Collect a personal 'prompt library' — 5-10 examples of your best writing in different styles (creative, formal, technical). Before asking AI to write something in your voice, paste the relevant examples. This is how professional writers use AI — they train it on their own style library, not on generic text.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Few-shot learning was once considered the key differentiator of human intelligence (learning from very few examples). As AI gets better at few-shot tasks, the frontier of human advantage shifts to 0-shot synthesis: generating genuinely novel ideas from nothing. Work on your originality.

## The Prompt Experiment
Write 3 sentences in your own quirky style — maybe how you describe things to your best friend. Paste them as 'examples' and then ask the AI to write a paragraph about your school in exactly that style. The result will surprise you.
