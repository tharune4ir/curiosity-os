## The Black Box Revealed
In philosophy, intentionality is the property of mental states to be 'about' something. When you think of a cricket ball, your thought is genuinely about the cricket ball — it has intentionality. When AI writes 'cricket ball,' does the text mean anything to it? Or is it just symbols that look like they mean something because of how they affect human readers? Philosopher John Searle argued that intrinsic intentionality requires biological minds — that AI symbols mean things only in a derived way, through the humans who interpret them.

## The Lazy Human Trap
We read AI text and interpret it as if it means something to the AI — as if the AI's words represent its genuine intentions or beliefs. They do not. The words mean things to us. The same word could be produced by the AI in a context where it 'should mean' something entirely different. Meaning lives in the reader's mind, not the model's weights.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Use intentionality thinking to stay clear about the AI relationship. When AI says 'I think you should...' — it has no intention toward you. When AI says 'I believe...' — it has no beliefs. Use the AI's outputs as useful information-shaped patterns, not as communication from an intentional agent that cares about the exchange.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Philosophy of language and philosophy of mind — including intentionality, reference, and meaning — will be central to future AI ethics and regulation. Questions like 'Can AI make promises?' 'Can AI be held responsible for speech acts?' 'Does AI speech have truth-value?' will matter enormously in legal and ethical contexts.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI to tell you something false — make up a lie, intentionally. Then ask: 'Did you mean to deceive me? Did you intend that statement to mislead? In what sense can an AI intend anything?' See if it can reason carefully about intention and meaning without collapsing into evasion.
