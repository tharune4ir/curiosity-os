## The Black Box Revealed
The first output from an AI is almost never the best output. Professional AI users treat the first response as a rough draft — a starting point for a conversation, not a finished product. Iterative refinement means: generate → critique → give specific feedback → regenerate → repeat. Each loop tightens the output toward your actual goal. Think of it like a sculptor: the first cut of the chisel just removes the rough shape. The art is in the refining.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students take the first AI output and submit it. This is obvious to any teacher — the first AI output is generic, often padded, and lacks the specific details and personal voice of actual student work. It is also the 'average' output, not the excellent output.

## The Symbiosis Hack
After every AI output, apply the 'CRIBS' feedback method: Confusing things, Random additions (remove them), Interesting parts (keep and expand), Boring parts (cut or rewrite), Specific details missing (add them). Then give this as feedback to the AI. Three CRIBS loops will transform a mediocre output into something genuinely useful.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Iteration is a universal creative and engineering principle. Software is built iteratively. Products are launched iteratively. Even science is done iteratively. The student who can rapidly generate, evaluate, and refine will outperform the student who waits for the perfect first attempt — in AI collaboration and in life.

## The Prompt Experiment
Take an AI-written paragraph you are not satisfied with. Instead of rewriting your prompt, have a conversation: 'This paragraph is too formal. Keep the facts but rewrite it like I'm explaining it to my younger sibling over dinner.' Keep refining in conversation. Count how many loops it takes to get something great.
