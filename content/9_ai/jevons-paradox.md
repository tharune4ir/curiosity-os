## The Black Box Revealed
In 1865, economist William Stanley Jevons noticed something counterintuitive: when steam engines became more fuel-efficient, coal consumption did not decrease — it massively increased. Why? Because cheaper engines enabled more applications, more factories, more uses. This is Jevons Paradox: making a resource more efficient increases its total consumption. Applied to AI: making thinking-assistance cheaper and more powerful does not mean humans will think less. It means human thinking will be applied to more problems, bigger problems, and new problems that were previously out of reach.

## The Lazy Human Trap
The panic about AI is that 'AI will make us lazy and we will stop thinking.' Jevons Paradox suggests the opposite: as AI handles the routine cognitive work, human minds will be applied to more creative, strategic, and complex thinking. The total volume of human thinking may actually increase, just shifted to higher-order tasks.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Use AI to eliminate your cognitive bottlenecks — the routine, the repetitive, the tedious. Then reinvest the freed mental energy in the problems that actually excite you. The student who lets AI do the formatting, the research collation, and the first draft can spend their energy on original ideas, creative leaps, and genuine problem-solving.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Jevons Paradox predicts that the AI age will be an age of expanded human intellectual production, not reduced production. The humans who thrive will be those who can identify new problems worth solving — problems that only become visible and tractable because AI cleared the path. Curiosity and problem-finding become the scarcest skills.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'Apply Jevons Paradox to AI itself. If AI makes information access and analysis dramatically cheaper, what NEW cognitive demands will humans face that they did not face before? Give me 5 specific examples of new problems that AI creates even as it solves old ones.' Evaluate the depth of its economic reasoning.
