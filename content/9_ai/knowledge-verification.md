## The Black Box Revealed
Because AI hallucinates with confidence, every claim it makes that you plan to use must be independently verified. Knowledge verification is the systematic practice of cross-checking AI outputs against primary sources — original textbooks, official websites, peer-reviewed papers, or direct expert consultation. This is not a critique of AI — it is the same protocol journalists, doctors, and scientists apply to any source. No single source is trusted without verification.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students treat AI verification as optional — something to do only when they suspect an error. This is backwards. Always verify claims you plan to use, especially statistics, historical facts, quotes, scientific claims, and anything about specific individuals. The errors come exactly where you do not expect them, because AI is most dangerous when it sounds most confident.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Use AI to help you verify itself. After getting a key fact, ask: 'How could I verify this claim? What primary source would prove or disprove it? Give me the exact search query I would use.' AI will often point you to the real source. You are using AI's knowledge of knowledge to guide your verification.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Source verification is the foundational skill for journalists, lawyers, scientists, and historians. In a world where AI can generate convincing disinformation at scale, the ability to trace a claim back to its primary source is not just academic — it is a survival skill for navigating information.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI for 5 specific statistics on a topic you find interesting. For each statistic, ask: 'What is the original source of this specific number? Give me the organization, report name, and year.' Then try to find the original source on the internet. How many of the 5 sources actually exist and say what AI claimed?
