## The Black Box Revealed
Learning FROM AI means: AI explains, you absorb. Learning WITH AI means: you struggle, attempt, reflect, and use AI as a thinking partner that challenges and supports your own process. The difference is who does the cognitive work. In 'from AI' mode, the AI learns nothing and neither do you. In 'with AI' mode, your brain builds genuine understanding. Reading a solved problem teaches you nothing. Struggling with it and then comparing to a solution teaches you everything.

## The Lazy Human Trap
AI makes the 'from' mode dangerously easy and immediately rewarding. You feel like you are learning because you are reading correct, well-explained content. But comprehension and retention require your own effort, errors, and reflection. The easy path feels like learning but is closer to reading entertainment.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Always engage before asking. For any topic, spend 5 minutes writing down everything you already think you know (even if wrong). Then use AI to fill gaps and correct errors. This 'pre-loading' of your own prior knowledge dramatically improves retention. The AI corrects a belief you held — that is memorable. The AI tells you a fact you never held — that is forgettable.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Active learning techniques (spaced repetition, retrieval practice, interleaving) are proven by cognitive science to produce durable learning. AI can scaffold all of these methods. The student who learns how to learn with AI will outperform the one who just learns from AI across every subject and every decade.

## The Prompt Experiment
Pick a topic for tomorrow's class. Tonight, spend 5 minutes writing your best current explanation of it — mistakes included. Photograph it. Then ask AI to gently correct your explanation and fill in gaps. The next day in class, track how many of the corrected points you remember perfectly.
