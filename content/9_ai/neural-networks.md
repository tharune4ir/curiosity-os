## The Black Box Revealed
Your brain has billions of neurons. When you learn something new — like riding a cycle — certain neurons fire together and form a connection. Neural networks in AI are a digital copy of this idea. They have thousands of artificial 'neurons' arranged in layers. Data goes in at one end, travels through layers where each neuron votes on what it sees, and an answer comes out the other end. The more they practice on examples, the stronger the right connections get.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People hear 'neural network' and think it's a real brain in a computer — that it feels, understands, or experiences things. It does not. It is pure mathematics: just multiplication and addition happening millions of times per second. There is no ghost in the machine.

## The Symbiosis Hack
A smart student uses this knowledge to understand *why* AI is bad at things humans find easy. Your brain can look at one photo of a dog and recognize all dogs forever. An AI neural network needs 10,000 dog photos to learn. Knowing this, you never ask AI to judge something from a single example. You give it many examples.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Neural networks are the foundation of almost every AI system — from Google Translate to YouTube recommendations. Even if the specific app you use changes completely, the underlying logic of 'layers of neurons learning from data' will remain. You understand the engine, not just the car.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask an AI: 'Explain what happens inside a neural network when it reads the word "Sachin" — trace the journey from input to output like a story.' See how it describes the layers.
