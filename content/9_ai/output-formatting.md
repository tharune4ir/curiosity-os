## The Black Box Revealed
AI can output the same information in hundreds of different formats: a table, a poem, a JSON list, a WhatsApp message, a formal report, a 3-act play script, a numbered list, or a 280-character tweet. Most people forget to specify format and get whatever default the AI picks. Specifying format is separate from specifying content — you must tell AI both *what* to say AND *how* to present it.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students ask for a 'summary' and get 6 paragraphs of dense text when they needed 5 bullet points to put in their notes. Or they ask for a 'plan' and get a narrative story when they needed a day-by-day table. Always specify the exact output format: 'Give me a table with 3 columns: Concept, Definition, Real-world Example.'

## The Symbiosis Hack
Build format templates for your most common AI tasks. 'Flash Card Format: Front: [Concept]. Back: [Simple definition in one sentence] + [One example].' Ask AI to produce 20 flashcards in this exact format for any chapter. Paste them directly into a flashcard app like Anki. You just automated your entire revision process.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Structured data output (JSON, XML, CSV) from AI is the foundation of building AI-powered apps. Knowing how to ask AI for structured output means you can feed AI output directly into other tools and programs. This is the bridge between 'chatting with AI' and 'building with AI.'

## The Prompt Experiment
Take one concept from your Science textbook. Ask AI to explain it in all 5 formats: (1) a tweet, (2) a table, (3) a story for a child, (4) a question-and-answer interview, (5) a rap. See how format reshapes your understanding.
