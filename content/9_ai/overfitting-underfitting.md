## The Black Box Revealed
Imagine a student who memorizes every single answer from last year's CBSE question paper. They score 100% on that exact paper but fail on the new exam because they memorized, not understood. This is overfitting — the AI memorized training data instead of learning patterns. The opposite is underfitting: the student barely studied anything and fails on everything. A good AI (and a good student) generalizes — uses patterns from the past to answer new, unseen questions correctly.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People think 'more data always makes AI smarter.' Not always. If training is too long or the dataset is too small and repetitive, the AI memorizes instead of generalizes. This is why AI can fail spectacularly on slightly unusual inputs that weren't in its training data.

## The Symbiosis Hack
When using AI for studying, a smart student tests it with unfamiliar problems, not just textbook examples. If the AI gives a perfect textbook answer, push it: 'Now apply this concept to a situation I made up: [novel scenario].' This tests whether it generalized the concept or just memorized an answer.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The best human minds don't memorize — they develop robust mental models that transfer across situations. Training your own thinking to generalize (and not just memorize for exams) is the human skill that remains valuable even as AI memorizes everything for us.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI to solve a standard physics problem. Then modify the problem slightly (different values, reversed scenario) and ask again. See if the quality drops significantly — this exposes the boundary between its generalization and memorization.
