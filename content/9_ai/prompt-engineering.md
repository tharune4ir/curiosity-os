## The Black Box Revealed
Prompt engineering is the art and science of writing instructions for AI to get the best output. It includes: being specific about format and length, giving context and role ('You are a CBSE Physics teacher'), using examples (few-shot), breaking complex tasks into steps, and specifying what you do NOT want. Think of it like directing a very capable but very literal actor. The actor is brilliant but does exactly what the script says — so you need to write a good script.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Most people write lazy prompts: 'Explain photosynthesis.' Then they complain when the answer is either too basic or too advanced. They blame the AI when the issue is the prompt. A vague question always gets a vague answer. Garbage in, garbage out.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Develop a standard 'prompt template' for studying. Example: 'You are my CBSE Class 10 Science tutor. Explain [TOPIC] in 3 levels: (1) a simple analogy for a 12-year-old, (2) the NCERT textbook explanation, (3) a harder concept for deeper understanding. End with 3 practice questions.' This one template, used consistently, will accelerate learning across every subject.

## The Future Proof Superpower
As AI becomes more powerful, prompts will become shorter and AI will fill in the gaps better. But the underlying skill — knowing what you want, specifying constraints, iterating on output — is a thinking skill that transfers everywhere. Prompt engineering is really just precise communication.

## The Prompt Experiment
Take a topic from your homework. Write the worst possible prompt (vague, one word). Save the output. Then spend 3 minutes writing the most detailed, specific prompt you can. Compare outputs. Calculate how much the prompt quality changed the result.
