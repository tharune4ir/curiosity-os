## The Black Box Revealed
Socrates was an ancient Greek philosopher who taught by asking questions, never giving answers. He believed that being challenged with questions was more educational than being given information. Socratic prompting inverts the normal AI dynamic: instead of asking AI for answers, you ask AI to ask you questions. 'Quiz me on Chapter 5 of NCERT Chemistry. Ask me one question at a time. If I get it wrong, do not give me the answer — instead, give me a hint and ask again.' The AI becomes a tutor, not a search engine.

## The Lazy Human Trap
The standard AI interaction makes you mentally passive: you ask, AI answers, you copy. This feels productive but builds no real understanding or memory. Passive information consumption is the learning trap of the digital age. Socratic prompting flips this and activates your brain.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Before any exam, run a 30-minute Socratic session. Set the AI in 'quiz master' mode: 'You are a strict CBSE examiner for Class 10 Science. Quiz me on [chapter]. Start easy, get harder. Score my answers. If I get 3 in a row right, move to the next topic. If I struggle, stay on the concept.' This is adaptive learning — customized to your exact weak spots.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The capacity to be challenged, to not know, and to figure it out through questioning is a foundational learning skill. The best universities teach this way. The students who can direct their own learning through structured self-challenge will adapt to any topic, in any future landscape.

## The Prompt Experiment
Open an AI and say: 'I am going to explain the water cycle to you as if you know nothing. Your job is to interrupt me with probing questions to test whether I actually understand, not just memorize. Ask me about edge cases, about why, about what would happen if one element changed. Begin.' Then explain the water cycle.
