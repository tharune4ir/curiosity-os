## The Black Box Revealed
Human brainstorming is limited by cognitive biases: we brainstorm within our experience and knowledge. We think of variations on what we already know. AI has no such limitation — it has been trained on the breadth of human thought across every domain. Synthetic brainstorming is using AI to generate ideas far outside your normal range, then using your human judgment to filter, combine, and refine. AI provides quantity and diversity; you provide quality and context.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students ask AI for 'creative ideas' and then take the first one without judgment. AI brainstorming without human curation produces generic, safe ideas — because the AI defaults to the most statistically common suggestions. The value is in generating 50 ideas and finding the two unusual ones that spark something real in your mind.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Run a structured synthetic brainstorm: 'Give me 20 ideas for my school science fair project — the first 10 should be conventional and safe, the next 5 should be unusual but feasible, and the last 5 should be completely wild and impossible. I will take the wild ideas as inspiration for something new.' The crossing of impossible and feasible is where real innovation lives.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The best innovators are those who can range widely across domains and then synthesize unexpected connections. Using AI to deliberately expose yourself to ideas from science, art, history, and culture that you would never encounter in your normal reading is a rapid way to expand your conceptual range.

## The Prompt Experiment
Think of a problem you are trying to solve — for school, a hobby, or your community. Ask AI: 'How would a jazz musician solve this? How would a sushi chef solve this? How would a 19th-century ship captain solve this? How would a 5-year-old solve this?' Take the most interesting idea from each.
