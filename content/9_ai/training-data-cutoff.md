## The Black Box Revealed
AI models are trained on data collected up to a specific date — the training cutoff. After that date, the AI knows nothing new. It does not know who won the last election, the latest scientific discoveries, or what happened in the news last month. If you ask about events after its cutoff, it will either say it does not know, or — worse — it will hallucinate a plausible-sounding but completely wrong answer based on prior patterns.

## The Lazy Human Trap
Students use AI for current events research — recent policies, new scientific papers, this year's CBSE changes, current sports statistics. The AI may confidently answer with outdated or entirely fabricated information. For anything time-sensitive, AI without internet access is the wrong tool.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Always check an AI's knowledge cutoff date before using it for research (usually listed in its documentation). For recent information, use AI with built-in web search (like Perplexity AI, or Claude/Gemini with search enabled). Alternatively, retrieve the article yourself and paste it into the AI conversation for it to analyze.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Understanding knowledge freshness is critical for all information sources, not just AI. Wikipedia has edit dates. Research papers have publication dates. News has reporting dates. The habit of asking 'When was this known? What might have changed since?' is fundamental critical thinking.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask an AI: 'Who is the current Prime Minister of India and what are their most recent policy announcements this month?' Compare the answer to actual current news. You will see the cutoff boundary in action — the AI either knows only up to its training date or admits uncertainty.
