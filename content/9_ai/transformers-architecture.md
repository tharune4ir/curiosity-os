## The Black Box Revealed
In 2017, a team at Google published a paper titled 'Attention Is All You Need.' The invention inside — called the Transformer — is the engine behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and almost every powerful AI today. Before Transformers, AI read text word by word, like a slow reader. The Transformer reads the entire paragraph at once and figures out which words are important *relative to each other*. It's like going from reading a cricket scorecard ball by ball to seeing the whole match summary at once.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People think each new AI (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) is a completely different invention. They are mostly all variations of the same Transformer architecture. Like how different cars all have the same basic engine structure, just with different tuning. Don't be dazzled by new brand names.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Because you know all major AI models share this architecture, you realize that prompt skills you develop for one AI transfer to all others. You are not learning 'how to use ChatGPT.' You are learning how to command Transformer-based systems. This skill applies everywhere.

## The Future Proof Superpower
Even if entirely new AI architectures replace Transformers someday (researchers are working on this), understanding the core problems they solved — reading context, tracking relationships in data — will help you evaluate what comes next.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'Explain how the Transformer architecture changed AI the way the steam engine changed manufacturing. Use that exact analogy and extend it.' Then evaluate: did it understand the metaphor or just repeat facts?
