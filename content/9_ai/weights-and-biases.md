## The Black Box Revealed
Inside every AI neural network are billions of little number dials called weights. Think of it like a mixing board in a music studio. Each weight decides how important one signal is compared to another. During training, the AI adjusts all these dials trillions of times until the music (the output) sounds right. 'Biases' are extra knobs that help the AI shift its answer in a direction. The final AI model is just a massive collection of these carefully tuned numbers.

## The Lazy Human Trap
People think AI 'decided' things consciously. It did not. The weights are just numbers found by a mathematical optimization process. There is no hidden agenda or preference — just patterns baked into numbers. When AI seems 'opinionated,' it is just reflecting patterns from its training data.

## The Symbiosis Hack
Understanding weights helps you understand fine-tuning. Big companies take a general AI and further train (fine-tune) its weights on specific data — like medical textbooks or legal documents — to make it an expert. A smart student can conceptually do this: tell the AI 'You are an expert CBSE Physics tutor. Always explain using the NCERT framework.' You are manually biasing its responses.

## The Future Proof Superpower
The concept of weights is why AI models can be saved, shared, and loaded. Open-source AI weights (like some models released freely online) mean anyone can run an AI on their own computer. Understanding this puts you in the conversation about who controls AI power.

## The Prompt Experiment
Ask AI: 'What would happen if all the weights in a neural network were set to zero? Walk me through what the AI would output.' This tests whether the AI can reason about its own architecture.
