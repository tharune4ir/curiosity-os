## The Invisible Reality
For every particle there is an antiparticle: same mass, opposite charge. For example, the electron has a positron (positive electron) partner. When a particle meets its antiparticle, they annihilate each other, converting into photons or other particles. Antimatter was abundant right after the Big Bang, but matter slightly won out. Antimatter and matter pair creation/annihilation is a direct consequence of energy-mass equivalence.

## The Everyday Encounter
We use antimatter in medicine: PET scans inject a small positron-emitting substance; when those positrons meet electrons, they produce detectable gamma rays, creating an image of your body. Light bulbs like fluorescent tubes also involve electrons (though not full antimatter, they involve electron transitions that create photons). Occasionally, cosmic rays hitting the atmosphere create positrons (we detect these particles in space experiments).

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Positrons: The electron’s antiparticle, used in PET imaging. 2. Antiprotons & Antineutrons: Made in accelerators and combined into antihydrogen. 3. Annihilation: Matter + antimatter → energy (photons) or other particles.

## The God Mode Tech
Antimatter is used in medical imaging (PET scans) and high-energy physics experiments. If we could produce and store enough, antihydrogen is considered for future high-efficiency rocket fuel, since matter-antimatter annihilation is the most energetic reaction.

## The Fascinating Truth
Our body contains a tiny bit of naturally formed antimatter: physics experiments found that even bananas produce positrons from potassium-40 decay. Also, humanity once trapped antihydrogen atoms – anti-atoms – confirming they orbit like normal atoms in magnetic traps. Sadly, in our matter-dominated world, any contact with antimatter causes explosions.
