## The Invisible Reality
Dark matter and dark energy are mysterious components making up about 95% of the universe’s content【111†L381-L385】. Dark matter acts like invisible mass: we infer it from gravity (galaxies spin too fast not to fly apart). It doesn’t emit or reflect light, so it’s truly dark. Dark energy is a uniform energy that fills space and causes the universe’s expansion to accelerate. Though we don’t know exactly what they are, their gravitational effects shape the cosmos.

## The Everyday Encounter
We can’t touch or see dark matter directly, but it’s hiding in our galaxy’s halo, helping to keep stars in orbit. Dark energy’s effects are measured by observing distant supernovae (explosions) whose brightness tells us the expansion speed. Technology doesn’t use dark stuff yet, but understanding it is crucial for fundamental physics.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Galaxy Rotation Curves: Stars orbit faster than can be explained by visible mass alone, implying extra invisible mass (dark matter). 2. Cosmic Expansion: Observations show space expansion speeding up, attributed to dark energy (often modeled as a constant energy density of space). 3. Composition: Roughly 27% dark matter, 68% dark energy, only ~5% normal matter【111†L381-L385】.

## The God Mode Tech
No devices directly use dark matter/energy. However, precise instruments like atomic clocks and satellite probes (like the probe measuring cosmic expansion) are designed knowing these effects.

## The Fascinating Truth
Strangely, everything you see (planets, stars, people) is just a small fraction (~5%) of the universe’s total mass-energy. The rest is dark – invisible but dominant【111†L381-L385】. Understanding dark matter and energy is the biggest modern mystery in physics.
