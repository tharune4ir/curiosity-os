## The Invisible Reality
The Doppler Effect is a change in frequency (pitch) of a wave due to motion between source and observer. If you move toward a sound, the sound waves compress, so you hear a higher pitch; moving away stretches the waves, lowering pitch. This applies not just to sound but to light as well (astronomers see stars’ light shifted). It happens because motion changes how often wave crests reach you.

## The Everyday Encounter
When a police siren zooms past, the pitch of the siren suddenly drops – that’s the Doppler Effect. Likewise, a passing car horn sounds higher as it approaches and lower as it recedes. Astronomers use this: they see spectral lines of stars shifted slightly (red or blue) to tell if stars are moving away or toward us.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Sound Doppler: Moving source or listener changes pitch of sound. 2. Light Doppler: Moving light source shifts color (redshift/blueshift). 3. Radar & Medical: Doppler radar measures weather (wind speed), and Doppler ultrasound measures blood flow by pitch change.

## The God Mode Tech
Speed cameras and police radars use Doppler-shifted microwaves to measure vehicle speed. Ultrasound doctors use Doppler shifts to see how fast blood flows in your veins. Astronomers measure expansion of the universe by looking at galaxy redshifts (light Doppler).

## The Fascinating Truth
The Doppler Effect shows that motion literally stretches or squeezes waves. A cool fact: planets produce slightly different radio signals as they rotate, and we can measure their rotation by Doppler shifts too. Even a siren’s pitch change proves space and motion affect waves directly.
