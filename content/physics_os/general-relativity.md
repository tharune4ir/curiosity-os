## The Invisible Reality
General Relativity says gravity isn’t a mysterious force pulling; it’s the warping of space and time by mass. Imagine space as a trampoline: a heavy mass (like Earth) makes a dip. Smaller masses move along the curved paths on this dip. So planets orbit stars because of curved spacetime, not invisible strings. In this way, what we felt as 'gravity' is really objects following straight lines in curved spacetime.

## The Everyday Encounter
The most practical example is GPS: clocks on satellites tick faster than ones on Earth because they’re higher up in Earth’s gravity well. Physicists must account for this gravitational time dilation or your phone’s position would drift rapidly. Another effect: light bending in gravity causes stars to appear slightly shifted near the Sun (seen during eclipses). This is a direct test of spacetime curvature.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Curved Spacetime: Mass tells spacetime how to curve, and curvature tells mass how to move (as John Wheeler said). 2. Gravitational Time Dilation: Clocks closer to a massive body run slower (a clock on a mountain runs slightly faster than at sea level). 3. Black Holes: Extremely dense mass creates holes in space where not even light escapes (a singularity inside).

## The God Mode Tech
Aside from GPS, general relativity is used to understand black holes and gravitational waves (like LIGO’s detection). It’s essential in astrophysics calculations (orbit of Mercury, signal timing for satellites).

## The Fascinating Truth
Near a black hole, space and time swap roles: what we think of as time would behave like a spatial direction inside the horizon. Also, gravity really does slow time – even Earth’s gravity is enough that precise clocks at different altitudes tick at measurably different rates.
