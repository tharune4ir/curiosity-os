## The Invisible Reality
Gravity is an invisible pull between any two masses. The Earth’s mass pulls on you and your mass pulls on Earth, even if you can’t see it. This force makes objects accelerate downward and keeps planets orbiting stars. Even though you can’t see gravity, it’s like an unseen magnet for mass. Einstein’s view adds that mass actually curves space, so objects follow that curvature as if being pulled.

## The Everyday Encounter
Every time you drop an apple or jump in the air, you see gravity at work: the apple falls and you come back down. A spinning ceiling fan’s blades stay attached by gravity. When you stand on a scale, it reads the force from gravity pulling you down. Pumping water into a high-rise tank shows gravity: the water won’t flow upstairs unless you push it up first, and then it falls through the pipes to your tap.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Universal Attraction: All masses attract each other. 2. Orbits: Satellites and planets stay in orbit because gravity pulls them in while they move forward. 3. Microgravity: In free-fall (like orbit), you feel weightless because you and your surroundings are falling together.

## The God Mode Tech
Gravity is essential for space travel and astronomy. Rockets must reach escape velocity to leave Earth’s gravity well. Satellites use gravity to stay in orbit (like GPS and weather satellites). Engineers even harness gravity in hydroelectric dams and in simple tools like water slides and roller coasters.

## The Fascinating Truth
Gravity is surprisingly weak compared to other forces, yet it dominates large-scale motion.
