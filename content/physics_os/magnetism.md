## The Invisible Reality
Magnetism comes from moving electric charges or the intrinsic spin of electrons. A magnet’s field is invisible, but it exerts forces on other magnets or iron. Actually, a magnetic field is just a twisted form of an electric field for moving observers. This is why a moving electric charge can act like a tiny magnet. Essentially, electricity and magnetism are two sides of the same coin – change one, and you get the other.

## The Everyday Encounter
You encounter magnetism with fridge magnets and compasses. Magnets hold your drawings on the refrigerator door without even touching it (that’s a force at work). The compass in your parents’ car or a phone points north because Earth is a giant magnet. Speakers convert electric currents into magnetic motion to make sound. Even credit cards and hard drives store data by tiny magnetic regions.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Permanent Magnets: Materials (like iron, nickel) where atomic magnets align. 2. Electromagnets: Coils of wire act like magnets when current flows. 3. Earth’s Magnetism: Generated by molten iron moving in Earth’s core, creating a giant bar magnet in space.

## The God Mode Tech
Electric motors and generators rely on magnetism: electricity creates a magnet that can push a rotor, or spinning coils near magnets generates electricity. MRI machines in hospitals use superconducting magnets. Magnetic levitation (maglev) trains float above tracks due to powerful electromagnets reducing friction.

## The Fascinating Truth
Magnetism is fundamentally related to electricity: Albert Einstein showed that a magnetic field is just an electric field seen in a moving frame【77†L187-L194】. In other words, if you ride alongside a moving electric charge, you’d perceive its electric field as a magnetic field. Also, the electrons spinning in their orbits are what make a magnet’s field – essentially we’re surrounded by loops of charge creating invisible fields.
