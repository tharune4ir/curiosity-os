## The Invisible Reality
An object stays at rest or moves in a straight line unless a force acts on it. In other words, objects resist changes in motion (this resistance is called inertia)【115†L590-L594】. When two objects interact, they push on each other equally and oppositely. These are the hidden rules behind every kick, crash, or collision: no formula is needed, just feel the push and pull.

## The Everyday Encounter
Imagine sitting on a moving bus and the driver brakes suddenly. Your body lurches forward because you were moving and want to keep going. Or think of a cricket ball sitting on the ground: it won’t roll until you kick it. These are everyday examples of Newton’s rules in action.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Inertia: Objects keep doing what they’re doing unless acted on by a force. 2. Force Changes Motion: A bigger push on a heavier object makes it move more. 3. Action-Reaction: For every push, there’s an equal push back (when you push a wall, it pushes you back).

## The God Mode Tech
Seat belts, roller coasters, and rockets all rely on Newton’s Laws. Engineers use inertia and forces to design safe cars and to launch satellites. Rockets blast down to lift off (push down equals lift off). Without understanding these rules, none of our vehicles or machinery could move predictably.

## The Fascinating Truth
Newton’s first law means you cannot feel constant motion; you only feel when motion changes. For example, an astronaut floating in space doesn’t feel gravity pulling them—only when they bump into something! Also, if a train derails, you and everything on it keep moving forward until something stops you.
