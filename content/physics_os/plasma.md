## The Invisible Reality
Plasma is an ionized gas where atoms have lost electrons, creating a soup of charged particles. Because of the charges, plasma conducts electricity and responds strongly to electric and magnetic fields. This makes plasma behave very differently from ordinary gas. The Sun and stars are huge balls of plasma. In plasma, collisions between charged particles are electromagnetic, so plasma can support waves (like light) and instabilities.

## The Everyday Encounter
You see plasma in neon or fluorescent lights – the glowing inside is ionized gas. Lightning is a natural plasma discharge through air. Some electronics (plasma TVs) used tiny cells of plasma to light pixels (now mostly replaced by LEDs). Even your stovetop flame has partially ionized plasma regions with tiny sparks.

## The Sub-Landscapes
1. Ionization: Heating or strong fields strip electrons off atoms. 2. Charged Dynamics: Plasmas support magnetic fields and currents, leading to complex structures. 3. Collisional vs Collisionless: In lab plasmas, particles collide; in space plasmas (solar wind), collisions are rare but fields dominate behavior.

## The God Mode Tech
Plasma cutters use jets of plasma (ionized gas) to slice metal. Magnetic confinement fusion (tokamaks) traps hot plasma with strong magnets to attempt sustained fusion. Plasma etching is used in semiconductor fabrication to clean and pattern surfaces.

## The Fascinating Truth
About 99.9% of visible matter in the universe is plasma【102†L299-L304】. Our everyday experience is mostly non-plasma (solid, liquid, gas) but out there, plasma rules – from the Sun’s core to interstellar clouds.
