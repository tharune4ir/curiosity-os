## The Invisible Reality
AR and VR developers build digital worlds or overlays. For VR, it's like creating an entire video game or movie world that you live in with goggles. For AR, they add layers to the real world (like Pokemon Go shows creatures on the camera view). They must align digital images with reality precisely, using math (like coordinates and 3D models) so virtual objects appear at the right place.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Virtual reality is used for training pilots, surgeons, and even in therapy. In augmented reality, hundreds of millions of people have used filters that put virtual sunglasses on their faces in video calls. AR/VR tech is growing fast; for example, AR glasses are being developed that look like normal glasses.

## The Player's Life
You program interactive scenes in engines like Unity, working with 3D models and code. You test on VR headsets or smartphone cameras, adjusting how things appear in view. You often collaborate with artists to create realistic objects and with hardware engineers to optimize performance.

## The Skill Tree
3D programming, knowledge of coordinate systems (3D math), and user interface design in virtual spaces.
