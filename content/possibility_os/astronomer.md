## The Invisible Reality
Astronomers look at space and see light from stars as messages. They treat telescopes like time machines (because looking far means seeing old light). For example, they know light from the Sun takes 8 minutes to reach us, so we see the Sun as it was 8 minutes ago. They think of planets orbiting like cars on a racetrack, and stars as giant balls of gas with their own rules.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Astronomers discovered that planets around other stars are common, and even spotted black holes (massive objects where not even light can escape). Right now, big telescopes and satellites (like the James Webb Space Telescope) are finding the earliest galaxies that formed in the universe.

## The Player's Life
You use telescopes (on Earth or in space) to gather light. You analyze data on a computer, looking for patterns or planets. You might stay up all night watching the sky, and calculate orbits and distances. Occasionally, you explain findings to others in papers, like how far a new planet might be from Earth.

## The Skill Tree
Observation (using telescopes), physics (for gravity and light), and patience.
