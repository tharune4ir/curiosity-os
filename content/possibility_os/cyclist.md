## The Invisible Reality
Cyclists use gears and body power to move. They pedal turns to torque (twisting force) on the wheels, and gears make pedaling easier or harder depending on hill or speed. Wind resistance pushes against them: at high speed, they crouch to become like a small arrow cutting through the air. Bikes are tuned so every pedal stroke transfers as much energy as possible to motion.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Tour de France cyclists can climb mountains at 25 km/h while keeping their hearts pumping at 180 bpm. They produce about 400 watts of power — that's like boiling 8 kettles of water at once! In a long race, their bodies burn fats and sugars nonstop.

## The Player's Life
You train long hours riding both flat roads and hills to build stamina. You do strength and flexibility work on off-days. Each race day, you strategize with your team on pacing and when to sprint or recover. Your bike is finely tuned by mechanics: tire pressure, gear setup, and body position are checked before each stage.

## The Skill Tree
Leg power, endurance, and tactical awareness.
