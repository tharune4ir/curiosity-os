## The Invisible Reality
Dentists treat teeth and mouth issues by using tools like drills and X-rays. They see mouths as tiny engines: they fix cavities (holes in teeth) by removing decay and filling them. They use mirrors and drills with precision to polish or repair teeth, and check gum health. Each tooth is like a living part, and they keep a whole row working together.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Dentists use microscopes and lasers in modern clinics. They can scan your teeth and show them on a screen, then fill cavities with materials that look like real teeth. Many dentists also train people on brushing technique to prevent problems before they start.

## The Player's Life
You examine patients' teeth, take X-rays, and diagnose issues like cavities or gum disease. You use drills and polishers to clean or fix teeth. Each day, you see many patients, often guiding them on better care. You also record findings and sometimes manage a small clinic.

## The Skill Tree
Manual dexterity (steady hands), biology of teeth, and communication skills (talking to patients calmly).
