## The Invisible Reality
Ecologists study living things and their environment as a system. They might think of a forest as a web of life: every plant, animal, and insect is connected. For example, if one species disappears, others may suffer. They use counting and observation to estimate animal populations or plant health, like counting trees in a forest and mapping which animals live there.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Ecologists help us understand ecosystems. Right now, they study things like how bees pollinate crops or how deforestation affects climate. Some ecologists work on preserving habitats, making sure national parks protect biodiversity.

## The Player's Life
You go into the field, taking notes on plants and animals. You might trap and tag animals to track them, and take soil or water samples. You also analyze data on a computer, looking for trends in population sizes. You share your findings in reports to guide conservation efforts.

## The Skill Tree
Fieldwork skills (patience and observation), data analysis, and understanding of biology and ecosystems.
