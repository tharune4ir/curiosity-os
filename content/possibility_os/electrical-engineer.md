## The Invisible Reality
Electrical engineers treat electricity like water flowing through pipes. They imagine circuits as paths that electricity travels, like how water flows down a river or current. By thinking of electrons (tiny charged bits) moving like people on a crowded train, they design wires and circuits so nothing overloads or breaks. This lets them make things like lights, phones, and robots work safely.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Every time you switch on a light or charge a phone, an electrical engineer made that possible. Today, whole cities keep lights on with smart power grids designed by electrical engineers, and even your home has circuits tested by them.

## The Player's Life
You often draw circuit diagrams and test them using computers. You use voltmeters (that measure electricity) and build small models of circuits. You also fix problems like a short circuit by tweaking wires and resistors so devices don't overheat. Many days, you might balance how much electricity a system uses to save energy.

## The Skill Tree
Understanding electricity and magnetism, working precisely with circuits, and solving technical puzzles.
