## The Invisible Reality
Environmental scientists see Earth as one big system, like a living breathing body. They study how air, water, plants, and animals interact. For instance, they might see a river as a long moving plant that carries nutrients. By measuring chemicals in water or the number of trees, they understand how clean or polluted a place is, and how changes (like cutting trees) affect the whole environment.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Right now, environmental scientists track pollution and climate change. They collect data from around the world—like how much CO2 is in the air—and use it to warn us about rising sea levels or endangered species.

## The Player's Life
You collect samples (like soil or water), use sensors and computers to test them for toxins or temperature, and make reports. You might measure air quality on a mountain or study how a forest grows. Every day you compare data to spot problems, like too many chemicals or no animals around.

## The Skill Tree
Observation skills (noticing small changes in nature), using lab tools and field equipment, and basic chemistry and biology.
