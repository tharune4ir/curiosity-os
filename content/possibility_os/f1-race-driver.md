## The Invisible Reality
A Formula 1 driver controls a machine pushing human limits. They feel G-forces (like heavy weight pressing on you) in turns, like trying to turn while lying flat. To react in split seconds, drivers train their muscles and mind. They see everything through a visor and know that even a hand movement must be precise to control the car. The race track is like a video game setting, but real and fast.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
When racing, drivers can experience forces 6 to 7 times their body weight in corners. That means if you weigh 50 kg, it feels like 300 kg pushing on you. These elite athletes are also test pilots of technology: they sweat in cockpits with sensors all over their suits, monitoring heart rate and breathing every second of the race.

## The Player's Life
You spend time in a race car simulator (a huge video game machine) perfecting track lines. Your day includes intense workouts for speed and reflexes, studying track maps, and working with engineers to set up the car. On race day, you drive at over 300 km/h, making split-second decisions about steering, braking, and changing gears.

## The Skill Tree
Lightning-fast reaction time, incredible focus under pressure, and physical fitness (to handle the G-forces).
