## The Invisible Reality
Gymnastics is about controlling your body in the air. A gymnast uses momentum like a pendulum: swinging legs, pushing off springs in the floor, and rotating in mid-air. They learn to twist and turn so precisely that they land on their feet. Their mind and body create careful arcs and spins, similar to a spinning toy or a boomerang, to do flips and twists.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Olympic gymnasts train to stick landings from double flips, all while flipping and twisting at speeds of dozens of rotations per second. They might jump 3 meters in the air and rotate around two or three times before landing perfectly on their feet.

## The Player's Life
Your day involves practice on different apparatus: floor, rings, bars. You repeat routines until your body moves exactly as planned. You train strength (like holding yourself on rings) and flexibility. Coaches use video to analyze your posture and angles for perfect landings.

## The Skill Tree
Strength (especially core strength), body awareness (knowing position in the air), and flexibility.
