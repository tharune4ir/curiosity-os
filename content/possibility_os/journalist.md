## The Invisible Reality
Journalists tell true stories from around the world. They gather facts by interviewing people and checking records, then write or report them. They think of news like a puzzle: finding facts and checking them from different angles. For example, they compare multiple sources to ensure accuracy.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
News can spread instantly now: reporters file stories that reach millions via the internet in seconds. Investigative journalists have uncovered scandals and truths that changed societies. They often work with video or print to present information clearly.

## The Player's Life
You interview eyewitnesses or officials, take notes, and verify facts. You write articles or scripts quickly under deadlines, and often travel to cover events. You also edit videos or publish posts on websites. Many journalists juggle busy schedules to report daily news.

## The Skill Tree
Writing and speaking skills, critical thinking, and research.
