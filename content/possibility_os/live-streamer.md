## The Invisible Reality
Live streamers broadcast what they're doing in real-time, often playing video games or doing activities while talking. It's like hosting a live TV show from home: they set up cameras, microphones, and a computer that sends audio and video to viewers online. They react instantly to chat messages, so it’s like having a conversation with thousands of people at once.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Popular streamers can have tens of thousands of people watching them live at the same time. Some streamers raise millions of rupees for charity in one day by encouraging viewers to donate. Streaming uses fast internet and computers so that video is delivered without delay.

## The Player's Life
You set up streaming software to capture your screen and camera. You play games or do activities for hours, interact with viewers, and manage any technical issues. Between streams, you plan content, check stream quality, and update equipment. Consistency is key: many streamers follow strict schedules.

## The Skill Tree
Communication skills, technical setup (using streaming software), and consistency (long broadcasts).
