## The Invisible Reality
Marine biologists study life in oceans and seas. They dive or use submarines to observe animals underwater, treating the ocean like a vast garden. They know how salt water affects creatures. For example, they learn how fish breathe dissolved oxygen and how coral reefs grow. They often use tools like nets or microscopes to catch and study tiny sea creatures.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Marine biologists have discovered new species in the deep ocean and track marine health. They use tagging to follow whales, and some work on protecting coral reefs from climate change. Our ocean research today even helps find medicines from sea organisms.

## The Player's Life
You conduct boat trips to collect water or sample fish, sometimes scuba diving for data. In labs, you analyze plankton or test how pollution affects marine life. You also write reports for conservation and might educate the public about protecting oceans.

## The Skill Tree
Swimming or diving skills, knowledge of biology (especially marine ecosystems), and curiosity.
