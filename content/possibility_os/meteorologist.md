## The Invisible Reality
Meteorologists study weather patterns in the short term. They use radars and satellites to see clouds and storms. Think of weather like moving waves in the sky, and they predict when rain or storms will come. They use equations to calculate how air pressure and temperature change, much like predicting how a ball will bounce.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Weather forecasts use supercomputers to simulate the atmosphere. Meteorologists predicted major storms like Cyclones weeks in advance. They help farmers, pilots, and everyone prepare for weather, saving lives by alerting us to hurricanes or heavy snowfall.

## The Player's Life
You analyze data from weather stations, satellites, and weather balloons. You run computer programs to forecast weather for the next hours or days. You also report this information, maybe on TV or apps, explaining if it will rain or shine.

## The Skill Tree
Physics and math (for weather equations), data analysis, and communication skills.
