## The Invisible Reality
Riding a MotoGP bike at high speed feels like balancing on a moving broom. Racers lean hard into corners, putting knees close to the track, because the tire grip and speed create a force pushing them out. They think about wind like water pushing against them and wear suits with tiny bumpers for protection. The engine's vibration is music to them—it tells exactly how the bike behaves on every turn.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
These racers reach speeds up to 350 km/h on tracks. That’s faster than most airplanes on the highway below. Despite being on a two-wheeled bike, they can brake from 300 km/h to 0 in just 7 seconds—the same effort as jumping off a one-story building and stopping safely.

## The Player's Life
You train your body and reflexes daily, often doing balance drills and gym work. You practice riding in simulators and on tracks, and after each ride mechanics tune your bike’s engine and suspension. You memorize every bend in the track and often replay your rides on video to shave off milliseconds in every corner.

## The Skill Tree
Extreme balance, physical fitness, and fearless focus.
