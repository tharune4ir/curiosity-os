## The Invisible Reality
Nurses are caregivers who know medical routines. They use skills like measuring temperature or giving injections, often acting like health helpers. They see patients regularly and notice changes (like a doctor’s assistant). In hospitals, they coordinate many tasks and help doctors by charting patients’ conditions.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Nurses form the largest group of health workers worldwide. Today’s nurses might operate life-support machines or use tablets to track patient data. They are often the first to recognize emergencies because they spend the most time with patients.

## The Player's Life
You provide care by checking vital signs (like blood pressure and pulse), giving medications, and helping patients with daily activities (like bathing). You also update patient charts and communicate with doctors. Many nurses work long shifts and must be ready for emergencies at any time.

## The Skill Tree
Compassion (caring for patients), attention to detail (monitoring changes), and teamwork (working with doctors and other nurses).
