## The Invisible Reality
Painters use brushes and colors to make images. They see a canvas as a window to imagination. They mix paints (like chemists mixing solutions) to get just the right shade, and apply layers to create depth. They might start with a sketch and then build up colors and textures, capturing light and shadows as in real life or fantasy.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Paintings can capture history and imagination for centuries. Famous artworks like the Mona Lisa or Starry Night were painted by hand. Modern visual artists also use digital tools to create paintings on tablets. Art can influence culture and even appear in virtual reality.

## The Player's Life
You gather or mix colors, prepare canvases, and begin painting. You work on composition (arrangement of elements) and refine details with small brushes. You may paint for hours, sometimes making dozens of attempts. You also showcase or sell art, and sometimes teach or illustrate books.

## The Skill Tree
Artistic vision, color mixing knowledge, and manual dexterity.
