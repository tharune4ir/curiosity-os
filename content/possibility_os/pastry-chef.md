## The Invisible Reality
Pastry chefs handle baking like precise chemistry. They measure flour, sugar, and eggs with exact balance. For instance, baking bread is like a science experiment: yeast (tiny living cells) eat sugars and release gas, making bread rise. Pastry chefs use math for proportions and timing to achieve flaky layers or fluffy cakes.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Perfect pastries and bread have the right texture because of exact temperature and timing. Some bakers have baked loaves that use wild yeast cultures from the air (sourdough). Pastry chefs may bake tens of perfect cookies or croissants every day, each batch needing consistency.

## The Player's Life
You measure ingredients carefully, mix dough or batter, and preheat ovens. You check things like oven temperature and humidity. You shape dough by hand or machine, then decorate pastries with icing or chocolate. Each day, you test one batch after another, making small adjustments to get the perfect taste and texture.

## The Skill Tree
Measuring and timing (for recipes), knowledge of ingredients (flour types, chocolate), and artistic decoration (for pastry appearance).
