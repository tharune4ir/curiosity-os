## The Invisible Reality
Physical therapists use exercises and manual techniques to heal the body. They see the body as interconnected parts: for example, a knee problem can affect how someone walks. They understand how muscles and bones work, using tools like resistance bands (like stretchy rubber) or massages to increase movement. Rehabilitation is like re-programming the body: with practice, muscles learn to move correctly again.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Physiotherapy helps people recover from injuries faster. Modern PTs use technology too: like underwater treadmills or video feedback so patients see their own movements. They work with stroke patients helping them walk again, and with athletes improving performance.

## The Player's Life
You evaluate a patient's movement and design exercise plans. You guide them through stretching and strengthening exercises. You track progress with measurements and modify exercises as needed. Each day, you might help patients walk, bend, or balance, giving hands-on adjustments.

## The Skill Tree
Knowledge of body mechanics, patience and observation, and hands-on skills.
