## The Invisible Reality
Product managers decide what a product should be. They act like a conductor of a team: combining technology, design, and customer feedback. They create product plans (roadmaps) and features list. It's like building a custom order: they make sure a new phone app has all features that users need and that it arrives on time.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Many tech products are planned by product managers. For example, deciding which new feature to add to a social media app or an online store involves a product manager analyzing millions of user actions. They ensure the final product is something people want.

## The Player's Life
You gather requirements (what customers want), write specifications, and work with engineers and designers to build it. You prioritize features (deciding what to build first) and write reports on progress. You also test prototypes and give feedback before the product launches.

## The Skill Tree
Communication (with teams), planning and organization, and customer empathy (understanding user needs).
