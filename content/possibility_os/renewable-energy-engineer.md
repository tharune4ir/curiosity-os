## The Invisible Reality
Renewable energy engineers design systems that use natural power like sun and wind. They treat the sun as a power plant: solar panels convert sunlight to electricity. Wind turbines use blades to capture wind energy like sails on a boat. They calculate exactly how to place equipment and wire it up so nature's power runs our machines.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Wind and solar farms now generate gigawatts of power worldwide. Engineers today build large solar fields and tall wind turbines. For example, offshore wind turbines can be over 200 meters tall, and their electricity helps power entire cities without burning fossil fuels.

## The Player's Life
You plan where to put solar panels or windmills, often using maps of sun or wind patterns. You design and test parts, like ensuring a blade won't break. You also work on systems to store energy (like big batteries) for when the sun isn't shining. Each day involves calculations, computer models, and on-site inspections.

## The Skill Tree
Knowledge of electrical systems, understanding of environmental conditions, and problem-solving.
