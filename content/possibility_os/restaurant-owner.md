## The Invisible Reality
A restaurant owner combines cooking with business. They see the restaurant like a stage show: chefs cook (backstage) while customers dine (audience). They manage staff and inventory, making sure there's enough ingredients (like stocking a pantry) and the kitchen runs smoothly. They also know which dishes sell well and which don't.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Running a popular restaurant means serving hundreds of meals a night. Owners need to maintain quality and profit. For example, they might use software to track which ingredients are used most or which shifts are busiest to schedule staff perfectly.

## The Player's Life
You handle both cooking tasks and paperwork. You plan menus, order ingredients, and set prices. You talk with chefs and waiters to ensure service is fast. After hours, you analyze sales and inventory reports, and think about improving the restaurant or making it unique.

## The Skill Tree
Business sense (managing money and staff), communication (leading a team), and customer service (ensuring guests are happy).
