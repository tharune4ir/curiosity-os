## The Invisible Reality
Robotics engineers combine machines, sensors, and software to build robots. Imagine building an animal-like machine: mechanical parts (like joints and wheels), brain (computer), and sensors (tiny digital feelers) to sense touch or light. They make sure the robot’s 'body' moves correctly by controlling motors, and its 'brain' (the program) tells the robot how to react, like moving a robot hand to pick up objects.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Self-driving car prototypes and factory robots are built by robotics engineers. Today, robots can even explore Mars or help perform surgeries because of their work.

## The Player's Life
You often glue together circuit boards, motors, and computer parts. You write code that tells the robot how to move and test it, like programming a robot arm to grab a cup. You also fix sensors and wiring, making sure the robot can see and feel properly with cameras or pressure sensors.

## The Skill Tree
Combining mechanics (machines) with electronics, programming knowledge, and problem solving for machines.
