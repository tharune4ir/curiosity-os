## The Invisible Reality
Small business owners run shops or services, managing everyday operations. They see their business like a family team: they might handle sales at the store, then at night update accounts. For example, a neighborhood grocery shop owner stocks shelves (like curating choices), sets prices, and takes care of any customer problems quickly. Every rupee they earn or spend affects their profit directly.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Small businesses make up a huge part of the economy, from street vendors to local factories. Right now, millions of people in India run small shops, tailoring, or farms as family businesses. Many start with little capital and grow slowly, relying on community trust.

## The Player's Life
You might open early to serve customers, manage inventory, and advertise in the community. You handle billing and bookkeeping at closing time. You also adapt daily: for example, if an item sells out fast, you plan to order more. Customer service and building relationships in the neighborhood are key parts.

## The Skill Tree
Basic accounting (managing money), customer service (talking with clients), and adaptability.
