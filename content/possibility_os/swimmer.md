## The Invisible Reality
Swimming fast involves moving through water like a dolphin. The swimmer's arms and legs push against water, and streamlined shape (head down, tight suit) reduces drag (resistance). They time every stroke and kick to breathe without slowing. Water provides resistance like a thick liquid, so swimmers train to cut through it efficiently.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Competitive swimmers swim 100 meters in under 50 seconds. They train muscles differently because water is 800 times denser than air. Olympic swimmers often hold their breath and stroke at rates that would exhaust a normal person in just one minute.

## The Player's Life
You start with laps in the pool, practicing your stroke and breathing technique. You use training equipment like paddles or snorkels to build strength. Each day includes dryland training (push-ups, squats) and careful timing of swims. Coaches time every lap and adjust your form, like tweaking your arm angle for speed.

## The Skill Tree
Breathing control, technique (perfecting stroke), and cardiovascular endurance.
