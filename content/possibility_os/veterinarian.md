## The Invisible Reality
Veterinarians diagnose and treat animals. They use knowledge of animal biology which can be very different from humans: for instance, how to check a dog's heartbeat or give medicine to a cat. They treat animals like patients, using tools similarly (with smaller instruments). For example, they might give a rabbit anesthesia so it doesn't feel pain during a check-up.

## The Mind-Blowing Hook
Vets work with a huge variety of creatures, from household pets to farm animals and even zoo animals. They may use the same technologies (like X-ray or ultrasound) as human doctors, and even perform surgeries like spaying or fixing broken bones. Some vets also help track diseases that could spread from animals to humans.

## The Player's Life
You see many animals a day, examining them as owners hold them. You give shots or perform minor surgeries, and advise owners on pet care. You also write reports about sick animals and might care for injured wildlife. Often you need patience, since animals can't explain where it hurts.

## The Skill Tree
Animal handling skills, medical knowledge (for different species), and observation (noticing subtle signs of illness).
